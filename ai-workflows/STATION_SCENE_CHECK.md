# Station Scene Check

Purpose: make scenes on Der Himmelskrug spatially, procedurally, and sensorially coherent.

Use this only when the scene happens on Der Himmelskrug or depends directly on station routing, station authority, station spaces, or station systems.

If the scene is off-station, do not force this checklist. Use location, jurisdiction, route, transport, communications, and authority checks instead.

## Required references

Use these before drafting or revising important station scenes:


- economy, law, communications;
- cannabis and the Green Room / Grünraum.

## Step 1: Locate the scene

Identify:

- level;
- district or room;
- public, semi-public, restricted, staff-only, or hidden status;
- operational owner;
- nearby routes;
- who is allowed there;
- what a visitor misunderstands about the space.

Do not use vague station geography when blocking matters.

## Step 2: Route access

Ask:

- How did each person get there?
- Did they pass Arrival Control?
- Are they clean-side or restricted-side?
- Is a route public, staff, maintenance, dirty-side, emergency, or hidden?
- Can a bubble elevator reach this level?
- Is an escort required?
- Is the route physically accessible?

## Step 3: Authority and procedure

Ask:

- Who is in charge of the space?
- Does Flight Control have authority here?
- Does Arrival Control have authority here?
- Does Station Security have authority here?
- Is Medical involved?
- Is a guild, board, court, cooperative, or external jurisdiction involved?
- What gets logged?
- What can be appealed?

## Step 4: Systems and consequences

Check whether the scene involves:

- transport;
- ship docking;
- public feeds;
- delayed media;
- clean/restricted routing;
- force or weapons;
- nonlethal disabling;
- fines, liens, or debt;
- labor protections;
- privacy;
- emergency procedure;
- cannabis or destination-law screening.

Nothing should work by magic when a known system applies.

## Step 5: Sensory access

Station scenes should be legible through more than sight.

Check:

- floor or surface feel;
- air movement;
- smell;
- sound profile;
- lighting without relying only on color;
- crowd density;
- route clarity;
- tactile cues;
- what machinery or people can be heard nearby.

## Step 6: Protected station locks

Do not accidentally break these:

- Bubble elevators are limited to public atrium levels.
- The Crown remains a civic cap, not a generic rooftop.
- Arrival Control is a real public entry institution.
- Flight Control is operational authority, not decoration.
- Starship Garage and Restricted Works remain separate.
- The Spare Tap is staff-only.
- The Green Room / Grünraum has rules; it is not a free-for-all.
- Clean and restricted routing matter.
- Public force has review and consequences.
- Media/public narrative is physical and procedural, not omniscient magic.

## Step 7: Candidate canon

If the scene creates a reusable station detail, mark it.

Use:

```text
Candidate canon: <detail>
Suggested owner file: canon/08-der-himmelskrug-station.md or relevant quick-reference file
Reason: <why it may matter later>
Risk: <possible conflict or uncertainty>
```

## Station principle

Der Himmelskrug is absurd, but it is not vague. Treat it like a working civic machine people can get lost in, argue with, maintain, love, fear, and legally appeal.
