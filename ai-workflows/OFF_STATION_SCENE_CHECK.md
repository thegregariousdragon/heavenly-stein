# Off-Station Scene Check

Purpose: support Heavenly Stein stories that take place away from Der Himmelskrug without forcing station geography into scenes where it does not belong.

Use this when a scene is set on a planet, colony, route, ship, port, corporate facility, media site, private residence, legal venue, or any other non-station location.

## First rule

Off-station does not mean outside canon.

The scene may still depend on:

- transport;
- destination law;
- communications limits;
- Flight Control reach;
- corporate authority;
- media visibility;
- labor contracts;
- guild rules;
- money, fines, liens, or debt;
- ship systems;
- relationship and character continuity;
- past or candidate story consequences.

## Step 1: Identify location

State:

- place name;
- type of place;
- owner or operator;
- public/private status;
- local authority;
- relation to Der Himmelskrug;
- whether the location already exists in canon or is new candidate canon.

Examples of place types:

- ship;
- port;
- planet;
- moon;
- colony;
- corporate site;
- cooperative site;
- court or hearing room;
- media office;
- home;
- route checkpoint;
- medical facility;
- repair yard;
- diplomatic venue.

## Step 2: Check jurisdiction

Ask:

- Whose law applies here?
- Is station law relevant?
- Is destination law relevant?
- Is local government relevant?
- Is a corporation or cooperative enforcing rules?
- Is Flight Control involved directly, indirectly, or not at all?
- Is there due process?
- Who can appeal or contest an action?

## Step 3: Check travel and routing

Ask:

- How did the characters get there?
- By ship, route, transfer, lift, shuttle, public transport, or something else?
- Is travel logged?
- Was Arrival Control involved at any point?
- Does clean/restricted routing matter before or after the off-station scene?
- Could a delay, customs rule, docking rule, or safety hold affect the scene?

## Step 4: Check communications

Ask:

- Can characters contact the station?
- Are messages delayed, filtered, monitored, public, private, or recorded?
- Is media present?
- Is a public feed involved?
- Does the scene create a public narrative that comes back to the station?
- Are staff privacy, Medical details, grief, labor conflict, or investigations protected?

## Step 5: Check character continuity

Use:

```text
ai-workflows/CHARACTER_VOICE_CHECK.md
```

Off-station scenes can change what a character wants, fears, knows, or can safely admit.

If that change matters later, mark it as candidate canon.

## Step 6: Check systems

Use relevant canon when the scene involves:

- ships;
- starship systems;
- transport and movement;
- law, economy, and communications;
- governance and labor;
- corporations, cooperatives, and institutions;
- weapons, combat, or force rules;
- tested story precedents.

## Step 7: Sensory and access

Off-station locations still need accessible scene grounding.

Check:

- surface;
- air;
- smell;
- sound;
- crowding;
- lighting without relying only on color;
- route clarity;
- local signage or customs;
- who can move freely and who cannot.

## Step 8: Candidate canon

If the scene creates a reusable location, institution, legal rule, route, ship fact, or relationship change, mark it.

Use:

```text
Candidate canon: <detail>
Suggested owner file: <file>
Reason: <why it may matter later>
Risk: <possible conflict or uncertainty>
```

## Report format

```text
Off-station scene status:
- Pass / needs jurisdiction check / needs transport check / needs canon check

Location:
- <place and type>

Authority:
- <local owner, institution, or law>

Connection to station:
- <none / route / media / Flight Control / commerce / labor / character tie>

Sensory notes:
- <sound, smell, surface, air, lighting>

Continuity risks:
- <risks or none>

Candidate canon:
- <items or none>
```

## Off-station principle

The station is the project anchor, not a cage. Off-station stories should expand the world while still respecting routes, law, labor, communications, character continuity, and consequence.
