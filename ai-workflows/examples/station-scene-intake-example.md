# Station Scene Intake Example

Canon status: non-canon workflow example.

Do not treat this as an active story event, active chronology, active scene blocking, or promoted canon.

## Working title

Atrium Queue Drift

## Status

Scene experiment.

## Desired output

- Rough scene.
- Station scene check.
- Character voice pass.
- Continuity audit.

## Premise

A public arrival queue starts drifting into the wrong marked lane during a busy station day. A station leader has to keep the moment humane while the process remains strict enough to protect routing and access.

## Location

Der Himmelskrug station.

Location notes:

- Public arrival-facing area.
- Route markings matter.
- Public crowd, staff instructions, and sensory access all matter.

## Station relevance

Station scene.

Relevant institutions:

- Arrival Control.
- Station Security.
- Media/public feed.
- Clean/restricted routing.

## Characters

- Character A: public-facing station authority; wants to keep visitors calm without weakening procedure.
- Character B: visitor or contractor; wants a clear answer and accidentally becomes the center of attention.
- Character C: staff observer; notices the practical route problem before the argument becomes emotional.

## Tone target

- Warm.
- Procedural.
- Publicly embarrassing.
- Absurd but consequence-aware.

Tone notes:

The humor should come from civic process under pressure, not from making staff or visitors incompetent.

## Required context packs

- Common foundation.
- General story drafting.
- Der Himmelskrug station scene.
- Character-heavy scene, if named recurring characters are used.
- Law/labor/governance/economy/media/public accountability, if public feed consequences matter.

## Continuity risks

- Do not make Arrival Control decorative.
- Do not ignore clean/restricted routing.
- Do not let public embarrassment erase privacy or accessibility.
- Do not invent a new station level or route unless marked as candidate canon.

## Candidate canon created by this idea

None by default.

If a new lane marking, public announcement protocol, or crowd-control tool is invented, mark it as candidate canon.

## Do not do

- Do not turn this into a random crowd gag with no procedure.
- Do not place restricted access in a public route without explaining how it is controlled.
- Do not rely only on color-coded signage.

## Open questions

- Which recurring character, if any, should lead the scene?
- Is media physically present, or is this just visible to nearby witnesses?
- Is the route error harmless, costly, or dangerous?
