# Off-Station Scene Intake Example

Canon status: non-canon workflow example.

Do not treat this as an active story event, active chronology, active location, or promoted canon.

## Working title

The Quiet Port Office

## Status

Scene experiment.

## Desired output

- Outline.
- Off-station scene check.
- Character voice pass.
- Continuity audit.

## Premise

A character connected to Der Himmelskrug has to resolve a paperwork problem at a small off-station port office. The situation seems minor, but the decision could affect route access, public accountability, or a later station-facing consequence.

## Location

Port or route checkpoint away from Der Himmelskrug.

Location notes:

- Not on the station.
- Local authority matters.
- Travel records and communications may matter.
- Any specific port name is candidate canon unless already established.

## Station relevance

Off-station but station institutions may matter.

Possible relevant ties:

- Flight Control reach.
- Route permissions.
- Commerce, fines, liens, or debt.
- Media/public narrative.
- Labor or contractor obligations.

## Characters

- Character A: station-connected person; wants the issue resolved without creating a public mess.
- Character B: local official or clerk; wants local rules respected.
- Character C: remote contact; may be delayed, filtered, or unavailable.

## Tone target

- Quiet and procedural.
- Warm but tense.
- Bureaucratic absurdity with real consequences.

## Required context packs

- Common foundation.
- General story drafting.
- Off-station scene.
- Character-heavy scene, if named recurring characters are used.
- Law/labor/governance/economy/media/public accountability, if the paperwork affects rights, money, public record, or institutional trust.

## Continuity risks

- Do not apply station geography to a non-station place.
- Do not assume station rules override local authority.
- Do not invent a new port, law, or route fact without marking it as candidate canon.
- Do not ignore communications delay, route logging, or jurisdiction.

## Candidate canon created by this idea

Potential candidate canon:

```text
Candidate canon: A small off-station port office exists on a route used by station-connected travelers.
Suggested owner file: canon/06-transport-and-movement.md or canon/21-corporations-cooperatives-and-institutions.md
Reason: Could become reusable route infrastructure.
Risk: May conflict with existing route or jurisdiction design if not checked.
```

## Do not do

- Do not make the station omnipotent.
- Do not make local authority meaningless.
- Do not turn paperwork into consequence-free comedy.

## Open questions

- Is this location already established?
- Is the scene meant to create a reusable route location?
- Does the decision come back to Der Himmelskrug publicly or privately?
