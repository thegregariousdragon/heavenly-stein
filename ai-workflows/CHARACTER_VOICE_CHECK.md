# Character Voice Check

Purpose: keep character behavior, voice, public role, private boundaries, and competence intact during drafting and revision.

Use this before writing or revising scenes with named recurring characters.

## First rule

Do not draft character voice from memory alone.

Use:

```text
quick-reference/CHARACTER_QUICK_CARDS.md
```

and relevant numbered canon files before writing or revising important character scenes.

## Core checks

For each named recurring character, confirm:

- name;
- pronouns;
- current role;
- public status;
- private boundaries;
- relationship status;
- institutional power;
- what they know;
- what they do not know;
- what they want in the scene;
- what they are responsible for;
- what they would refuse to do.

## Competence check

The Heavenly Stein uses comedy with consequences.

Do not make competent characters incompetent just to create a joke.

A character can be funny because:

- they are under pressure;
- they are technically correct at the worst possible moment;
- they are following a process no one else understands;
- they are emotionally honest in an inconvenient place;
- they are socially awkward but operationally right;
- the infrastructure is absurd but real;
- civic rules collide with beer, romance, ships, labor, media, or safety.

## Public/private check

Ask:

- Is this character speaking publicly, privately, or semi-publicly?
- Who can hear them?
- Is media present?
- Is a feed delayed, filtered, or recorded?
- Are Medical, grief, labor, security, or investigation details being exposed?
- Would this character protect someone else's privacy?
- Would this institution allow the moment to be public?

## Relationship check

For relationship-sensitive scenes, confirm:

- current status;
- public knowledge versus private knowledge;
- who is teasing whom;
- whether teasing is affectionate, intrusive, tactical, or harmful;
- whether the scene changes the relationship;
- whether that change is candidate canon.

## Authority check

For characters with institutional roles, check:

- what authority they actually have;
- what authority they do not have;
- what procedure they must follow;
- who can overrule them;
- what due process or labor rule applies;
- whether public pressure changes anything;
- whether consequences are delayed, logged, appealed, or enforced.

## Voice texture

Voice is not just dialect or catchphrases.

Check:

- sentence rhythm;
- what the character notices first;
- how they handle danger;
- how they handle embarrassment;
- how they handle public attention;
- whether they use humor, precision, warmth, silence, bureaucracy, bluntness, or deflection;
- what they avoid saying.

## Scene report

When reporting a voice check, use:

```text
Character checked:
- <name>

Status:
- Pass / needs revision / canon check needed

Voice notes:
- <notes>

Continuity risks:
- <risks or none>

Candidate canon:
- <items or none>
```

## Character principle

Characters should feel like people living inside a working civic system, not props dragged through a joke.
