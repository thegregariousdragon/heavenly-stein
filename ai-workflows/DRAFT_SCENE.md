# AI Scene Drafting Workflow

Use this workflow when drafting new story prose, outline beats, or scene revisions.

## First rule

Do not draft from memory alone.

Select a context pack from `ai-workflows/CONTEXT_PACKS.md`, then draft from the active canon and current user request.

## Step 1: Classify the request

Identify the task type:

- New prose scene.
- Continuation of an existing scene.
- Outline or beat sheet.
- Dialogue pass.
- Sensory/accessibility pass.
- Continuity repair.
- Character voice pass.
- Station blocking pass.
- Action/safety pass.
- Planning or arc development.

## Step 2: Load context

Always load the common foundation from `CONTEXT_PACKS.md`.

Then add any relevant task pack:

- Der Himmelskrug station scene.
- Character-heavy scene.
- Action, force, weapons, ships, or emergencies.
- Law, labor, governance, economy, media, or public accountability.
- Cannabis / The Green Room / Grünraum.
- Planning or story queue.
- Canon update or canon promotion.

## Step 3: Resolve the active premise

Before drafting, establish:

- What is the scene trying to accomplish?
- Where does it happen?
- Who is present?
- What authority, route, or role owns the space?
- What active canon is mandatory?
- What old chronology must stay inactive?
- What uncertainty should be named instead of invented?

## Step 4: Draft with constraints active

Core constraints:

- Ridiculous premise, serious engineering underneath.
- Competent people should not become stupid for comedy.
- Warmth and humor can coexist with safety, oversight, labor, law, and consequence.
- Heinz should not automatically become the center of every problem.
- Flight Control, Arrival Control, Medical, Security, Engineering, Dirty-Side Operations, Compliance, Hospitality, Residential Life, Governance, and Labor have real authority in their lanes.
- Do not treat nonlethal force, disabling fire, transport, cannabis, clean/dirty routing, or public communications as consequence-free.

## Step 5: Station-scene drafting checks

For any Der Himmelskrug scene, know:

- Level.
- Public/restricted status.
- Route class.
- Operational owner.
- Arrival/departure route if someone enters or leaves.
- What the public can and cannot access.
- Whether bubble elevators, service cores, Medical lifts, resident lifts, command cores, garage routes, or restricted-side routes are appropriate.
- Surface, lighting, air/smell, sound, and wayfinding details.

Use two or three specific sensory details per scene beat, not a full tour unless the scene is about arriving, being lost, touring, inspection, or system stress.

## Step 6: Character drafting checks

For every named character:

- Confirm name and pronouns from active canon.
- Confirm current role and authority.
- Confirm relationship status.
- Confirm stress behavior and voice.
- Give the correct person the correct problem when possible.
- Do not reduce a professional role to a running joke unless the story is correcting that disrespect.

## Step 7: Output options

For short drafting requests, provide:

1. Draft.
2. Brief continuity note if useful.

For larger story-development requests, provide:

1. Draft or outline.
2. Canon files used.
3. Continuity checks passed.
4. Open questions or uncertainties.
5. Suggested next step.

## Step 8: Do not silently promote canon

New draft facts are provisional unless the user explicitly asks to canonize them or a canon update branch promotes them.

If the draft creates a potentially reusable fact, mark it as:

- Provisional scene detail.
- Candidate canon.
- Continuity risk.
- Requires canon promotion.

## Common failure modes to avoid

- Direct teleportation into public hospitality, Residential, school, brewery production, Command, Services, Starship Garage, or Restricted Works without routing.
- Press physically entering Crown Command because they want a better angle.
- Bubble elevators serving Residential, Services, Medical, school, Command, Starship Garage, or Restricted Works.
- The Green Room producing casual corridor odor.
- The Spare Tap becoming tourist content.
- Legal cannabis overriding destination law or safety-duty sobriety.
- Nonlethal force being harmless.
- Disabling fire being neat and consequence-free.
- Heinz overriding Flight Control, Security, Medical, or Compliance just because he is the protagonist.
- Old story events becoming active through implication.

## Closing note

The goal is not to make every scene bureaucratic. The goal is to make the warmth, music, comedy, beer, family, romance, and absurdity feel real because the infrastructure underneath them works.
