# Media and Public Perception

## Source-of-truth note

This file remains the authority for media editorial posture, political framing, public-perception rules, and how outlets respond to Der Himmelskrug, governance, labor, route safety, and Heinz/Feli.

For media organizations as institutions, including headquarters, business pressures, and corporate/institutional story use, see `canon/21-corporations-cooperatives-and-institutions.md`.

## Media institutional locations

These base locations are canon unless a later story deliberately revises them. Editorial behavior remains governed by the outlet descriptions below.

- **INN / Interstellar News Network**: New York, Earth; major interstellar corporate news network with legacy-institutional weight.
- **Galaxy News**: Los Angeles, Earth; major interstellar corporate news network connected to Earth media, entertainment, labor, culture, and public-image industries.
- **Civic Meridian**: Lagrange Nine Commercial Ring; centrist process-focused outlet close to legal, insurance, arbitration, and corporate-hearing institutions.
- **Redline Commons**: distributed network with a major Kestrel Minor node; decentralized activist-media collective rooted in worker, small-hauler, debt, and anti-private-authority networks.
- **RouteCast / BeaconWatch**: Relay Haven plus distributed beacon bureaus; route-conditions and urgent-alert institution trusted by working crews.
- **Starlight Tonight**: Los Angeles, Earth; entertainment and celebrity interstellar feed tied to festival, music, fashion, and public-image machinery.
- **Across the Table**: Glasswater; food, brewing, hospitality, sourcing, allergen-safety, and supply-chain feed.
- **Open Route**: Port Irie, Zion Drift, with a Lagrange Nine sales office; travel and destination feed with advertiser and tourism-board ties.
- **Frontier Truthline**: Dallas, Texas, Earth, with distributed route contributors, affiliated personalities, clip channels, local stringers, grievance forums, podcasts, and touring live events.
- **Technical feeds**: distributed specialist feeds; bases vary by discipline, route, institution, or engineering community.

## Editorial posture and public-perception behavior
- **INN / Interstellar News Network**: center-right institutional media and conservative cable-news
  equivalent. Values order, defense readiness, private property, strong
  borders, disciplined institutions, and route stability. INN may
  respect Heinz and Der Himmelskrug as frontier initiative and
  disciplined infrastructure while still asking hard questions about
  private authority and accountability. INN is not Frontier Truthline;
  it can be skeptical and uncomfortable without being bad faith.
- **Galaxy News**: center-left institutional media and liberal cable-news equivalent. Values humanitarian
  systems, mutual aid, cultural pluralism, labor networks, worker
  safety, civic pluralism, and accountability. Galaxy often sees Der
  Himmelskrug as collaborative civic infrastructure but should continue
  asking serious governance, labor, oversight, and
  concentrated-authority questions. Galaxy scrutiny is not automatically
  bad faith.
- **Frontier Truthline**: active far-right / right-populist outrage media ecosystem. It is independent from OmniWay. Its flagship base is Dallas, Texas, Earth, with affiliated streamers, podcasts, local stringers, clip channels, grievance forums, route-based contributors, and touring live events. Frontier Truthline weaponizes real anxieties about private authority, frontier infrastructure, family influence, cultural legitimacy, labor politics, security, and public safety.
  Its main Der Himmelskrug frame is that Heinz is an unelected frontier king using hospitality, family charm, rescue mythology, and route-safety authority to rule The Knot. Its secondary frame attacks the station as soft-pluralist social engineering. Its tertiary frame escalates security and border panic whenever arrivals, transport, customs, dirty-side routing, or public events create exploitable fear.
  Frontier Truthline is an outrage machine, not a truth machine. It should not be written as magically wrong about every fact. Its danger is that real documents, sincere witnesses, procedural mistakes, cultural discomfort, and legitimate governance questions can enter a media system that turns them into grievance, scapegoating, harassment, security panic, and operational pressure.
  Frontier Truthline may align with OmniWay's interests when attacking regulators, unions, restitution frameworks, IFP oversight, station governance, or "elite" civic infrastructure, but that alignment is opportunistic and ideological, not evidence of OmniWay control. It may also attack OmniWay or similar corporations when corporate power fits its grievance narrative.
  Recurring Frontier Truthline frames may include "Who elected the beer king?", "One family should not own The Knot," "Soft borders, hard consequences," "The Knot belongs to route families, not station elites," "They call it pluralism. You call it being processed," and "Every checkpoint has a taproom now." Possible internal brands include **The Truthline Desk** for the flagship studio feed, **The Line Holds** for security, border, and route-panic coverage, and **Truthline Live** for touring grievance events.
- **Redline Commons**: independent far-left / left-populist activist
  feed. It is not controlled by OmniWay. It is sincerely anti-corporate,
  anti-private authority, anti-militarization, pro-worker, pro-resident
  democracy, and skeptical of IFP delegation to semi-private
  institutions. It often raises real questions about Der Himmelskrug’s
  power but can frame them in ideologically rigid, purity-driven,
  implication-heavy, or bad-faith ways. Core Redline frames include “Who
  owns The Knot?”, “beer kings are still kings,” “private rescue is not
  public governance,” “safety feudalism,” “soft-power laundering,” and
  “charismatic infrastructure authority.” Redline should sometimes be
  partly right; that makes it more dangerous and more interesting.
- **Civic Meridian**: centrist media feed known for being neutral to a
  fault. Its motto may be “Context before conclusion.” It is careful,
  process-focused, timeline-oriented, usually accurate, and often useful
  for clean summaries. Its flaw is balance addiction: it may treat every
  dispute as though truth must be halfway between all stated positions,
  even when one side has already acted in bad faith or used violence.
  Example flaw: “both sides before judgment, even when one side has a
  hostage.”
- **Technical feeds**: independent specialist feeds that cover
  telemetry, docking records, course geometry, maintenance evidence,
  route geometry, tactical timelines, engineering claims, safety
  procedures, and race data. They can become influential when public
  narratives depend on technical truth.

- **Starlight Tonight**: entertainment and celebrity-focused interstellar
  feed covering music, film, television, public figures, festivals,
  fashion, relationships, awards-style moments, and pop-culture clips. It
  is glossy, excitable, clever, and sometimes shallow, but not inherently
  malicious or bad faith. It can trivialize serious context by focusing on
  spectacle, romance, celebrity access, viral moments, and public image,
  but it also captures cultural moments that political feeds miss. Its
  Der Himmelskrug coverage treats the station as a major festival venue
  and celebrity setting, with special interest in Heinz's public image,
  Feli's communications role, Heinz/Feli public relationship moments, the
  Explorer Lass ma einen bauen, and musical guest appearances such as
  Culcha Candela.
- **Across the Table**: interstellar food and hospitality feed focused on
  chefs, restaurants, foodways, brewing, agricultural sourcing, service
  culture, and the practical systems behind feeding people across settled
  space. It is curious, sensory, process-oriented, and usually fair. It
  prefers chef, brewer, service-worker, supply-chain, and hospitality
  perspectives over celebrity-founder framing. Its Der Himmelskrug
  coverage focuses on station restaurants, brewing operations, route
  cuisines, ingredient logistics, allergen safety, staff meals,
  restaurant workers, and the people who make hospitality work.
- **Open Route**: interstellar travel and destination feed focused on
  ports, planets, stations, route culture, visitor experience, lodging,
  transit, family travel, accessibility, practical itineraries, respectful
  travel behavior, and the infrastructure behind memorable destinations.
  It is inviting and polished without reality-feed sensationalism. Its
  Der Himmelskrug coverage treats the station as both a destination and
  working route-safety institution, emphasizing Arrival Control, the
  public platz, food, festivals, clean/dirty rules, family-friendly
  navigation, observation views, and practical visitor guidance.
- **RouteCast**: interstellar space-weather and route-conditions feed
  focused on solar activity, radiation storms, beacon status, hyperspace
  corridor conditions, debris advisories, comm distortion, gravity-shear
  warnings, traffic delays, safe-harbor recommendations, station/route
  authority bulletins, and working-pilot route safety. It is pilot-facing,
  calm, practical, and highly trusted by working crews. RouteCast treats
  Der Himmelskrug primarily as route infrastructure at The Knot and
  frequently cites Lichtbrunn Approach advisories from Amara Reid's Flight
  Control team. It is not a political feed, though factual reporting can
  shape political narratives when safety claims are disputed.
- **BeaconWatch**: RouteCast's urgent-alert bulletin format for immediate
  hazard warnings, beacon failures, solar events, route closures,
  emergency holds, safe-harbor advisories, radiation alerts, and
  critical flight-safety updates.
- **Opening-day precedent**: the first dock was a working freighter with
  a crew of five after a successful cargo run; media framed it through
  labor/frontier symbolism in different ways.
- **Media presence rule**: avoid leaving INN, Galaxy News, Civic
  Meridian, Redline Commons, Frontier Truthline, Starlight Tonight, Across the Table, Open
  Route, RouteCast, or other media physically present on station unless
  there is a specific story reason such as a scheduled segment, summit,
  investigation, hearing, public event, weather/route advisory, festival,
  food/travel feature, public-safety review, or negotiated access.

The media rule is mature: honest scrutiny can be painful and valid; bad-faith framing can come from more than one ideology; neutral framing can become morally obtuse; technical truth matters; and private grief does not belong to cameras by default.

Likely reactions to governance reform:

- INN: “Frontier founder accepts oversight while preserving crisis
  command.”
- Galaxy News: “Der Himmelskrug takes major step toward accountable
  civic infrastructure.”
- Redline Commons: “Beer station adds committees, still not democracy.”
- Frontier Truthline: “Beer king adds committees, keeps throne.”
- Civic Meridian: “Der Himmelskrug answers critics with historic
  governance shift; questions remain from all sides.”

Useful media-response principles:

- Answer honest questions; refuse bad-faith conclusions.
- Do not use evidence, family grief, or private harm as propaganda just
  because it helps the station.
- Not every uncomfortable question is an attack.
- Not every neutral frame is wise.
- Not every anti-corporate critique is fair.
- The dead are not content.
