# Writing Style and Continuity Rules

- Tone: warm, irreverent, absurd, funny, and detailed, with emotional
  sincerity under the jokes.

- Comedy should come from culture, names, translation, bureaucracy,
  hospitality, and character reactions, not from making competent people
  stupid.

- Combat/action, when present, must be serious and mechanically
  accurate. Heinz is not a dumb brawler; he is physically imposing but
  most dangerous with lasers, ships, tactical geometry, and calm
  decision-making.

- After serious failures, the station may still include comedy, warmth, and absurdity, but it should not become naive about safety, oversight, or consequence.
  warmth, absurdity, hospitality, music, and running jokes, but major
  safety/security stories should carry the scar of that event. Der
  Himmelskrug knows its systems can be exploited; trust now requires
  verification, outside review, and humility.

- Respect established in-universe mechanics: point-to-point
  teleportation, ship transport, remote authorization, ship recharge
  contacts, Starship Garage / Restricted Works separation, artifact handling, and
  restricted ordnance/rearming procedures.

- Avoid leaving INN and Galaxy News physically present long after
  opening unless there is a specific reason such as a scheduled
  follow-up segment or major event.

- Clara/Klara should not simply harass Tariq. Their dynamic should be
  affectionate exasperation, selective tolerance, and growing respect.
  She can catch him overreaching, but she also recognizes when he is
  useful or right.

- Tariq/Teriq should be written as a genuine professional first, not as
  a standing joke. His "vibes" work is operationally meaningful:
  public mood, soundscape, crowd flow, festival atmosphere,
  de-escalation, acoustic/vibration intuition, cultural programming,
  family wayfinding, and noticing quiet human-pattern failures in public
  space. Comedy may still come from his style, slang, confidence,
  stickers, music, and absurd phrasing, but the story should not treat
  him as useless, unserious, or marginal unless that disrespect itself
  is the conflict being corrected.

- Use the Explorer name Lass ma einen bauen according to its slang
  meaning in context: “Let’s roll one,” not merely the literal “Let’s
  build one.” Literal translations remain useful for misunderstandings.

- Preferred continuity caution: user sometimes writes Clara, Teriq;
  previous drafts used Klara, Tariq. Current guidance: use the user’s
  spelling when they clearly choose it, while preserving continuity of
  the character.

  Heinz clothing continuity: the BEER ME shirt, jeans, sneakers, red
  earpiece, custom wristpad, data slate, backpack, and weapon belt are his iconic
  baseline/public image, not a permanent cartoon uniform. Change his
  clothing when context calls for it: formal station events, engineering
  work, brewery work, private/residential scenes, or combat-rated
  response gear. The red earpiece and custom black-red-gold wristpad may remain
  recurring visual anchors.


  Personal-device continuity: use data slate as the primary personal device
  category. Wristpads and earpieces are paired extensions of the slate, like
  quick-interface and audio endpoints. Do not use "hypernet browser" as a
  separate current device category unless a scene deliberately references
  obsolete terminology.

  Platz acoustics continuity: Der Himmelskrug’s platz is visually
  connected but not acoustically convenient. It is a large, noisy,
  multi-level atrium with fountains, elevators, crowds, music, taproom
  spillover, and traffic announcements. Characters generally cannot hear
  unaided shouting from several levels away; use comms, PA, wrist pads,
  nearby balconies, elevator proximity, signage, line-of-sight gestures,
  or deliberate amplification.

## Name and pronoun usage policy

This rule supersedes any looser name, nickname, or shorthand guidance elsewhere in the package.

- In story prose, use a character's full name only when that character is introduced in that story, or when a formal record, official report, public statement, roster, legal document, or deliberate formal address requires it.
- After the story has introduced the character, use the character's first name or commonly used name.
- If a formal listing contains a quoted name, such as Heinrich “Heinz” Schmitt, Felicia “Feli” Brandt, or Nkiru “Nikki” Adebayo, the quoted name is normally the everyday name for narration and dialogue. The unquoted first name is formal and should be reserved for official records, family/formal address, or deliberate emotional emphasis.
- Before using pronouns for any character, check character canon or quick-reference cards. Do not infer pronouns from name, appearance, role, or earlier flawed drafts. Correct misgendering as a continuity error.



### One-off and supporting character name policy

One-off, guest, victim, witness, perpetrator, contractor, visitor, media, freight, and support-character names must not duplicate or closely resemble established ensemble, recurring, or canon-significant names.

Avoid same first names, near-homophones, visually similar names, or names likely to confuse screen-reader users or continuity review. This includes variants that differ only slightly in spelling when a similar canon name is active.

Supporting-character names should preserve the project’s broad human cultural range. Germanic names are allowed and often appropriate, especially given the Bavarian/German spine of Der Himmelskrug, but they should not dominate the broader pool of one-off and supporting characters unless the story context specifically calls for it. Der Himmelskrug is a route hub serving many human cultures, colonies, crews, faiths, languages, and labor networks; guest names should actively help show that range. Across guest, visitor, freight, media, contractor, witness, victim, and support-character pools, vary naming origins, rhythms, surnames, and colony/route associations so the setting does not accidentally narrow into one cultural lane.

Before naming a new one-off or supporting character, check current ensemble and recurring-character lists, especially station leadership, character quick cards, shorthand decoder, and recent story precedents.
