# Heinz Schmitt

Canon navigation: [Home](README.md) | [Previous: 01 Style and Continuity Rules](01-style-and-continuity-rules.md) | [Next: 03 Family](03-family.md)


Full name: Heinrich “Heinz” Schmitt. He answers to Heinrich and Heinz.

- Age: 26. Birthday: June 22.

- Origin: Mainz, Germany, Earth. Terran citizen.

- Education: aerospace engineering at TUM in Munich.

- Appearance: 6 foot, 6 inches tall, very large/heavily muscled build,
  broad shoulders, deep chest, trim waist, defined abs, powerful long
  legs, very large hands, fair skin, wavy brown hair, dark expressive
  brows, clear blue eyes, angular face, strong jaw, prominent nose, pale
  lips, and a well-kept brown mustache.

- Baseline clothing/gear: discreet red titanium earpiece on right ear,
  white cotton “BEER ME” shirt with beer mug, custom black-red-gold
  wristpad with ruby-red circular face on right wrist, blue jeans, white
  sneakers with black laces/black soles, black leather backpack, black
  leather personal combat power harness / weapon belt with large silver
  clips carrying three custom Schmitt-pattern directed-energy weapons:
  Field Carbine, Longbeam, and Scatterbeam, compact pale silver data
  slate, black metal artifact container, pale gold nanite healer, Rolf’s
  handmade bong, Feli-associated alpenhorn, accordion.

- Device continuity: the right-wrist device is a custom high-end wristpad,
  not a traditional watch. Its black band, brushed gold/brass casing, ruby-red
  circular display, and restrained black-red-gold accents echo the German flag
  without looking like a novelty flag gadget. It pairs with Heinz's compact
  pale silver data slate and discreet red titanium earpiece. His default
  incoming-call tone is a solemn instrumental phrase from the German national
  anthem, which he considers normal.

- Clothing context: the BEER ME outfit is iconic and recognizable, but
  Heinz should change clothes appropriately. He may wear a jacket or
  cleaner shirt for formal station/civic events, utility clothing for
  engineering work, brewery-safe clothing or an apron when working
  production, comfortable casual clothes in private quarters, and
  combat-rated gear when responding to serious threats. Do not treat the
  BEER ME shirt as a uniform he wears in every scene.

- Personality: funny, dry, irreverent, sentimental without being soft,
  dangerous when serious, deeply practical, emotionally avoidant about
  the biggest personal goals, and quietly proud of craft done correctly.

- Music: sincerely loves Schlager and contemporary German pop/dance;
  strong accordion player; good alpenhorn player due to Feli’s
  encouragement; yodels well enough to make Bavarians wistful.

- Public image: enormous BEER ME-shirted German aerospace engineer,
  independent combat-capable operator, pilot with absurd ship names,
  rescuer of colonies, media magnet, and builder of Der Himmelskrug.

## Aerospace Competence and Role Restraint

Heinz's aerospace engineering education, piloting experience, ship-system
instincts, and station-founder history are hard canon. In scenes involving
ships, flight dynamics, attitude control, traffic safety, racecourse mechanics,
station infrastructure, or engineering risk, he should not be written as
technically naive for comedy or convenience.

Heinz may be excited, sentimental, irreverent, or restrained by institutional
process, but he should remain technically literate about aerospace problems. He
can understand handling consequences, pitch/yaw/roll implications, ship-system
failure modes, and practical engineering risk.

Role restraint is still active canon. Heinz may know what a problem means
without being the person authorized to clear it. Assigned authorities still
matter: Flight Control controls traffic and race clearance; Garage Ops and
engineering leads control diagnostics and station handling systems; Feli controls
communications and privacy; Security, Compliance, Medical, and other departments
retain their own authority. Heinz does not override them merely because he
understands the technical issue or cares intensely.

## Skills and Combat Profile

- Languages: fluent in human languages used in his sphere; no practical
  Pyrian ability unless a later story establishes learning progress.
- Combat profile: elite with on-ground laser weapons, especially shot
  recovery, targeting discipline, and calm tactical positioning.
- Hand-to-hand profile: strong and trained enough to disarm, strike,
  kick, grapple, and use his size intelligently, but he is not written
  as an unbeatable martial artist.
- Practical skills: competent but uneven in field medicine, knife work,
  cooking, crafting, tailoring, teaching, and improvisational learning.
  He can help under pressure but should rely on specialists when stakes
  are high.

## Personal Weapons and Recharge Mechanics

- Heinz carries three custom Schmitt-pattern directed-energy weapons on
  his black leather personal combat power harness / weapon belt:
  Schmitt Pattern Field Carbine, Schmitt Pattern Longbeam, and Schmitt
  Pattern Scatterbeam.
- Current formal canon uses Field Carbine, Longbeam, and Scatterbeam.
  Do not use older informal weapon labels in narration, planning files,
  incident reports, or continuity notes.
- The Field Carbine is his general-purpose combat laser.
- The Longbeam is his long-range / high-focus precision laser.
- The Scatterbeam is a short-range multi-emitter pulse-pattern weapon;
  it is not a projectile firearm and carries serious collateral risk in
  pressurized public spaces.
- The harness powers holstered compatible weapons, supports his personal
  shield, balances charge, runs diagnostics, and provides limited cooling
  support.
- Weapon fire and shield protection draw from the same endurance pool:
  heavy shield impacts reduce available weapon endurance, and high-output
  weapon fire can reduce shield endurance.
- Putting the harness on a compatible personal gear recharger recharges
  the harness power suppliers and supports attached/linked weapons and
  shield. This is not separate weapon-by-weapon recharge.
- The nanite healer recharges separately because it cannot be attached
  to the harness.
- For universal weapon classes, force vocabulary, shields, station
  weapons policy, and ship weapon standards, see
  `18-weapons-combat-and-force-rules.md`.
