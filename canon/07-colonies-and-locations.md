## Canon status key

- **Hard canon:** Established by existing canon, accepted story text, or current quick-reference locks. Treat as binding unless deliberately revised.
- **Soft canon:** Approved for future use and available for drafting, but exact details may still flex until used in story.
- **Name bank:** Available names or concepts not yet binding.

## Regional overview

Der Himmelskrug is permanently based in the Fünfstrom-Sektor at Fünfstromknoten, commonly called The Knot by pilots, near the reliable solar-power star Lichtbrunn. Five major traffic streams converge there: the New Bavaria Route, Zion Drift Route, Tannenfall Route, Relay Haven Spur, and Coreward Freight Line.

The setting should feel regionally varied rather than uniformly German. German/Bavarian naming and cultural reference should remain concentrated around Heinz, his family, New Bavaria, Heinz's personal ships, and Der Himmelskrug's deliberate Bavarian/German brewing identity. IFP facilities, unrelated colonies, corporate stations, alien jurisdictions, and coreward institutions should use varied, neutral, institutional, colony-specific, or non-German naming conventions.

The core story ecology is location-first: corporations, unions, co-ops, guilds, freight houses, insurers, and media networks should grow out of specific local economies and legal cultures rather than appear as generic brand names.

## Hard canon locations and jurisdictions

### Der Himmelskrug

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** orbital brewery, free-port, civic hub, route-safety station, clean/dirty logistics facility.  
**Location:** Fünfstromknoten / The Knot, near Lichtbrunn.

Der Himmelskrug is a giant beer-stein-shaped orbital station that looks ridiculous from a distance and becomes more serious the closer a person gets. It is a brewery, port, food hall, hospitality destination, sector support hub, clean/dirty logistics facility, resident community, school site, media venue, and IFP-recognized route-safety institution.

Its public face is warm, musical, multilingual, festive, and sometimes absurd: beer halls, food counters, festival clothes, the observation band, partner-colony shops, family traffic, exhausted haulers, and tourists who absolutely should have read the signs. Under that warmth is a disciplined station culture built around Arrival Control, Flight Control, dirty-side routing, customs, ventilation, weapons checks, food labeling, worker safety, and post-incident review.

Der Himmelskrug is not a sovereign state and not simply Heinz's private property. It is privately founded, partner-backed, and IFP-recognized civic infrastructure. Its legitimacy depends on oversight as much as competence.

**Government / authority:** privately founded and partner-backed, with formal IFP recognition and delegated route-stability authority. The Governing Board, emerging Assembly structures, Flight Control, Security, Customs/Compliance, Medical, Dirty-Side Operations, and IFP liaison authority all matter in their own lanes.

**Economy and exports:** beer, hospitality, food hall operations, route support, docking, cargo handling, maintenance coordination, public events, festival traffic, partner-colony exchange, vendor contracts, and reputation itself.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** adult cannabis use is legal only under station policy in licensed designated areas such as The Green Room. `Grünraum` is the internal/licensing/technical term. Public use outside approved areas is prohibited. No minors, no weapons, no cannabis/alcohol bundle promotions, no unverified products, and no odor bleed into corridors. Destination-law screening is mandatory for takeaway. Legal here does not mean legal in your luggage.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** central to the station. Dirty cargo, maintenance tools, external hull access, biological cargo, industrial residue, weapons, and suspect parts must stay within proper routing. Dirty-side workflow failures can become catastrophic.

**Corporate relevance:** corporations want access to, legitimacy from, contracts with, influence over, or leverage against Der Himmelskrug. The station attracts vendors, auditors, insurers, route-service firms, food suppliers, media bureaus, repair contractors, safety consultants, and predatory actors trying to launder their reputations through a trusted public space.

**Known characters connected:** Heinz, Feli, Rolf, Amara, Janelle, Sanaa, Tariq/Teriq, Davi, Keisha, Lena, Dr. Santos, Nikki, Sven, Elsbeth, Sima, Rennik Cho, and the broader station cast.

**Story uses:** public-space comedy with real safety rules; governance growing pains; labor disputes; sabotage consequences; corporate lobbying; food-safety incidents; festivals; route emergencies; media pressure; grief handled without spectacle.

### Fünfstrom-Sektor / Fünfstromknoten / Lichtbrunn / The Knot

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** strategic route-convergence sector.  
**Role:** Der Himmelskrug's permanent home.

Fünfstromknoten, commonly called The Knot by pilots, is the convergence point of five major routes: the New Bavaria Route, Zion Drift Route, Tannenfall Route, Relay Haven Spur, and Coreward Freight Line. The nearby star Lichtbrunn provides reliable solar-power conditions, making the sector unusually valuable for station infrastructure and traffic support.

The Knot is not just a scenic place to park a novelty brewery. It is a traffic problem made physical. Cargo priorities, passenger confusion, dirty-side hazards, festival traffic, race restrictions, convoy delays, emergency vectors, and partner-colony politics all collide here. Der Himmelskrug exists because this region needs a trusted traffic, hospitality, maintenance, and civic interface.

**Government / authority:** IFP route-stability authority, Der Himmelskrug operational authority within assigned zones, partner-colony agreements, and specific Flight Control instructions all interact here.

**Economy and exports:** route access, docking appointments, cargo movement, emergency routing, solar-power reliability, maintenance windows, passenger transfer, and freight-flow credibility.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** The Knot itself does not need an independent social policy separate from station, ship, IFP, and destination law. Intoxicant issues matter because passengers cross jurisdictions here. A traveler may arrive from permissive Zion Drift and depart toward restrictive Tannenfall or severe Veyr-adjacent space.

**Corporate relevance:** prime territory for freight corporations, insurers, route-data firms, maintenance vendors, executive travel services, discount competitors, appointment-slot brokers, and route-advisory media.

**Story uses:** traffic disputes; route closures; multi-jurisdiction mistakes; corporate pressure over docking priorities; small hauler complaints; Flight Control authority; the principle that elections, festivals, money, and status do not modify traffic law.

### New Bavaria

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** Bavarian-influenced human colony.  
**Known places:** Grün-Sechs port, festival districts, Sankt Vroni traditions.

New Bavaria is a German/Bavarian-influenced human colony with strong brewing, festival, craft, family, and civic traditions. It is one of Der Himmelskrug's key legitimacy partners, not just a decorative cultural source. Its people helped make the station's Bavarian identity feel earned rather than costumed.

New Bavaria values public order, hospitality, tradition, technical competence, and proper respect for the dead. It can be warm and funny, but it is not unserious. Its institutions care about how things are named, documented, blessed, mourned, licensed, and explained to families.

New Bavaria's connection to Der Himmelskrug is emotionally and politically important. The colony remains a foundational partner with the right to ask hard questions about whether the station deserves trust.

**Government / authority:** local colony government, port authority, cultural and festival offices, liaison offices, and route agreements with Der Himmelskrug and the IFP.

**Economy and exports:** brewing culture, beer and brewing supplies, festival logistics, food goods, crafts, cultural programming, agricultural products, design legitimacy, and family businesses.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** cautious. Medical allowances exist, but public intoxication and cannabis/alcohol pairing are socially and politically sensitive. Beer culture does not mean all impairment is treated casually. Public hospitality norms, safety-sensitive work, festival family spaces, and dignity matter.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** festival cargo, food products, brewing materials, cultural goods, family passenger traffic, and memorial or religious materials need clean documentation and respectful handling.

**Corporate relevance:** supports breweries, agricultural processors, festival logistics firms, craft exporters, design guilds, family shipping businesses, and cultural licensing bodies. It resists corporations that flatten tradition into branding while ignoring responsibility.

**Known characters connected:** Senior Liaison Anja Reiter, Clara/Klara, Tante Hedi, Maren Kapfer, Paul, and New Bavaria route families.

**Story uses:** family visits; festival politics; brewing standards; cultural appropriation versus cultural partnership; colony officials trying to protect both tradition and modern infrastructure.

### Zion Drift / Port Irie

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** human colony and major partner route.  
**Known identity:** Rasta/reggae/tropical/community influence; warm, musical, competent.

Zion Drift, with Port Irie as its most important known port identity, is a warm, musical, community-centered colony tied to Der Himmelskrug through rhythm, food, craft, rum, spice, performance, hospitality, and people. It should always be written with care: relaxed does not mean careless, musical does not mean unserious, and warmth does not mean lack of discipline.

Zion Drift gives Der Himmelskrug some of its soul. Its influence can be felt in music programming, festival nights, food stalls, public-space design, craft goods, and the station's understanding that atmosphere is operational, not decorative.

This is the home cultural context for Amara Reid, Davi, and Tariq/Teriq. That matters because all three show different forms of competence: Flight Control command, culinary/community exchange, and public-experience work.

**Government / authority:** local colony government, port authority, route agreements, cultural offices, and normal IFP route frameworks.

**Economy and exports:** rum, spices, music, hospitality goods, craft products, performance programming, culinary exchange, cannabis-adjacent products where locally legal, and public-atmosphere expertise.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** broadly permissive. Adult cannabis use is culturally normal in many contexts, especially around music, rest, hospitality, private life, and medical use. However, crew impairment rules remain strict. Zion Drift permissiveness does not override Der Himmelskrug station rules or destination law.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** spice, plant, food, rum, cannabis-adjacent, craft, and performance cargo require clear declaration when crossing into stricter destinations. Legal origin does not make mixed-route cargo harmless.

**Corporate relevance:** rum distillers, spice exporters, music and performance agencies, hospitality cooperatives, cannabis cultivators, wellness firms, craft houses, and cultural-tourism operators. Corporate misuse risk: outsiders packaging Zion Drift culture while underpaying performers, growers, kitchen labor, or station-atmosphere specialists.

**Known characters connected:** Amara Reid, Davi, Tariq/Teriq.

**Story uses:** music episodes; cultural exchange; cannabis law contrast; public-space design; Tariq/Teriq proving that vibes are real operations; permissive-origin cargo colliding with restrictive destination law.

### Tannenfall / Larkspur Landing

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** agricultural-engineering colony.  
**Known identity:** orchards, reliable weather control, strict customs, tidy civic culture.

Tannenfall is a conservative, orderly agricultural-engineering colony where orchards, weather control, compliance labels, customs protocols, and food-chain integrity matter deeply. Larkspur Landing is the known port identity and should feel clean, efficient, well-labeled, and quietly judgmental in the way only a customs office with excellent stationery can be.

Tannenfall is not anti-fun. It is anti-contamination, anti-chaos, and anti-people-who-think probably fine belongs near a food chain. Its culture prizes continuity, traceability, pruning schedules, declared cargo, and the kind of civic patience that makes other people feel underdressed.

Its relationship with Der Himmelskrug is valuable because it forces the station to prove that hospitality and safety can coexist. Tannenfall's board presence gives customs and compliance a political voice, not just an administrative one.

**Government / authority:** local colony government, customs prefecture, agricultural boards, compliance offices, port authority, and route agreements.

**Economy and exports:** apple brandy, orchard products, weather-control expertise, compliance labels, agricultural robotics, biosecurity services, and Tannenfall Cultural Appendix material.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** restrictive rather than purely prohibitionist. Medical use is tightly regulated. Recreational possession or use exists only in licensed zones or declared private-use contexts. Public use is prohibited. Plant material, seeds, residue, oils, molds, unlabeled food products, and uninspected botanical cargo can trigger inspection or decontamination review.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** unusually strict. Agricultural cargo, botanical matter, soil, dust, spores, live cultures, food packaging, alcohol, and botanical intoxicants require careful declaration and route separation.

**Corporate relevance:** orchard exporters, weather-control maintenance firms, food-labeling auditors, agricultural robotics companies, biosecurity labs, cider/brandy producers, and compliance consultancies. Corporate tension comes from firms trying to bypass fussy rules that are actually life support for an agricultural economy.

**Known characters connected:** Deputy Customs Prefect Mara Venn; Elsbeth Ruun, Senior Compliance Orchardist and Route Continuity Advocate / Tannenfall board representative.

**Story uses:** customs comedy; mislabeled food or cargo; Tannenfall passengers reacting to The Green Room; agricultural biosecurity; Elsbeth being technically correct and politically inconvenient; labels are life support.

### Relay Haven

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** vulnerable service station between trade lanes.  
**Known identity:** survival memory, coffee culture, recovered station, board partner.

Relay Haven is a smaller human service station that survived earlier danger and understands how fragile small-service infrastructure can be under outside pressure. Its identity is shaped by vulnerability, stubbornness, service labor, improvised competence, and memory.

Relay Haven should feel more worn than Der Himmelskrug. Its corridors are not designed to impress tourists. Its best people keep systems running because if the old beacon dies, someone may not make it home. It has coffee culture, service-station humor, patched panels, and the kind of quiet civic pride that comes from surviving when help was late.

Its board representative, Sima, gives Relay Haven a voice in Der Himmelskrug's governance. Mina Kade and Oran Pell anchor its survival memory: people who kept systems alive and protected civilians when institutions failed or were manipulated.

**Government / authority:** station authority, route-guild relationships, partner-board representation through Sima, IFP-recognized route relevance, and local service-station emergency protocols.

**Economy and exports:** coffee goods, beacon support, repair basics, crew rest, emergency shelter, route memory, service labor, and credibility earned through survival.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** pragmatic and tolerant in some zones, but strict about safety-duty impairment. Supply controls may be uneven. Maintenance mistakes can endanger survival infrastructure, so impairment around beacon, comms, docking, life support, and emergency systems is treated severely.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** patched systems and limited capacity make clear routing important. Emergency repairs, beacon hardware, salvage, fuel, old parts, and improvised cargo must be handled with discipline rather than romance.

**Corporate relevance:** vulnerable to asset-protection firms, discount infrastructure operators, predatory maintenance contracts, emergency-credit lenders, and route-service consolidators. Also a natural place for repair co-ops, coffee suppliers, beacon technicians, route-guild mutual aid, and anti-consolidation politics.

**Known characters connected:** Sima; Mina Kade, backup comms/galley tech who survived after keeping the old beacon alive; Oran Pell, route-guild pilot killed protecting civilians.

**Story uses:** corporate overreach; recovery politics; coffee as civic symbol; small station dignity; why partner-route solidarity matters. Key phrase: THE COFFEE IS BETTER NOW.

### IFP Bastion Meridian

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** hardened IFP rapid-response installation.  
**Role:** secure base for combat readiness and Heinz's dedicated combat ships.

IFP Bastion Meridian is not a public station, tourist stop, hospitality port, or civilian hangout. It is a hardened security installation built for combat readiness, emergency deployment, secure ship storage, rearming, maintenance, tactical coordination, and authorized personnel transport.

Its tone is functional, controlled, and unforgiving. Nothing there exists because it is charming. Access requires valid authorization, destination acceptance, and IFP security clearance. Bastion Meridian can deny, delay, reroute, or lock down transport during security events.

Heinz's major combat ships normally belong here rather than at Der Himmelskrug. That distinction matters. Der Himmelskrug is civic infrastructure with public trust obligations. Bastion Meridian is where serious force is staged, controlled, reviewed, armed, and deployed.

**Government / authority:** IFP military/security command structure. Der Himmelskrug does not control Bastion Meridian.

**Economy and exports:** not a normal public economy. Its relevant activity is readiness, repair, classified maintenance, tactical logistics, authorized transport, and secure storage.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** functionally severe in all operational zones because of military/security readiness. Even if personal off-duty rules exist elsewhere in IFP life, intoxicant use near deployment, weapons, rearming, tactical operations, or secure transport must never be casual.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** secure rather than hospitality-driven. Weapons, classified components, combat-damaged hull sections, reactor parts, shield modules, and rearming workflows require strict separation and authorization.

**Corporate relevance:** defense contractors, secure maintenance vendors, classified logistics suppliers, military auditors, and IFP procurement offices may orbit this place, but civilian corporations do not own it. Corporate corruption here should be high-stakes and rare, not routine sitcom friction.

**Known characters connected:** Heinz, IFP personnel, combat ship maintenance crews.

**Story uses:** emergency deployment; contrast with Der Himmelskrug; secure transport; combat-ship access; IFP oversight; showing that Heinz's force is not just sitting under the beer hall.

### Earth / Mainz / Munich / TUM

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** origin world and education/cultural anchor.  
**Known places:** Mainz, Munich, TUM, Eisgrub-Bräu.

Earth is not the center of every story, but it remains emotionally important because Heinz is from Mainz and studied at TUM in Munich. Earth carries family, education, brewing tradition, early friendships, old expectations, and the Schmitt family’s life outside Der Himmelskrug.

Mainz is the Schmitt family anchor: Eisgrub-Bräu, Matthias, Karin, Lukas's roots, Annika's university life, and the ordinary family reality that keeps Heinz from becoming only a mythic station founder. Munich and TUM anchor Heinz's aerospace education, Rolf, Feli, and the early social growth that made the later station possible.

Earth should feel older, denser, more administratively layered, and emotionally familiar. It is the place where interstellar infrastructure becomes a family phone call, a brewery ledger, a university corridor, or a sister walking home after surviving a corporate-linked assault.

**Government / authority:** Earth and its regional/national/local governments remain complex and varied. Use specific jurisdictions only when a story needs them.

**Economy and exports:** universities, banks, old breweries, aerospace research, legal firms, cultural products, media bureaus, and corporate headquarters.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** leave detailed Earth cannabis law flexible unless a story needs a specific jurisdiction. For Mainz/New Bavaria-adjacent family tone, public intoxication and cannabis/alcohol pairing should be treated cautiously. Earth legal diversity can be useful, but avoid turning it into a lecture unless plot-relevant.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** Earth-origin goods and personnel enter interstellar routes through formal customs, university, corporate, or port channels. Earth is emotionally familiar but not administratively simple.

**Corporate relevance:** Earth supports universities, banks, old breweries, aerospace research institutions, media bureaus, legal firms, and corporate headquarters. OmniWay-linked retaliation against Annika proves Earth is not automatically safe just because it is home.

**Known characters connected:** Heinz, Matthias, Karin, Lukas, Annika, Rolf, Feli, Schmitt family, Eisgrub-Bräu.

**Story uses:** family pressure; proposal/marriage anxiety; Annika's recovery and anger discipline; academic contacts; old classmates; corporate retaliation reaching home.

### Coreward Freight Line

**Status:** hard canon.  
**Type:** major freight corridor.  
**Role:** economically crucial traffic stream feeding The Knot.

The Coreward Freight Line is less culturally unified than the partner-colony routes but economically essential. It is the heavy bloodstream of The Knot: freight schedules, cargo priority disputes, bulk haulers, logistics deadlines, perishables, components, insurance clauses, route advisories, and tired crews who do not care how charming the stein looks if their appointment slot is late.

Coreward's identity is practical, impatient, and commercially diverse. It is not one colony. It is a route culture made of dispatchers, haulers, brokers, cargo-flow advocates, repair needs, and pressure from clients several jumps away.

Rennik Cho represents Coreward Freight Line interests on the Governing Board. Sanaa El-Masri is from the Coreward Freight Line but represents Der Himmelskrug Stores/Supply Chain internally, not Coreward on the board.

**Government / authority:** mixed corridor law, IFP route frameworks, shipmaster authority, cargo contracts, and Der Himmelskrug operational authority when traffic enters station-controlled space.

**Economy and exports:** freight movement, cold chain, bulk cargo, parts, perishables, insurance, fuel, docking slots, route advisories, and cargo-flow data.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** route-dependent. The Coreward Freight Line requires destination checks rather than a single social norm. Shipmaster authority and crew contracts matter. Safety-duty impairment is strict.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** high volume makes classification and separation essential. Dirty industrial cargo, biological freight, food cargo, restricted goods, and passenger transfer must not blur.

**Corporate relevance:** freight giants, cargo brokers, insurers, cold-chain operators, route-data firms, discount rest-stop chains, lien companies, and predatory appointment-slot services.

**Known characters connected:** Rennik Cho; Sanaa El-Masri by origin, though her station role is Der Himmelskrug Stores/Supply Chain.

**Story uses:** cargo priority disputes; independent haulers versus big shippers; small-operator escalation path; docking fairness; supply-chain stories; corporate pressure without needing a villain in a cape.

### Meridian Rest Platform

**Status:** hard canon, underdescribed before this gazetteer expansion.  
**Type:** OmniWay-linked or OmniWay-adjacent rest/service platform.  
**Role:** cautionary contrast to Der Himmelskrug.

Meridian Rest Platform is a polished, discount-route service platform associated with OmniWay's infrastructure model. It likely sold itself as efficient, affordable, predictable, and frictionless. Its public surfaces were clean enough to photograph, with simplified menus, bundled rest packages, route discounts, and cheerful signage that made travelers feel processed rather than welcomed.

Its deeper function is contrast. Where Der Himmelskrug is chaotic but accountable, Meridian Rest represents infrastructure optimized for margin, control, and plausible deniability. It should not be cartoonishly filthy. The danger is subtler: thin staffing, underpaid maintenance, outsourced security, minimized complaints, quiet data capture, and safety culture shaped by quarterly reports.

**Government / authority:** corporate platform governance under whatever route, IFP, and local agreements applied before OmniWay's public collapse or exposure. Exact current status can remain story-dependent.

**Economy and exports:** discount lodging, food-service bundles, route rest, cargo-adjacent service packages, passenger processing, data capture, and contractor relationships.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** likely restrictive by corporate policy, not necessarily moral principle. It may ban or tightly limit intoxicants to reduce liability while quietly profiting from alcohol, sleep aids, or paid wellness services. Exact policy can remain flexible.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** likely technically compliant on paper while vulnerable to shortcut culture, outsourced inspection, and minimized reporting.

**Corporate relevance:** OmniWay, subcontractors, asset-protection firms, food-service chains, discount lodging, app-based cargo services, outsourced cleaning/maintenance, and reputation-management vendors.

**Known characters connected:** OmniWay-linked actors and possible ex-employees or witnesses.

**Story uses:** OmniWay contrast; recovered documents; witnesses; ex-employees; corporate influence; cheap infrastructure whose real cost was hidden in labor and safety.

### Veyr Compact

**Status:** hard canon as a known severe jurisdiction; details still expandable.  
**Type:** external jurisdiction / alien or nonhuman legal-cultural power.  
**Role:** sharp contrast for intoxicant, customs, and diplomatic law.

The Veyr Compact is a severe external jurisdiction whose law treats certain unauthorized intoxicant or neuroactive violations with extreme seriousness, including possible execution statute or permanent penal exile for trafficking, undeclared import, restricted-zone possession, distribution to crew, or repeat smuggling.

The Compact should not be written as evil because strict. It should feel old, precise, jurisdictionally serious, and uninterested in whether tourists found the warning inconvenient. Its legal culture may be based on historical disasters involving neuroactive contamination, crew coercion, military mutiny, spiritual violation, biological vulnerability, or social collapse. The exact reason can be developed later, but the severity should feel internally coherent.

Der Himmelskrug's policy toward Veyr-bound travelers should be careful, multilingual, documented, and almost painfully explicit. Feli's office, Customs, The Green Room staff, and Flight Control all have reasons to care. A funny mistake here can become a diplomatic nightmare.

**Government / authority:** Veyr Compact law and enforcement. IFP, station, and origin-world law do not erase Veyr jurisdiction once a traveler or cargo enters its legal boundary.

**Economy and exports:** leave exact Veyr economy flexible. Its immediate story function is legal contrast, diplomatic pressure, and customs severity.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** severe prohibition jurisdiction. No sealed takeaway sales to Veyr-bound passengers or crew. Passengers bound for Veyr jurisdiction must be offered surrender, storage, disposal, or compliant sealed-transfer options before boarding.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** neuroactive, biological, food, medical, botanical, and sealed personal cargo require strict screening. Translation errors can become legal errors.

**Corporate relevance:** corporations fear Veyr enforcement but may exploit Veyr restrictions for compliance products, secure cargo certifications, legal insurance, or black-market smuggling. This is a natural place for a corporation to claim it makes compliance simple and then fail dangerously.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet.

**Story uses:** destination-law tension; customs warnings; diplomatic incidents; smuggling plots; Feli making frightening law understandable; station policy saving someone from themselves.

## Soft canon expansion locations

### Vesta Crown

**Status:** soft canon.  
**Type:** asteroid-belt manufacturing habitat.  
**Primary role:** precision fabrication, ship components, pressure hull panels, replacement parts.

Vesta Crown is a hard-hat manufacturing habitat built into and around a dense asteroid cluster. It produces the kind of parts nobody sings about until they fail: pressure panels, hull ribs, heat-stressed brackets, docking collars, pump housings, tool assemblies, certified replacement kits, and ugly components with beautiful tolerances.

Its culture is practical, loud, union-heavy, and allergic to glossy nonsense. Vesta Crown workers trust documented torque values, safety holds, shop stewards, and scars on machinery more than executive presentations. They are not anti-business. They are anti-being-rushed-into-killing-someone.

The habitat should feel industrial but alive: machine halls, rotating shift diners, union notice boards, vibration underfoot, pressure doors with handwritten jokes, and old mechanics who can hear a bad bearing through three walls.

**Government / authority:** habitat authority, fabrication guilds or unions, safety inspectors, route-certification offices, and contract courts. Exact structure can flex by story.

**Economy and exports:** precision parts, pressure hull components, docking collars, certified replacement kits, shop tools, inspection services, and fabrication expertise.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** moderate and labor-contract-driven. Off-duty adult use may be legal in residential zones, but impairment around fabrication, pressure work, welding, heavy machinery, inspection, and load testing is treated seriously. Their culture is likely less moralistic than New Bavaria but stricter than Zion Drift on shop-floor sobriety.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** metal dust, tool cartridges, fabricated assemblies, sealed kits, pressure-rated components, cutting residue, and certification tags require careful handling. Tool-cartridge traceability from places like Vesta Crown matters enormously.

**Corporate relevance:** fabrication guilds, unionized suppliers, tool-certification firms, hull-part manufacturers, and quality auditors. Vesta Crown gives the universe a place for honest but expensive industrial suppliers.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet. Rolf would likely respect the place quickly.

**Story uses:** forged parts; supplier disputes; union solidarity; Rolf respecting someone instantly; a corporation smearing safety delays as laziness; dirty-side cartridge-supplier investigations.

### Pelican Yards

**Status:** soft canon.  
**Type:** orbital freight-and-repair yard above an ocean colony.  
**Primary role:** freighter refit, cargo pod rebuilds, salvage conversion, small-ship repair.

Pelican Yards is where ships go when they are too useful to scrap and too damaged to pretend they are fine. It orbits an ocean colony and carries a shipyard culture full of cranes, pressure scaffolds, dockside superstition, greasy lunch counters, cargo nets, welders, hull singers, and pilots arguing with mechanics who are usually right.

It should feel less polished than Der Himmelskrug but more socially open than Bastion Meridian. Pelican Yards lives on repair, not spectacle. Its people know the difference between a captain who is broke, a captain who is lying, and a captain who is both.

The yards are a natural gathering point for independent haulers, salvage crews, insurance adjusters, shipwrights, repair apprentices, and old captains who remember which corporations pay claims and which ones send condolences with invoice numbers.

**Government / authority:** yard authority, port safety office, salvage courts, repair guilds, insurers, and local colony oversight.

**Economy and exports:** freighter repair, refits, cargo pod rebuilds, salvage conversion, tug services, spare parts, ship paint, emergency patchwork, and repair apprenticeships.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** port-zone regulated. Off-duty use may exist in licensed crew areas, but shipyard work, tug operations, crane control, hull cutting, fuel handling, and flight clearance require strict sobriety.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** hull damage, fuel residue, salvage contamination, industrial tools, cargo pod residue, pressure assemblies, and shipyard cutting waste require strict separation.

**Corporate relevance:** repair houses, salvage brokers, parts suppliers, insurers, refit lenders, ship-paint firms, tug operators, and legal offices specializing in liens and damage claims.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet.

**Story uses:** small hauler debt; emergency refit; ugly repair compromise; salvage ethics; shipyard comedy; a captain choosing between bankruptcy and unsafe work.

### Lagrange Nine Commercial Ring

**Status:** soft canon.  
**Type:** polished corporate station near a major Earth/coreward route hub.  
**Primary role:** finance, insurance, arbitration, media offices, executive transit.

Lagrange Nine Commercial Ring is a corporate station designed to make power look calm. It has quiet carpets, glass partitions, controlled lighting, discreet security, excellent espresso, expensive silence, and meeting rooms where nobody raises their voice because the contract already did the violence.

This is where insurers, banks, corporate law firms, cargo-risk pools, media bureaus, executive travel services, public-relations firms, and arbitration houses cluster. People here speak in risk language. They do not say they abandoned a station. They say asset exposure exceeded recovery tolerance.

Lagrange Nine is useful because it gives the setting a place for non-combat corporate conflict. Feli can hate it with a smile. Clara/Klara can turn legal language into a knife. Heinz can feel like the exposed pipes are missing and therefore everyone is lying.

**Government / authority:** station commercial authority, arbitration panels, corporate lease law, executive security, IFP liaison offices, and route-finance regulators.

**Economy and exports:** finance, insurance, legal services, corporate arbitration, media bureau space, reputation management, executive travel, risk scoring, and contract enforcement.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** highly regulated by liability culture. Licensed private clubs may exist for executives and residents, but public use is discouraged or prohibited. The real concern is not morality; it is reputational, contractual, and insurance exposure.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** physical dirty routing is less central than data, evidence, executive security, legal records, and sealed corporate cargo. Clean surfaces can hide dirty contracts.

**Corporate relevance:** headquarters, arbitration panels, banks, insurers, media bureaus, compliance consultants, reputation management, executive security, and corporate intelligence.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet, though Feli, Clara/Klara, media figures, insurers, and OmniWay-adjacent executives can plausibly appear here.

**Story uses:** board hearings; insurance disputes; OmniWay fallout; media negotiations; settlement talks; contract traps; polite villains; Feli winning by making language exact.

### Saint Brigid's Lantern

**Status:** soft canon.  
**Type:** medical hospice waystation and crew-care port.  
**Primary role:** long-haul rest, grief care, medical transfer, chaplaincy, family notification support.

Saint Brigid's Lantern is an older waystation with monastery-descended traditions, now functioning as a medical rest, hospice, crew-care, and grief-support port. Its corridors are quiet without being sterile. It has soup, prayer rooms, family waiting rooms, old lamps, practical nurses, transport chaplains, social workers, and medical staff who know how to stand beside people without turning grief into theater.

It should be multi-faith and secular-capable, not narrowly denominational despite the name. Saint Brigid may be historical, cultural, or inherited from the founding relief order. The point is care, shelter, and dignity.

This location gives the universe a place where aftermath can breathe. Not every consequence belongs in a hearing room or a firefight.

**Government / authority:** medical port authority, hospice care board, chaplaincy or relief-order governance, local medical law, insurance interface, and IFP medical-transfer protocols.

**Economy and exports:** medical transfer, hospice care, crew rest, grief counseling, family notification support, medical supply logistics, and charity transport.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** medical cannabinoids may be allowed under strict clinical supervision, especially for pain, appetite, trauma, or end-of-life care. Recreational use is likely prohibited in medical, hospice, and family-care zones. Visitors are expected to be sober enough to consent, grieve, and not burden staff.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** medical biohazards, patient transfer, sealed remains, medicines, personal effects, medical waste, and family-access corridors require dignity and discipline.

**Corporate relevance:** medical contractors, charity transport funds, insurance companies, pharmaceutical suppliers, grief-care foundations, and predatory claim administrators who should be treated with suspicion.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet. Dr. Santos, Feli, or Father Anton's wider religious/community network could connect naturally.

**Story uses:** medical transfer disputes; insurance cruelty; quiet scenes for Feli, Dr. Santos, or Heinz; a place where comedy softens but does not vanish.

### Caldera City

**Status:** soft canon.  
**Type:** volcanic/geothermal industrial colony.  
**Primary role:** power generation, mineral refining, ceramics, heat-shield materials, industrial exports.

Caldera City is built around heat: geothermal vents, volcanic glass, refinery towers, ceramic kilns, heat-exchanger farms, thermal tunnels, and festivals that treat endurance as civic art. It is industrial, colorful, noisy, and proud of making materials that survive conditions other colonies only model on screens.

The colony should not feel grimdark. It is dangerous but vibrant. Workers know ash forecasts the way farmers know rain. Cafes serve food designed for people coming off hot shifts. Local jokes involve burns, pressure seals, and tourists who wore the wrong shoes.

Caldera's exports are essential and dirty-side complicated. Its cargo can include refined minerals, hot-process ceramics, shielding tiles, industrial catalysts, abrasive dust, and containers that absolutely do not belong in clean passenger corridors.

**Government / authority:** colony industrial authority, geothermal/mining regulators, labor unions, environmental monitors, refinery boards, and port hazmat offices.

**Economy and exports:** geothermal power, minerals, ceramics, heat-shield materials, industrial catalysts, refinery products, thermal engineering, and ruggedized industrial gear.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** local recreational use may be tolerated in residential and festival areas, but industrial impairment rules are strict. Heat work, refinery duty, pressure systems, heavy transport, and hazardous-material handling require sobriety.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** extremely high. Ash, mineral dust, abrasive residue, hot-process ceramics, refinery cargo, catalysts, and industrial containers belong in declared dirty-side channels.

**Corporate relevance:** energy companies, mining firms, refinery contractors, heat-shield manufacturers, environmental monitors, labor unions, and industrial insurers.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet.

**Story uses:** dirty-side routing pressure; contamination mistakes; corporate shortcuts; mining labor disputes; essential supplier with ugly politics; Tannenfall objecting to Caldera residue near food-chain cargo.

### Glasswater

**Status:** soft canon.  
**Type:** domed lake colony.  
**Primary role:** water purification, aquaculture, bioplastics, algae cultures, life-support bio-materials.

Glasswater is a quiet, reflective colony built around protected lakes, transparent domes, water-processing towers, aquaculture basins, algae farms, and life-support research. It is beautiful in a restrained way: water light on ceilings, soft public voices, strict lab doors, and signs that look gentle until they deny your cargo.

Glasswater's people are polite because panic is bad for containment. They are not relaxed about biology. A teaspoon of the wrong culture in the wrong tank can become an economic, ecological, or life-support disaster.

Glasswater pairs well with Tannenfall but should feel different. Tannenfall is orchard, soil, weather, and label discipline. Glasswater is water, filtration, microbes, controlled growth, and soft-spoken refusal.

**Government / authority:** colony water authority, aquaculture boards, biosecurity offices, life-support regulators, lab licensing bodies, and port containment inspectors.

**Economy and exports:** water purification systems, aquaculture, algae cultures, bioplastics, life-support media, filtration systems, and food-science exports.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** plant material, oils, edibles, vape residues, and culture media are tightly controlled near aquaculture and life-support zones. Medical or adult use may be permitted in residential areas, but biological import rules are strict. Unlabeled botanical products are a customs problem.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** biological cargo, cultures, water-treatment media, food products, oils, residues, packaging, and personal botanicals need careful separation.

**Corporate relevance:** life-support firms, water recyclers, aquaculture exporters, algae-bioplastic companies, bio-lab vendors, filtration auditors, and food-science corporations.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet. Sanaa or Keisha would have strong reason to care about Glasswater suppliers.

**Story uses:** food safety; mislabeled organic products; contamination scares; life-support supplier disputes; a gentle customs officer ruining someone's day correctly; Sanaa or Keisha in their element.

### Kestrel Minor

**Status:** soft canon.  
**Type:** small debtor port and independent-hauler enclave.  
**Primary role:** low-cost docking, used parts, cargo brokerage, lien courts, desperate but real community.

Kestrel Minor is the kind of port where ships arrive with one good engine, three bad contracts, and a captain insisting everything is under control. It is not lawless, but it is poor, over-leveraged, and crowded with people one bad inspection away from losing the only thing they own.

Its public spaces should feel tired but not hopeless: old neon, pawned ship parts, cheap noodles, honest mechanics, lien notices, bulletin boards, cargo brokers, debt counselors, and pilots pretending not to read the emergency loan terms.

Kestrel Minor is important because it shows the underside of the route economy. Small operators are not automatically noble, and debt brokers are not always illegal. But the system can corner people into choices that look reckless from a clean boardroom.

**Government / authority:** port authority, lien courts, cargo brokers, debt courts, repair inspectors, local security, and patchwork mutual-aid systems.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** patchy and economically shaped. Cheap intoxicants may circulate, but port authority cracks down hard when impairment touches docking, ship repair, cargo handling, or debt-court appearances. Smuggling risk is real.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** used parts, salvage, poor documentation, rushed repairs, and desperate shortcuts make classification difficult. Kestrel cargo should often require extra verification at Der Himmelskrug without treating Kestrel crews as automatically criminal.

### Veyr Compact Border Station

**Status:** soft canon extension of hard-canon Veyr jurisdiction.  
**Type:** border/customs/security station.  
**Primary role:** controlled entry into Veyr jurisdiction; customs, neuroactive control, diplomatic transit.

The Veyr Compact Border Station is the physical face of Veyr law: precise, severe, quiet, and impossible to charm. It has clean lines, controlled lighting, minimal ornament, layered waiting areas, translation chambers, sealed evidence lockers, and officials who do not mistake friendliness for compliance.

This station is where Der Himmelskrug's destination-law warnings become real. A passenger who laughed at The Green Room staff is no longer funny here. The border station does not care that the package was legal at origin, that the label was cute, or that the traveler did not mean it.

The trick is to make the Veyr officials fair within their own system. They can be frightening without being stupid or sadistic. That makes the warning more powerful.

**Government / authority:** Veyr Compact customs, border security, legal translation offices, evidence officials, diplomatic transit officers, and restricted-cargo courts.

**Economy and exports:** customs control, certified transfer, diplomatic transit, secure disposal, legal translation, and restricted-cargo screening.

**Cannabis / intoxicant law:** severe prohibition. Unauthorized intoxicants and neuroactives may trigger extreme penalties depending on quantity, intent, location, and prior conduct. Veyr-bound travelers from Der Himmelskrug should be screened carefully before departure.

**Clean/dirty routing concerns:** neurological, biological, food, medical, botanical, personal, diplomatic, and sealed corporate cargo all require exact screening. A mislabeled edible is not a cute mistake here.

**Corporate relevance:** secure-transfer firms, compliance insurers, customs brokers, translation-law consultants, diplomatic couriers, black-market smugglers, and companies selling Veyr-safe certification.

**Known characters connected:** none locked yet.

**Story uses:** near-miss customs incident; diplomatic rescue; Feli's warning language preventing disaster; smuggling hidden inside legal cargo; corporation falsely certifying a product as Veyr-safe.

## Interstellar Federation of Planets / IFP

The Interstellar Federation of Planets, usually shortened to IFP, is the major interstellar security, diplomatic, and route-stability authority recognized across much of settled space. It is not a single-planet military and not merely a police force. Its practical role includes route security, colony defense coordination, emergency response, interstellar law-enforcement support, traffic-safety agreements, diplomatic protection, and recognition of major infrastructure authorities.

Der Himmelskrug's authority at Fünfstromknoten / The Knot comes from formal IFP recognition and delegated route-stability authority, with additional authority and legitimacy provided by the partner colony governments that helped build, staff, supply, and politically support the station. The IFP recognizes Der Himmelskrug as operational infrastructure for Lichtbrunn Approach and the five converging routes, giving the station authority over traffic control, docking priority, emergency routing, safety enforcement, summit-security coordination, and clean/dirty approach compliance within its assigned operational zone.

Partner colonies such as New Bavaria, Zion Drift, Tannenfall, and Relay Haven provide additional legal, cultural, economic, and political backing. They have real stakes in the station's success because their routes, citizens, businesses, crews, consulates, festivals, and reputations are tied to Der Himmelskrug's stability. This makes Der Himmelskrug neither a sovereign state nor merely Heinz's private beer station. It is a privately founded, partner-backed, IFP-recognized civic and route-safety institution with delegated authority at The Knot.

IFP Bastion Meridian is the secured rapid-response installation where Heinz's major combat ships are normally based. Bastion Meridian is not a public station, hospitality port, or civilian hub. It is a hardened IFP facility built for combat readiness, emergency deployment, secure ship storage, rearming, maintenance, tactical coordination, and authorized personnel transport. Access requires valid authorization, destination acceptance, and IFP security clearance; Bastion Meridian can deny, delay, reroute, or lock down transport during security events.

## Corporate geography seed rule

When adding corporations, first decide where they grew up, what local problem they originally solved, what scale changed or corrupted, and what Der Himmelskrug needs from them, fears from them, or competes with them over.

Location should shape corporate behavior:

- Vesta Crown corporations should understand fabrication, certification, union pressure, and safety delays.
- Pelican Yards corporations should understand ships, salvage, refit debt, and repair compromise.
- Lagrange Nine corporations should understand finance, law, risk, reputation, arbitration, and executive distance.
- Saint Brigid's Lantern organizations should understand care, grief, medicine, and the moral ugliness of insurance denial.
- Caldera City corporations should understand heat, power, mining, hazardous cargo, labor, and environmental regulation.
- Glasswater corporations should understand water, biology, containment, food safety, and life-support trust.
- Kestrel Minor firms should understand debt, liens, small-hauler desperation, and the difference between help and predation.
- Veyr-adjacent corporations should understand severe compliance, customs translation, and the danger of simplifying law for profit.

## Maintenance notes

- Keep location-specific cannabis and intoxicant rules in this file. Keep only broad universal rules in mechanics/law quick-reference files.
- Do not make all locations funny in the same way. Each place should have its own rhythm, stakes, and bureaucracy.
- Do not turn corporate locations into cartoon evil. The strongest corporate stories come from incentives, contracts, liability, labor pressure, shortcuts, and plausible deniability.
- Do not treat permissive cannabis law, beer culture, or festival culture as permission to ignore safety-duty impairment.
- Do not treat strict jurisdictions as automatically villainous. Strict law should have a history, internal logic, and procedural reality.
- Use soft-canon locations freely for story planning, but promote them to hard canon only after they appear in accepted story text or are explicitly locked by later canon updates.
