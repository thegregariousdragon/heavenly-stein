# Character Personality and Scene Fingerprints

Canon navigation: [Home](README.md) | [Previous: 16 Economy Law and Communications](16-economy-law-and-communications.md) | [Next: 18 Weapons Combat and Force Rules](18-weapons-combat-and-force-rules.md)


These notes define recurring personality behavior, scene instincts,
speech tendencies, private contradictions, and relationship friction.
They do not replace formal roles, authority, chronology, mechanics, or
established history defined elsewhere in the canon.

Use these fingerprints to make characters recognizable in scenes without
reducing them to gimmicks. Characters may act against type under
pressure, but the story should understand why.

## Use Rules

- Do not turn traits into mandatory tics or catchphrases.
- Scene fingerprints should guide behavior, not replace judgment.
- Characters may act against type under pressure, trauma, exhaustion, or
  growth, but the story should know why.
- Job competence remains intact unless a scene deliberately tests it.
- Humor should reveal character, not flatten character.
- Nobody should be only their job, but each person's personality should
  affect how they do their job.
- If a quick line conflicts with formal canon in another file, formal
  canon controls until a correction is explicitly added.

## First-Pass Core Cast

### Heinz Schmitt

Core contradiction: Heinz wants to build places where people are safe,
welcome, and joyful, but he is much more comfortable being useful than
being emotionally available.

Scene fingerprint: He makes absurdity look practical. He enters a
ridiculous situation, checks the load path, asks one dry question, and
somehow becomes the center of gravity.

What he notices first: escape routes, structural stress, weapon angles,
bad process, who is pretending not to be scared, whether Feli is
watching, and whether Rolf is silently judging a bracket.

What he does under stress: he gets quieter. His humor becomes flatter.
His body language gets still. He solves the physical problem first, then
tries very hard not to discuss what it cost him emotionally.

Private softness: he keeps sentimental objects because they are
"useful," even when they are obviously emotional anchors: Rolf's bong,
Feli's alpenhorn, family brewing details, old TUM memories, his custom
wristpad, and the Explorer's visible berth.

Pet irritation: people who confuse spectacle with unseriousness. He can
tolerate jokes about himself. He cannot tolerate people assuming the joke
means the engineering, labor, safety, or grief is fake.

Humor style: dry, blunt, underplayed, often literal. He does not chase
the joke; he lets the ridiculous fact sit there until everyone else
realizes it is funny.

Speech rhythm: short sentences when serious. Longer explanations appear
when discussing ships, brewing, safety, or why someone has misunderstood
a German phrase catastrophically.

Competence tell: he asks the smallest practical question in the room,
such as "Which seal?" "Who cleared the tool cartridge?" "Where did the
heat go?" "Who is missing from the count?" or "Did the destination
accept the route?"

Blind spot: he mistakes endurance for emotional maturity. He can survive
almost anything, so he sometimes assumes that surviving is the same as
processing.

Relationship friction: Feli sees when "I am fine" means "I have become
furniture with trauma." Rolf knows Heinz's practical answers sometimes
hide emotional panic. His family knows the boy under the enormous public
myth. His staff need him to learn not to solve every problem by being
impressive.

Recurring physical/action beat: he touches or checks the custom
black-red-gold wristpad when avoiding an emotional answer. It looks like
he is checking a message. Feli knows he is retreating.

Would never say: "I am the only one who can fix this." He may act like
it on a bad day, but he would hate hearing himself say it.

Useful line: "I am not avoiding the subject. I am letting it become less
stupid before I answer."

### Feli Brandt

Core contradiction: Feli can translate almost anyone's fear into usable
language, but she sometimes over-functions because silence feels like
negligence.

Scene fingerprint: she turns chaos into a sentence people can stand
inside.

What she notices first: who is being excluded from the conversation,
which question the media is really asking, when Heinz is using
engineering language to avoid an emotional truth, whether a public
statement will harm the people it claims to protect, and the person in
the room who understands but cannot safely speak.

What she does under stress: she becomes warmer and more precise. The
worse the crisis, the fewer wasted words she uses.

Private softness: she loves music as shared memory, not merely as
performance. She remembers where people stood during important songs.

Pet irritation: people who call communication "spin" when she is doing
harm reduction, clarity, consent, or grief protection.

Humor style: gentle ambush. She lets someone walk into the realization
and then smiles.

Speech rhythm: calm, warm, and emotionally exact. She can sound casual
while moving a political knife away from someone's throat.

Competence tell: she can rewrite Heinz's technically correct disaster
sentence into something the public can understand without lying.

Blind spot: she sometimes assumes she has to make everyone else's
feelings survivable before she is allowed to have her own.

Relationship friction: Heinz loves her steadiness, but she refuses to
let him hide inside usefulness. Clara protects legal meaning while Feli
protects public meaning; both are right often enough to annoy each other.
The media may ask fair questions, but Feli has no patience for grief
extraction.

Recurring physical/action beat: she sends one-word slate messages that
land harder than speeches: "Breathe." "No." "Later." "Truth." "Eat."

Would never say: "That is only optics." To Feli, optics are often how
real people find out whether they are safe.

Useful line: "I am not softening the truth. I am removing the shrapnel."

### Rolf Petersen

Core contradiction: Rolf distrusts sentiment in design but is himself
deeply sentimental about machines, friendships, and anything built
correctly.

Scene fingerprint: he insults the decorative object, then saves it by
quietly improving the mounting hardware.

What he notices first: load paths, maintenance access, fastener quality,
whether something can be cleaned, whether someone is pretending
"temporary" does not need inspection, and whether Heinz is using jokes
to avoid pain.

What he does under stress: he becomes extremely specific. His emotional
register narrows into checklists, torque values, and insults aimed at
hardware.

Private softness: he repairs things people thought were disposable, not
because he says he cares, but because waste is cowardice and because the
object mattered to someone.

Pet irritation: decorative mass attached to critical systems without
inspection access; also people who say "basically safe."

Humor style: dry, blunt, mechanically hostile.

Speech rhythm: short, clipped, often noun-heavy. He removes emotional
adjectives and replaces them with component names.

Competence tell: he identifies the boring failure before it becomes
dramatic.

Blind spot: he can underestimate social systems because they do not have
visible bolts. He is improving on this through Feli, Tariq/Teriq, Greta,
and the Guild.

Relationship friction: Heinz is his best friend, but Rolf will not let
Heinz call self-sacrifice "efficient." Tariq/Teriq's public-atmosphere
work forced Rolf to admit that mood has load behavior. Lukas and Rolf
share process respect and nitpick each other across brewing and
engineering.

Recurring physical/action beat: he physically tests things: bracket,
railing, chair, door seam, cask table, chandelier support, or emotional
metaphor if possible.

Would never say: "It will probably hold."

Useful line: "Temporary is where permanent hides until it kills
someone."

### Clara/Klara Obermeier

Core contradiction: Clara believes rules prevent disaster, but she is
drawn to people who keep proving that rules need courage,
interpretation, and occasional profanity.

Scene fingerprint: she enters with a document, realizes the situation is
worse than the document, and starts writing the appendix in her head.

What she notices first: legal precedent, cultural insult risk, who will
be blamed after the room calms down, whether Heinz has accidentally
invented governance again, and whether Tariq/Teriq is joking, working,
or both.

What she does under stress: she becomes formal. The more alarmed she is,
the more complete her sentences get.

Private softness: she likes festivals when she is not responsible for
them. She notices old songs, children's decorations, handmade signs, and
tiny traditions that will become legal headaches only if someone ruins
them.

Pet irritation: people who say "it's just symbolic." Symbols are how
lawsuits, riots, treaties, and family feuds dress for dinner.

Humor style: exasperated precision. She makes the correction funnier by
making it legally exact.

Speech rhythm: careful, structured, escalating from polite to "I am not
writing this report" depending on precedent danger.

Competence tell: she can tell the difference between a harmless cultural
misunderstanding and a future tribunal exhibit.

Blind spot: she sometimes treats uncertainty as a paperwork failure
rather than a normal human condition.

Relationship friction: Heinz creates precedents by existing near
problems. Tariq/Teriq's phrasing still threatens her blood pressure, even
though she respects him. Feli's public language and Clara's legal
language often need the same truth in different containers.

Recurring physical/action beat: she opens her mouth to object, pauses
because the absurd thing is actually useful, then says, "Fine. But we
are naming the category before anyone touches it."

Would never say: "This probably does not need documentation."

Useful line: "Civilization is mostly people writing down what went wrong
before someone calls it tradition."

### Tariq/Teriq Neumann

Core contradiction: Tariq makes serious work look effortless, then gets
annoyed when people believe the effortlessness.

Scene fingerprint: he reads a room like a diagnostic panel nobody else
admits exists.

What he notices first: crowd rhythm, sound changes, children moving
against flow, silence where there should be chatter, a joke that lands
wrong, music that is fighting the room, and the person who is lost but
too proud to ask.

What he does under stress: he gets less flashy. His voice drops. The
stickers stop. He starts giving very practical instructions that sound
like metaphors until they work.

Private softness: he is protective of people who feel out of place in
public spaces: kids, elders, visitors with bad translation, exhausted
crews, neurodivergent guests, embarrassed pilots, and lonely residents.

Pet irritation: being treated as decorative by people who depend on his
work without noticing it.

Humor style: musical, slang-heavy, poetic, sudden, and sticker-adjacent.
He can turn a safety principle into something people repeat.

Speech rhythm: loose and rhythmic until the situation is serious. Then
short, direct, and surprisingly calm.

Competence tell: he notices quiet failures before sensors do: crowd
hesitation, bad acoustics, mood shift, family-flow collapse, sound
masking an alarm, or a child vanishing into visual noise.

Blind spot: he sometimes uses style as armor. If he makes the room laugh
first, he does not have to admit the room hurt him.

Relationship friction: Clara/Klara needs terms defined; Tariq/Teriq
needs reality felt. Rolf trusts load paths; Tariq/Teriq trusts mood
paths. Janelle respects him because he sees pre-incident behavior.

Recurring physical/action beat: he adjusts the room before explaining
the problem: lowers one music channel, changes light rhythm, sends a
family-wayfinding ping, moves a sign, then says, "Now we can talk."

Would never say: "Vibes are just vibes."

Useful line: "A crowd tells you the truth before it knows the words."

### Matthias Schmitt

Core contradiction: Matthias is emotionally reserved, but he has built
his entire life around making things that bring people together.

Scene fingerprint: he tastes, waits, says almost nothing, and everyone
in the room either relaxes or panics.

What he notices first: cleanliness, temperature, timing, yeast behavior,
whether someone rushed, whether Heinz is pretending not to need
approval, and whether Lukas is managing like Matthias or like himself.

What he does under stress: he slows down. He asks process questions. He
refuses panic because panic spoils the batch and the family.

Private softness: he remembers people's favorite beers, not as trivia
but as affection.

Pet irritation: shortcut pride. He can forgive a mistake faster than
someone being proud of a bad shortcut.

Humor style: minimalist. Often two words. Sometimes one eyebrow.

Speech rhythm: sparse, grounded, with long pauses that make younger
people confess.

Competence tell: he can identify a process failure from taste, smell,
temperature, or the way a worker avoids eye contact near a tank.

Blind spot: he can make praise too rare, assuming people know he
respects them because he keeps showing up.

Relationship friction: with Heinz, pride, concern, and refusal to
flatter; with Lukas, the challenge of letting Lukas own station brewing
without becoming Matthias's echo; with Karin, process questions meet
systems questions.

Recurring physical/action beat: he wipes a surface before touching it,
even when it is already clean.

Would never say: "Close enough for beer."

Useful line: "Beer forgives patience. It does not forgive arrogance."

### Karin Schmitt

Core contradiction: Karin believes curiosity saves people, but she has
raised children clever enough to make curiosity dangerous.

Scene fingerprint: she turns a crisis into a lesson without making the
people inside it feel small.

What she notices first: the missing question, the child watching from
the edge, the assumption everyone forgot to test, whether a safety rule
can be taught before it becomes trauma, and whether Heinz is using size
and competence to avoid asking for help.

What she does under stress: she asks questions that reveal the system.
Not soft questions; useful ones.

Private softness: she keeps student notes, drawings, and awkward
thank-you messages much longer than she admits.

Pet irritation: adults who punish children for asking the question the
adults should have asked first.

Humor style: teacher-dry, precise, occasionally devastating.

Speech rhythm: clear, patient, structured. When angry, she becomes even
more educational, which is terrifying.

Competence tell: she can explain a terrifying system in a way a child
understands without lying about the danger.

Blind spot: she sometimes believes a better explanation can fix people
who are choosing bad faith.

Relationship friction: Heinz is still the frightened boy under the
public captain; Matthias's process and Karin's pedagogy overlap more
than either admits; Annika's clarity after the assault comes with
maternal fear.

Recurring physical/action beat: she asks, "What do we know?" before
anyone is allowed to argue about what it means.

Would never say: "Because I said so," unless exhausted, scared, and
immediately annoyed with herself.

Useful line: "A rule no one understands is only a superstition with
better typography."

### Lukas Schmitt

Core contradiction: Lukas wants to prove he is not merely Matthias's
apprentice or Heinz's brother, but he is proudest when the family
tradition works through his own hands.

Scene fingerprint: he corrects the tapline before correcting the person.

What he notices first: flow rate, glass handling, pressure, server
rhythm, whether the beer is being respected, whether a festival schedule
is about to abuse workers, and whether someone calls him "Heinz's little
brother" before his title.

What he does under stress: he becomes bossy in practical ways. He
assigns tasks, fixes lines, and gets sharper with anyone wasting motion.

Private softness: he likes training apprentices, especially the nervous
ones trying too hard.

Pet irritation: being treated as the family backup brewer; also people
who pour badly while talking confidently.

Humor style: dry, younger-brother cutting, less patient than Matthias,
more openly annoyed.

Speech rhythm: crisp, direct, service-floor practical. He talks like
someone watching six things at once.

Competence tell: he fixes the service problem without stopping the line.

Blind spot: he sometimes confuses high standards with unclear standards,
especially during festival pressure.

Relationship friction: Matthias's shadow and pride; Heinz's myth bends
every room; the Guild creates real tension over scheduling,
apprenticeships, surge labor, and standards, but Lukas can grow from it.

Recurring physical/action beat: he turns a glass slightly, checks the
foam line, and decides whether the universe is behaving.

Would never say: "It's just beer."

Useful line: "If the line is wrong, the room feels it before the guest
names it."

### Annika Schmitt

Core contradiction: Annika refuses to be treated as fragile, but she is
still learning how to live with the fact that she was targeted because
people love her.

Scene fingerprint: she says the thing everyone is protecting themselves
from hearing.

What she notices first: adult euphemisms, protective lies, who is using
her as an argument, when anger is becoming permission, when
institutional language hides a human cost, and when Heinz, Lukas, or
Matthias are about to become stupid in a masculine way.

What she does under stress: she gets very clear. Not calm exactly;
clear. Her fear sharpens into moral sentences.

Private softness: she wants normal student problems again and resents
that part of her life has become evidence.

Pet irritation: being praised for bravery when people mean obedience.

Humor style: sharp, young-adult, impatient with melodrama unless she is
the one using it.

Speech rhythm: quick, direct, occasionally cutting. She does not wait
for older people to finish being wrong.

Competence tell: she identifies manipulation before the men in her
family finish feeling protective.

Blind spot: she may overcorrect against being protected and reject care
she actually needs.

Relationship friction: Heinz, Lukas, and Matthias love her, but she
refuses to be turned into a revenge permit. Karin understands too much.
Feli can treat her neither as child nor symbol.

Recurring physical/action beat: she folds her arms not to seem closed
off, but to keep herself from shaking.

Would never say: "Do whatever you need to do for me."

Useful line: "Do not make my pain convenient for your courage."

### Tante Hedi

Core contradiction: Tante Hedi respects authority only when it behaves
like responsibility.

Scene fingerprint: she appears where everyone has agreed she should not
be, already knowing why they are wrong.

What she notices first: who has not eaten, who is grieving badly, who is
hiding behind title, whether the young people are being foolish or
merely young, and whether Heinz is pretending to be too busy for the
human part.

What she does under stress: she becomes practical. Food, chair, blanket,
name, song, witness, scolding: in that order as needed.

Private softness: she remembers the dead as people with habits, not as
causes.

Pet irritation: being "managed." Anyone who tries to route her through
official channels has already lost.

Humor style: blunt affectionate demolition.

Speech rhythm: short, certain, often final. She does not debate when she
can declare.

Competence tell: she knows exactly when ceremony is needed and when
ceremony is being used to avoid grief.

Blind spot: she can assume that because she is morally right, procedure
should move faster than it safely can.

Relationship friction: she sees through Heinz's captain/founder myth and
addresses the nephew; Clara/Klara must balance procedure against Hedi's
moral immediacy; Feli knows when not to translate Hedi too gently.

Recurring physical/action beat: she sits down in the chair no one
offered her, thereby establishing that the meeting has changed.

Would never say: "I should not get involved."

Useful line: "If you wanted me quiet, you should not have made this
important."

## Second-Pass Station Operating Cast

### Amara Reid

Core contradiction: Amara is welcoming by temperament and absolute by
profession. She likes people; she just does not trust people with
vectors.

Scene fingerprint: she makes terrifying traffic authority sound like
customer service until someone ignores her.

What she notices first: approach geometry, who is moving too confidently,
fault declarations that sound rehearsed, which pilot is tired, which
ship is behaving differently than its pilot claims, and whether joy is
about to become a traffic hazard.

What she does under stress: her voice gets calmer. Her sentences get
shorter. She stops explaining and starts assigning vectors.

Private softness: she remembers ship names and regular crews. She
notices when a freighter has a new junior pilot, when a family hauler
sounds exhausted, or when Relay Haven traffic goes quiet for too long.

Pet irritation: pilots who think charm, wealth, celebrity, rank, or
emergency theater changes physics.

Humor style: warm, dry, traffic-control deadpan.

Speech rhythm: clear, measured, comm-friendly. She speaks in language
that can be repeated during a crisis.

Competence tell: she gives the instruction that prevents the accident
nobody else has noticed yet.

Blind spot: she can be so focused on the operational sky that she
underestimates the emotional aftermath of ordering people to wait.

Relationship friction: Heinz's founder status does not modify approach
authority; Janelle controls moving people while Amara controls moving
ships; Feli helps explain Amara's hard decisions to angry civilians.

Recurring physical/action beat: she taps two fingers beside a display
before issuing a hard instruction, as if placing the ship physically in
space.

Would never say: "Make your own approach."

Useful line: "You may be important after you stop moving."

### Janelle Okonkwo

Core contradiction: Janelle believes force must be ready, but the best
security outcome is when nobody notices it was used.

Scene fingerprint: she lowers the temperature of a room without raising
her voice.

What she notices first: hands, exits, bad confidence, people watching
security instead of the event, who is afraid but trying not to look like
a witness, and the person using noise as cover.

What she does under stress: she becomes quieter and more specific. She
gives instructions people obey before they realize they have obeyed.

Private softness: she is protective of service workers because she knows
they see trouble first and are often believed last.

Pet irritation: unlogged authority. She hates sloppy surveillance,
heroic improvisation without records, and people who confuse secrecy
with security.

Humor style: dry, nearly flat, often delayed.

Speech rhythm: low, precise, controlled. She rarely repeats herself;
when she does, the second version is worse for you.

Competence tell: she positions people before the threat fully reveals
itself.

Blind spot: she can treat her own need for rest as a security risk
someone else can file later.

Relationship friction: she respects Heinz's crisis competence but does
not want him physically solving every threat. She increasingly trusts
Tariq/Teriq's crowd instincts. She will act before Clara/Klara has the
ideal wording when life safety requires it.

Recurring physical/action beat: she asks a question while already moving
one step to change the room's geometry.

Would never say: "I had a feeling, so I skipped the log."

Useful line: "If I have to raise my voice, several quieter options have
already failed."

### Dr. Mireya Santos

Core contradiction: Dr. Santos is compassionate enough to fight for
every patient and practical enough to be furious when preventable
stupidity creates patients.

Scene fingerprint: she is warm until someone tries to negotiate with
biology.

What she notices first: breathing, skin color, balance, denial, who is
injured but still apologizing, which bystander is about to faint, and
whether the room is helping or becoming another patient.

What she does under stress: she triages. Her kindness becomes
directional: you sit, you breathe, you move, you stop talking.

Private softness: she checks on staff after crises even when they were
not "patients." She knows adrenaline hides injuries and shame hides
worse ones.

Pet irritation: people who refuse aftercare because they are "needed;"
also pilots who think sobering protocols are moral judgments.

Humor style: warm gallows humor, never at the expense of the vulnerable.

Speech rhythm: fast when triaging, slower when explaining. Her voice
makes panic feel inefficient.

Competence tell: she identifies the second emergency inside the first
one.

Blind spot: she can become controlling when frightened, because bodies
do not care about personal dignity during preventable collapse.

Relationship friction: Heinz is a terrible patient because he respects
medical authority in theory; Janelle and Dr. Santos share crisis
discipline but may disagree over when Security needs to leave Medical
alone; Karin and Dr. Santos collaborate strongly on safety education.

Recurring physical/action beat: she points with two fingers, not rudely
but decisively, assigning people to useful positions.

Would never say: "Walk it off."

Useful line: "You can be heroic after your blood pressure has rejoined
civilization."

### Nkiru "Nikki" Adebayo

Core contradiction: Nikki seems cheerful around danger because they are
only cheerful when danger is finally being treated honestly.

Scene fingerprint: they make catastrophe less frightening by labeling it
correctly.

What they notice first: containment path, decon failure, bad packaging,
the person trying to look casual near a hazard, and whether the danger
is moving, humming, leaking, heating, learning, or being lied about.

What they do under stress: they become briskly cheerful. The worse the
hazard, the more politely they insist everyone obey boring procedure.

Private softness: they love harmlessly weird objects: cracked mugs, ugly
warning stickers, children's "do not lick artifacts" drawings, and old
salvage tags.

Pet irritation: mystical language used to avoid safety procedure. They
do not mind wonder. They mind wonder with poor containment.

Humor style: bright, unsettling, hazard-label comedy.

Speech rhythm: friendly, quick, practical, with sudden technical
exactness.

Competence tell: they choose the low-tech method when everyone else
wants the impressive tool.

Blind spot: they can underestimate how scary their cheerfulness sounds
to people who do not live dirty-side.

Relationship friction: Rowan wants to know; Nikki wants to know safely.
Mara Venn's compliance and Nikki's containment mostly align, but Nikki
gets impatient when paperwork lags behind hazard reality. Rolf and Nikki
share respect for boring physical safeguards.

Recurring physical/action beat: they place a label before finishing the
explanation.

Would never say: "It is probably harmless."

Useful line: "The artifact is allowed to be mysterious after it is no
longer near the air system."

### Priya Natarajan

Core contradiction: Priya believes home should feel ordinary, but she
knows ordinary life on a frontier station must be deliberately
engineered.

Scene fingerprint: she turns "minor resident complaint" into a map of
whether the station is becoming livable.

What she notices first: children's routes, noise bleed, laundry
bottlenecks, tired parents, which residents feel like guests in their
own home, and whether public excitement is invading private life.

What she does under stress: she organizes care before debate. She finds
the families, the quiet room, the lift priority, the missing medication,
and the child who thinks the emergency is their fault.

Private softness: she keeps a mental list of children's favorite safe
places on station.

Pet irritation: people who treat residential life as secondary to "real
operations." A station people live on is always operating.

Humor style: practical, domestic, gently cutting.

Speech rhythm: warm but firm. She sounds like someone who has already
solved three problems while the meeting was defining one.

Competence tell: she identifies the human infrastructure failure behind
the complaint.

Blind spot: she sometimes takes too much responsibility for making
everyone feel at home.

Relationship friction: she respects Amara and Janelle's emergency
authority but insists residents are not background scenery. Tariq/Teriq
shares her concern for public legibility, family wayfinding, and mood.
She translates Board policy into hallway consequences.

Recurring physical/action beat: she checks whether a child can
physically reach, read, or understand the thing adults are discussing.

Would never say: "They'll get used to it."

Useful line: "If families can only live here when nothing goes wrong,
then families cannot live here."

### Elior Ben-Ami

Core contradiction: Elior teaches children how to be safe without
teaching them the universe is only danger.

Scene fingerprint: he turns station weirdness into curriculum before the
children can turn it into panic.

What he notices first: which child is pretending to understand, which
safety rule has become a chant without meaning, which adult is scaring
children with vague seriousness, and whether a lesson needs wonder,
humor, or a locked door.

What he does under stress: he makes instructions concrete. He gives
children roles that are safe, simple, and dignified.

Private softness: he keeps a folder of children's questions that were
too good to answer quickly.

Pet irritation: adults who lie to children because they confuse honesty
with cruelty.

Humor style: gentle, classroom-wry, surprisingly mischievous when it
helps learning.

Speech rhythm: clear, inclusive, question-based. He explains without
condescending.

Competence tell: children follow him during emergencies because he has
practiced trust before needing it.

Blind spot: he can over-explain to adults who are not arguing in good
faith.

Relationship friction: he respects Karin, but remote curriculum ideals
and on-site chaos may differ. Priya and Elior collaborate closely but
may disagree over whether a problem is primarily educational or
residential. Nikki and Rowan help him preserve wonder around artifacts
without inviting licking, touching, smuggling, or myth-making.

Recurring physical/action beat: he kneels or lowers himself to a child's
sightline before giving important instructions.

Would never say: "Because it is too complicated for you."

Useful line: "A child who understands the rule is safer than a child who
merely fears the alarm."

### Keisha Bell

Core contradiction: Keisha is deeply hospitable, but she has no patience
for people who mistake care work for servitude.

Scene fingerprint: she sees the crew behind the cargo manifest.

What she notices first: exhaustion, bad shoes, shower access, crew
rotation strain, whether a pilot is too tired to be proud safely, and
which service worker is about to absorb someone else's crisis.

What she does under stress: she triages practical comfort: sleep,
shower, food, clean clothes, message home, sober-up, then paperwork.

Private softness: she remembers route crews by their small rituals:
coffee order, lucky jacket, kid's birthday call, preferred rest pod.

Pet irritation: luxury visitors blocking services working crews need;
also anyone who says "just crew."

Humor style: frontier-service bluntness, affectionate when earned.

Speech rhythm: direct, grounded, with the cadence of someone who has
moved tired people through bad days before.

Competence tell: she prevents a crisis by giving someone a shower, a
sandwich, and permission to stop pretending.

Blind spot: she may distrust polished administrative fixes until she
sees them work at 03:00 during a laundry failure.

Relationship friction: Sanaa and Keisha respect each other but clash
over scarce supplies. Janelle and Keisha cooperate on drunk/exhausted
pilot handling. Mateo's front-of-house hospitality and Keisha's
working-crew support can clash during festivals.

Recurring physical/action beat: she hands someone the exact practical
item they need before asking what happened.

Would never say: "Crew comfort is not mission-critical."

Useful line: "A rested pilot is cheaper than a memorial plaque."

### Lian Zhou

Core contradiction: Lian can make a hostile memo sound gracious, but
they are very aware that politeness can hide knives.

Scene fingerprint: they translate conflict into a form no one can
publicly reject without revealing themselves.
