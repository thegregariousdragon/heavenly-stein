# Der Himmelskrug Station

Status: station interior layout revised for the public/residential redesign pass. This file supersedes older references that placed the public atrium through Services, placed the Crown press room in the lid, placed senior staff housing in the Crown, or treated the Cultural Level as the primary exhibit level.

## Name and Concept

- Name: Der Himmelskrug, German for “The Heavenly Stein.”
- Identity: a tall beer-stein-shaped orbital brewery/free-port/station and multicultural hub in stein form.
- It is a collaboration between Heinz, Rolf, Feli, New Bavaria, Zion Drift, the Schmitt family brewing tradition, and other supporting colonies.
- It is funny from a distance and serious up close: a brewery, port, public hospitality space, clean-side/restricted logistics facility, sector support hub, residential town, civic-learning center, museum, and route-safety institution.
- Architectural principle: public joy sits on top of serious routing. Every warm public space must have hidden structure for security, Medical, Flight Control, decontamination, labor, logistics, and emergency response.

## Exterior

- Der Himmelskrug keeps the iconic tall, heavy-lidded beer-stein silhouette. The public levels, Residential, Services, Starship Garage, and Restricted Works make it a very tall stein, but the first read from approach should still be “impossible orbital beer stein.”
- The exterior has two readable faces: the public stein face and the working service side.
- The public stein face carries the warm civic image: pale ceramic-like armored panels, dark brushed-metal bands, restrained Bavarian blue/white motifs, warm amber public lighting, green botanical strips, the glowing Observation band, and the giant handle.
- The working service side carries the infrastructure: articulated solar truss, radiator and utility arcs, exterior maintenance rails, drone ports, service panels, Starship Garage interfaces, and Restricted Works interfaces.
- Crown/lid: four-quadrant civic cap containing Command, Administration/Civic, Learning Center, and Museum. It looks like a stein lid from afar; command, civic, education, and museum infrastructure become clear up close.
- Observation band: glowing 360-degree ring just below the crown. It is the station’s main public exterior light band and should not be crowded by solar arrays or heavy utility hardware.
- Handle: one giant handle, but internally separated. The upper handle connection serves public passenger arrival/departure into Arrival Control. The lower handle connection serves the Starship Garage clean-side portal only. A separate utility/maintenance chase may run within the handle structure. The handle is not a casual shortcut between public levels and ship operations.
- Main solar collection: the primary articulated solar-array truss is mounted on the working service side near the Lichtbrunn-facing utility arc, not on the public glass band and not on the main passenger handle. It may track, fold, isolate segments, and support maintenance crawlers without blocking public approach views.
- Radiators and heavy utility systems: concentrate on non-public service arcs, lower industrial body bands, and the working side. They should not dominate the public stein silhouette.
- Starship Garage exterior: clean-side docking collars, berth doors, cargo locks, tow points, utility hookups, approach lights, and berth-status signals.
- Restricted Works exterior: lower body/base restricted-side doors, decon locks, quarantine berths, shielded industrial doors, restricted ordnance/rearming interfaces, remote drone ports, and hazard-lit access points. These are separate from the handle and do not invite public approach.
- Lighting modes: normal warm/docking/garden/hazard lights; festival handle bubble light chase and glowing Observation band; emergency hard blue-white traffic lights, red restricted-side hazard markers, dimmed public glow, and possible armor shutters.

## Crown / Lid: Fixed Four-Quadrant Civic Cap

The Crown is fixed as a four-quadrant civic cap. The quadrants share civic importance, not shared access.

### Command Quadrant

- Contains Flight Control, route control, secure communications, emergency operations, crisis coordination, command support, and strictly controlled technical spaces.
- Access is hard-restricted to authorized command, Flight Control, emergency, security, and technical personnel.
- The Command Quadrant is not a press area, public shortcut, school corridor, consular waiting room, museum back hall, or senior-housing annex.
- It has dedicated secure lifts/cores and controlled connections to Medical, Services, Starship Garage operations, and emergency response paths.

### Administration / Civic Quadrant

- Contains station administration, consular services, permits, documentation, governance support, labor/governance rooms, mediation, civic records, appointment rooms, and controlled public-facing counters.
- Access is screened or appointment-based. Once past the front office, consular offices, station administration, records, and other civic functions have their own internal controls.
- This quadrant is public-facing but not public-wandering space.

### Learning Center Quadrant

- Contains the Himmelskrug Learning Center and associated services for child and adult education.
- Functions include child education, adult education, apprenticeships, route-safety classes, station orientation, language/cultural education, remote-learning support, school commons, counselor/school-nurse support, and protected school-group handling.
- It has a protected resident/school route from Residential and a separate scheduled public/class route. Children do not need to cross public nightlife, consular waiting rooms, or Command to reach school.

### Museum Quadrant

- Contains station history, colony exhibits, brewing history, route-safety/process exhibits, rotating galleries, educational displays, and artifact exhibits only if cleared through dirty-side/research/legal procedure.
- The Museum is controlled and curated; it is not the Entertainment Level and not a shopping concourse.
- It may connect to the Learning Center for supervised education, but not to Command or unrestricted Residential corridors.

### Press Placement

- The dedicated press suite is no longer in the Crown. It belongs on Hospitality Level 1 as a controlled public-facing press/civic information suite with remote feeds from Crown Command/Admin.
- Press access never grants physical access to Flight Control, secure comms, consular back offices, Residential, Medical, or restricted service routes.

## Public Entry and Arrival Control Complex

- Upper handle public docking/arrival leads into the station’s Arrival Control Complex, not directly into the public atrium, taproom, hotel, school, museum, Residential, brewery production, or Command.
- The Arrival Control Complex is one integrated arrival-security system with multiple intake lanes, not repeated checkpoints. Physical docking passengers and authorized personnel-transport arrivals enter the same clearance process through different controlled entry points.
- Public-facing arrival sequence: docking or authorized personnel transport -> secured arrival zone -> shared identity/security/decontamination/customs/weapons/clean-dirty declaration process -> public release gate -> controlled entrance corridor -> Hospitality Level 1 atrium edge.
- Arrivals and departures use paired but separate entrance and exit corridors. Incoming cleared visitors do not cross departing passengers, weapons-return stations, exit customs, or re-screening queues.
- The public arrival threshold contains orientation, information, guest services, translation help, and the first controlled view into Hospitality Level 1 and the public atrium.
- Friendly sign canon: “Clear security. Wash off space. Ask before translating German. Drink later.”
- Operating principle: “One arrival. One clearance. No appearing behind the beer.”

### Transit Reception Center / Controlled Wormhole Reception

- Der Himmelskrug includes a controlled personnel-transport reception center just off the secured arrival system. Formal title: Transit Reception Center. Staff slang varies depending on how annoying the most recent arrival was.
- The Transit Reception Center handles authorized personnel wormhole/teleport arrivals so no one appears directly in the taproom, public atrium, hotel, Residential corridors, Command, school, museum, brewery production, or other public/staff-only areas.
- It is a receiving, screening, and routing lane within the Arrival Control Complex, not a universal free-teleport hub and not a second security checkpoint.
- The center does not override established transport mechanics. Outbound movement must still follow authorized ship-console, docking-bay, landing-pad, station-pad, or remote-transport rules as applicable.
- Standard process: arrivals appear on marked reception pads, are logged by arrival-control/security systems, pass into the shared identity/security/decontamination/customs/weapons/clean-dirty declaration process, and then proceed through the public release gate if cleared.
- Already pre-cleared VIPs still receive identity confirmation and a safety scan, then may be routed through secure VIP/consular corridors instead of public foot traffic.
- Station staff use fast staff lanes when authorized, but arrivals are still logged.
- Medical emergencies route directly from the secured arrival system to Medical without crossing public areas.
- Dirty, hazardous, undeclared, intoxicated, undocumented, hostile, armed, legally complicated, or artifact-adjacent arrivals are diverted before public release to Security, Medical, consular review, weapons storage, dirty-side containment, or other appropriate handling.
- Public-facing rule: “Arrive here. Declare honestly. Exit happier.”
- Security-facing rule: “Nobody appears behind the beer.”

## Public Atrium / Platz

The public platz is a grand vertical atrium with ring/balcony levels around an open central void. It is now shorter and more public-focused than older layouts. Top to bottom:

1. Observation Level
2. Entertainment Level
3. Hospitality Level 1
4. Hospitality Level 2
5. Retail Level

- The public atrium ends at Retail. Services, Residential, Starship Garage, and Restricted Works are not part of the public atrium.
- Retail contains the public atrium terminus: a catch-basin, water/light, or foam-and-starlight feature tied to the chandelier above. Older references to the public fountain basin on Services are superseded.
- Observation Level ceiling supports the massive chandelier descending into the atrium, like frozen bubbles/starlight/foam. The chandelier is a rated suspended system, not reachable decoration.
- Bubble-themed elevator cars are iconic public scenic elevators. They serve the public atrium levels only: Observation, Entertainment, Hospitality 1, Hospitality 2, and Retail. They do not normally stop at Crown, Residential, Services, Starship Garage, Restricted Works, Medical, school, museum, brewery production, or Command.
- The chandelier occupies the central visual zone. Bubble elevators travel around the atrium perimeter/inner wall rather than through the chandelier.
- Bubble elevators are not the emergency backbone. Hidden hard-service cores, protected stairs, Medical lifts, staff/service lifts, resident lifts, and command/security cores provide real emergency and operational routing.
- Acoustic rule: the platz’s open vertical design gives dramatic sightlines, not casual long-distance conversation. Normal crowd noise, water/light feature sound, elevator movement, taproom spillover, public music, and traffic announcements make shouted dialogue across multiple levels unreliable. Cross-level exchanges should normally use staff comms, PA, wrist pads, nearby balcony/elevator proximity, visible gestures, or intentional amplification.

## Interior Surface, Lighting, Air, and Sound Rules

Use these rules when writing or designing any interior scene on Der Himmelskrug. They are sensory canon, not decorative suggestions.

### Global sensory rules

- Bavarian materials are influence, not costume. White plaster, dark wood, brass, carved-beam language, restrained blue/white motifs, cask wood, hop/plant life, polished metal, and warm lights appear most strongly in public hospitality spaces, not everywhere equally.
- Public warmth never overrides routing. A cozy room still has security, Medical, Flight Control, decon, labor, logistics, and emergency structure behind it.
- Lighting changes by authority owner. Public hospitality is warm. Command, Medical, Starship Garage, and Restricted Works are task-led. Residential is circadian and domestic. Museum and Learning use controlled civic/educational lighting.
- Odor bleed is a failure unless the scene is specifically about a malfunction. Brewery, food, cannabis, salon products, Medical disinfectant, garage air, decon, and Restricted Works air must be locally controlled.
- Acoustic separation is architecture. Entertainment, hotel rooms, Residential, Medical, Crown workrooms, and the public atrium cannot rely on people simply being quiet.
- Use two or three sensory details per scene beat. Do not stop every scene for a station tour unless someone is arriving, lost, giving a tour, or noticing a system under stress.

### Crown sensory palette

- Surfaces: pale civic panels, dark metal, quiet wood accents, frosted glass, acoustic wall fabric in meeting rooms, and clean stone-like or ceramic composite floors.
- Lighting: calm white-gold civic lighting. Command uses cooler task lighting. Museum uses controlled gallery lighting. Learning Center uses warmer daylight-balanced lighting.
- Air and smell: neutral, filtered, faint paper, clean electronics, and wood. No food, alcohol, cannabis, brewery, garage, or decon smell.
- Sound: hushed and compartmented. Command has low comms and console tones; Learning has contained classroom sound; Museum is quiet and paced.

### Observation sensory palette

- Surfaces: glass, polished railings, pale floor, brass or dark-metal guard details, and acoustic ceiling treatment hidden in the chandelier canopy.
- Lighting: balanced starfield/exterior light with soft amber event lighting. The chandelier gives the signature foam-and-starlight glow.
- Air and smell: clean, cool, lightly filtered. No food-court, brewery, cannabis, garage, or decon odor.
- Sound: spacious but controlled. Soft crowd murmur in ordinary use; event sound only when programmed.

### Entertainment sensory palette

- Surfaces: darker woods, brass trim, sound-absorbing panels, durable floors, acoustic vestibules, and flexible venue interiors.
- Lighting: marquee warmth, stage lighting, controlled color, and venue-specific cues. Public promenade lighting remains readable and safe.
- Air and smell: mostly neutral with faint drinks, clothing, and approved stage haze where permitted. No cannabis odor bleed from the Performer Staging Suite/Künstlerzimmer.
- Sound: intentionally zoned. Festhalle is loudest; Music/Listening is best isolated; Cabaret/Comedy is contained; Games/Social is lively but buffered.

### Hospitality Level 1 sensory palette

- Surfaces: iconic beer-hall blend of white plaster, dark wood, polished brass, cask wood, tile or durable stone-like flooring, and warm seating.
- Lighting: warm amber, arrival-friendly, and wayfinding-clear. The taproom glows across the atrium from Arrivals.
- Air and smell: beer, bread or pretzel, coffee, warm food, and polished wood. No decon smell at public release and no garage odor.
- Sound: lively but intelligible. Arrival corridors are calmer and controlled; the taproom is warm and loud but not a safety hazard.

### Hospitality Level 2 sensory palette

- Surfaces: food-hall durability, hotel-grade quiet corridors, personal-services finishes, acoustic buffer walls, and brewery-grade sealed floors behind the largest bar.
- Lighting: food and bar areas are warm and active; hotel areas are softer and lower; personal services use clean flattering light; brewery work zones are brighter and task-oriented.
- Air and smell: food hall aromas, brewery malt/yeast near the bar, and salon/barber products in the Personal Services District. Hotel corridors remain neutral and quiet.
- Sound: public ring lively; hotel ring buffered; brewery machinery and bars must not bleed into guest rooms.

### Retail sensory palette

- Surfaces: market-stall wood, polished metal, practical display cases, durable floors, and secure cases in Tools/Brewing and The Green Room/Grünraum.
- Lighting: bright enough for shopping, warm enough to feel like a market, clearer at tools/parts counters, softer at Music/Gifts.
- Air and smell: packaged goods, paper, textiles, and light coffee/tea where allowed. No corridor cannabis odor from The Green Room.
- Sound: browsing, low music, shop doors, bubble-elevator movement, and the atrium catch-basin/water-light feature. Retail is not a nightclub and not a garage.

### Residential sensory palette

- Surfaces: quieter domestic finishes, resilient floors, warm wood accents, soft acoustic panels, garden materials, and washable family-safe surfaces.
- Lighting: circadian-friendly, softer at night, brighter in the Wohnhof during day cycle, and never public-spectacle lighting.
- Air and smell: clean domestic air, plants, laundry, and cooking only in residential kitchens or common rooms. No bar, cannabis, garage, or decon bleed.
- Sound: child-safe and shift-worker respectful. Acoustic separation matters more than visual drama.

### Services sensory palette

- Surfaces: durable washable floors, sealed wall panels, privacy glass, medical-grade surfaces, workwear lockers, and practical seating.
- Lighting: clean and steady. Medical is brighter and clinical; Crew Recovery is softer; the Starship Garage transition is more industrial.
- Air and smell: clean laundry, Medical disinfectant in Medical areas, and gear/metal only near garage transition. No public food/bar aroma.
- Sound: low announcements, status boards, showers/laundry, tired crew, and Medical privacy. No tourist noise.

### Starship Garage sensory palette

- Surfaces: industrial floor plating, docking seals, dark metal, rubberized safety edges, numbered berth panels, utility hookups, and inspection-friendly panels.
- Lighting: bright task lighting, berth-status lights, docking signals, emergency strips, and no decorative public glow except controlled pride-berth feed angles.
- Air and smell: filtered machine air, metal, and coolant/ozone hints within safety limits. No food, hotel, cannabis, or brewery smell.
- Sound: clamps, collars, utility hum, service carts, low comms, and hull ticks. No public music.

### Restricted Works sensory palette

- Surfaces: sealed industrial panels, blast-rated doors, washable/decon surfaces, hazard striping, remote-handling interfaces, and heavy engineering surfaces.
- Lighting: functional, segmented, status-colored, red/hazard markers where appropriate, and clear decon lighting.
- Air and smell: ideally neutral because filtration is working. Any odor means containment, decon, or ventilation has become part of the scene.
- Sound: pressure doors, drones, ventilation, tools, alerts, and remote handling. Warmth comes from staff professionalism, not decor.

## Non-Public Stack

### Residential Level

- Located below Retail and above Services.
- Residential is for staff, families, long-term residents, senior/critical staff, rotational workers, visiting workers, and long-stay crews. It is not short-term tourist hotel lodging.
- Organized around a quieter internal residential atrium/commons: the Wohnhof. The Wohnhof is a protected domestic commons, not a tourist plaza.
- Residential quadrants:
  - Family Quadrant: family apartments, child-safe corridors, playrooms, domestic gardens, school-route access.
  - Senior / Critical Staff Quadrant: modest senior staff apartments, duty-rest suites, secure call rooms, crisis sleep rooms, quick-response lift to Command, not elite penthouse housing.
  - Rotational Crew / Visiting Worker Quadrant: dorms, compact rooms, shared kitchens, lockers, shift-ready changing, labor/union information.
  - General Staff / Long-Term Housing Quadrant: studios, shared lounges, communal kitchens, quiet rooms, ordinary domestic life.
- Senior staff housing is no longer in the Crown except for small duty-rest/call rooms that directly support emergency operations.
- The Himmelskrug Learning Center is no longer inside Residential. Residential has a protected resident/school route to the Crown Learning Center Quadrant.

### Services Level

- Services is a controlled staff/pilot/crew/operational support deck, not a general public atrium level and not a public convenience floor.
- Services sits between Residential above and the Starship Garage below. Its purpose is to keep station staff, residents, pilots, crews, contractors, and authorized workers treated, rested, documented, routed, and cleared enough for the public station above to function.
- Access is limited to station staff, residents, authorized pilots and starship crews, visiting workers/contractors, Medical patients/escorts, Security, garage staff, maintenance, and operational users with a specific purpose. Casual tourists, hotel guests, shoppers, press, school groups, and entertainment crowds do not wander Services.
- Central hub: Services Control. It handles credential checks, triage/routing, crew/pilot check-in, Medical intake routing, Starship Garage transition, staff assistance, status boards, and Security presence.
- Medical and Pharmacy District: intake, triage, treatment rooms, occupational health, fatigue/intoxication evaluation, pharmacy, medication storage, clean-side isolation rooms, and controlled dirty-side Medical interface. Medical uses hard-service/emergency cores, not bubble elevators, unless the incident is already in public space.
- Crew Recovery District: showers, laundry, workwear cleaning, rest pods, fatigue rooms, quiet sleep rooms, hydration/food support, lockers, gear drying, decompression alcoves, and no-fly/sobering hold rooms.
- Pilot and Crew Operations District: pilot check-in, crew boards, contract boards, route advisories, berth assignment displays, cargo/inspection status, departure holds, crew credentials, shipmaster consultation rooms, union/guild/route-user notices, and controlled route toward the Starship Garage.
- Documentation / Translation / Mediation District: operational documentation, temporary crew/contractor access, cargo discrepancy forms, berth extensions, medical clearance paperwork, translation help, mediation for route/vendor/crew disputes, and private booths. Public consular/civic services remain in the Crown Admin/Civic Quadrant.
- Quiet Room / Memorial / Decompression District: quiet room, small memorial/reflection alcove, low-stim room, post-incident decompression, counselor/mediator call point, and escorted family/staff waiting. It is not a tourist memorial.
- Starship Garage Transition District: credential gates, pilot/crew access control, clean cargo interface, tool/gear pickup, ship service requests, inspection check-in, maintenance dispatch, emergency route back to Medical, and controlled access to the Starship Garage.
- Public-facing grooming, tailoring, shoe repair, luggage repair, hotel guest services, and similar presentable personal services belong on Hospitality Level 2, not Services.
- Public legal/admin/consular functions belong primarily in Crown Admin/Civic or H1 arrival/guest-services interfaces unless a controlled Services appointment is specifically needed.
- Services may include a future crew galley/pilot counter as a crew-facing recovery/support function, not a public breakfast venue.

## Staff-Only Off-Shift Space: The Spare Tap

- Status: hard canon.
- The Spare Tap is a staff-only off-shift bar and decompression room. It is not secret; it is simply not public.
- Location: two corridors behind the Hospitality Level 2 brewery service level, past a staff-only pressure door. It is back-of-house, not part of the public taproom, public hospitality areas, Residential corridors, command areas, or brewery production floor.
- It has a controlled stock/service relationship with the lower brewery/cellar so serving staff can retrieve alcohol quickly and safely. It is not a pass-through corridor for brewery workers, public bar staff, guests, or patrons.
- Function: a private staff decompression space where Der Himmelskrug workers can eat, drink, sing, complain, celebrate, play cards, mark off-shift milestones, and be ridiculous away from guest-facing hospitality and media attention.
- Look and feel: warm, copper-lit, deliberately unpolished, and staff-owned in atmosphere. Old stools, a bar made from retired tank-paneling, retired tap handles along the corridor, scratched festival signs, a dented brass bell of disputed purpose, a staff-only photo wall, a small karaoke setup, and personal mugs no one is allowed to wash except their owners.
- Rules: active-duty staff do not drink alcohol. Flight Control, Security, Engineering, Medical, Dirty-Side, and other safety-sensitive roles follow duty-specific restrictions. Off shift is not off record unless everyone agrees. Recording requires room consent; external sharing requires consent from identifiable staff.
- Operating principle: a public civic station still needs private rooms. Accountability does not mean surveillance, and staff joy is not station content unless staff choose to make it content.

## Garage and Restricted Industrial System

Formal map names:

- **Starship Garage**: the clean-side ship harbor and normal garage level.
- **Restricted Works**: the restricted dirty-side / hazardous industrial level.

Staff may still say “clean side,” “clean garage,” “dirty side,” or “dirty garage” in dialogue, but formal signage and planning should use Starship Garage and Restricted Works.

### Starship Garage

- The Starship Garage is the controlled clean-side ship harbor below Services. It handles normal docking, berthing, clean cargo, crew transition, inspection, safe departure handling, and non-hazardous ship support.
- Lower handle portal connects to the Starship Garage only. Public visitors do not enter it; passengers and crew still pass through Arrival Control, Services, or departure processing as appropriate.
- Typical functions: standard berths, extended-stay berths, priority/emergency clean berths, maintenance/inspection berths, floor-contact ship recharging, clean cargo automation, crew access, pilot services, supply access, personal gear recharge/service zones, garage operations, and maintenance dispatch.
- Garage Ops owns the local floor: berth assignment, docking collar status, utility hookups, cargo bay timing, garage safety alarms, staff dispatch, and local emergency response. Flight Control owns approach, traffic, and release windows.
- Clean cargo uses separate cargo corridors, locks, scan lanes, manifest checks, seal verification, clean sorting, freight lifts, and service routes. It does not move through crew showers, Medical waiting, hotel corridors, taproom corridors, Residential family routes, or public Retail.
- The Starship Garage can handle safed inspections, diagnostics, ordinary clean maintenance, non-live weapon-system checks, and declaration paperwork. It does not casually handle live missile, torpedo, mine/denial-charge, combat-drone munition, armor-piercing kinetic reloads, or open ordnance crates.
- If a ship, person, or cargo is reclassified as hazardous, suspicious, contaminated, battle-damaged, artifact-adjacent, weapons-irregular, or otherwise unsuitable for clean-side handling, Garage Ops freezes movement and the item routes to Restricted Works by sealed transfer, tow cradle, or controlled service lock.

## Internal Routing Flow Rules

- Der Himmelskrug uses route separation as a station-wide safety principle: public visitors, clean-side pilots, dirty-side cargo, residents, staff, VIPs, medical cases, and emergency traffic should not casually share the same paths unless a scene explicitly justifies it.
- Public visitors normally move through the upper handle, Arrival Control Complex, controlled entrance corridor, Hospitality Level 1, and then the public atrium levels: Observation, Entertainment, Hospitality 1, Hospitality 2, and Retail.
- Retail is the bottom of normal public movement. Below Retail requires purpose, clearance, duty, medical need, resident status, pilot/crew authorization, or emergency routing.
- Bubble elevators are public/scenic and stop only on the public atrium levels. They are not the station’s emergency, residential, school, Command, Medical, or garage backbone.
- Clean-side long-stay pilots and crews may move from the Starship Garage through Services, controlled crew/pilot routes, and public areas only after normal clearance.
- Dirty-side crews, salvage, artifacts, contaminated cargo, battle damage, dirty repair components, restricted ordnance, and suspicious cargo route through Restricted Works doors, locks, decontamination, quarantine, drone handling, ordnance, research, legal, and hazard workflows. They do not pass through public routes until cleared.
- Residents normally use dedicated residential access, resident/staff lifts, the Wohnhof, protected school corridors/lifts, and controlled service connections rather than tourist bubble elevators during ordinary daily life.
- Station staff use role-appropriate staff corridors, service routes, controlled garage links, and departmental access. Staff shortcuts exist, but Security, Flight Control, and departmental heads can close them during festivals, emergencies, VIP events, school movements, or dirty-side incidents.
- VIPs and consular visitors may use secure corridors from Arrival Control to Crown Admin/Civic areas when needed, but their route is still logged and screened.
- Medical cases route to Medical by the shortest safe controlled path, not through spectacle areas for drama unless the emergency has already reached public space.
- Festival/event routing adds crowd lanes, family-safe zones, PA/staff-channel communication, spectator exclusion zones, weapons-check enforcement, medical points, and controlled crew-service access. Excitement is allowed because routing is serious.
- Emergency routing may override normal comfort, but not clean/dirty separation unless the alternative is worse. “Probably fine” still goes below.
- Clean declaration means clean. Undeclared artifacts, salvage, contaminants, hazards, dirty repair components, and suspicious cargo must use dirty-side routing.
- Dirty-side items never leave the Restricted Works area unless properly cleared, processed, decontaminated, researched, stored, or disposed of.
- Flight Control routes ships, drones, cargo transfer, repair workflows, artifact containers, and personnel to keep dirty-side traffic separate from clean-side/public routes.
- Friendly rule: “If it belongs below, say so before we find it.”
- Running phrase: “Probably fine goes below.”
- Dishonest declarations create impoundment, decon/containment/admin costs, research/legal review, loss of clean-side privileges, bans from public docking where appropriate, and emergency dirty-side processing only when required by law.

## Permanent Location and Relocation Arc

Der Himmelskrug’s original unnamed location was a temporary assembly
mooring / construction anchorage, chosen for controlled construction,
low traffic, safe testing, and manageable logistics. It was never meant
to be the station’s permanent home.

Permanent destination: Fünfstrom-Sektor, with the exact five-route
convergence point known as Fünfstromknoten and commonly called “The
Knot” by pilots.

Nearby star: Lichtbrunn, selected as a reliable solar-power source for
the station. Permanent address language: Der Himmelskrug,
Fünfstrom-Sektor, near Lichtbrunn / Lichtbrunn Approach.

Five converging trade routes: New Bavaria Route, Zion Drift Route,
Tannenfall Route, Relay Haven Spur, and Coreward Freight Line.

Narrative purpose: the relocation marks Der Himmelskrug’s transition
from novelty brewery-port to permanent town-like hub. It strengthens the
case for the residential ring, staff-family quarters, Himmelskrug
Learning Center, expanded flight control, and long-term civic life.

Station movement canon: Der Himmelskrug moves using a temporary external
relocation framework clamped/anchored around the station, fitted with
powerful maneuvering engines and paired with massive tug ships capable
of pulling, pushing, and stabilizing a station of this scale.

Relocation operation: the move requires synchronized tug thrust,
structural stress monitoring, controlled acceleration/deceleration,
temporary route closures, traffic windows, and extensive Flight Control
coordination. Rolf treats decorative mass and thrust balance as personal
enemies.

Security and escort canon: partner-colony escort ships and IFP
(Interstellar Federation of Planets) assistance keep the relocation
corridor clear and safe. Their role is route control, traffic exclusion,
emergency response, and deterrence rather than turning the move into a
battle story by default.

Arrival infrastructure at Lichtbrunn: the relocation arc should include
solar collection deployment, energy buffer setup, approach-lane mapping,
and expansion of Flight Control authority for the five converging routes
at The Knot.

Likely relocation infrastructure: solar collection arrays near
Lichtbrunn, energy-buffer systems, expanded approach lanes, formal
traffic authority agreements, route-priority negotiations, clean/dirty
vector planning, and public recognition that Der Himmelskrug is becoming
fixed infrastructure rather than a temporary spectacle.
