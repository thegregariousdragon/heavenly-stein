# Economy, Law Enforcement, and Communications

This file defines baseline world mechanics for interstellar commerce,
station jurisdiction, law enforcement, and personal communications. These
rules should support story tension without turning every scene into a
technical manual.

## Economy

The common interstellar settlement unit is the **IFP Standard Credit**,
usually shortened to **standard credit** or simply **credit**. This does
not make the Interstellar Federation of Planets a bank-state. It means
that the IFP certifies and protects the clearing standards that allow
colonies, stations, route guilds, insurers, cargo brokers, governments,
and private operators to trade across long distances.

Local colony currencies, Earth currencies, corporate accounts, barter
traditions, guild vouchers, and vendor-specific credits still exist.
However, docking, recharge, official fees, fines, worker payroll,
inter-route contracts, insurance settlements, and most major station
accounts normally clear in standard credits.

Der Himmelskrug earns revenue through docking and berth fees, ship
recharge, clean-garage services, dirty-side processing and containment,
repair-center work, legal rearming services, taproom and brewery sales,
food hall and retail leases, lodging, rest pods, showers, laundry,
events, vendor rents, route-authority subsidies, partner-colony
contributions, IFP safety-infrastructure grants or contracts, festival
and race permits, summit hosting, insurance reimbursements, and recovery
of violation costs.

Fines and penalties are not treated as ordinary profit. After serious failures,
serious penalties, impoundment recoveries, and violation costs are
routed through restricted safety, audit, restitution, victim-support, or
infrastructure-repair funds when appropriate. The station must not look
as though it profits from catching people in danger.

Heinz can be wealthy from salvage, artifact recovery, combat and rescue
contracts, ship ownership, founding equity, emergency-response
compensation, licensing, and private investments he does not like talking
about. However, Der Himmelskrug's legitimacy cannot depend forever on
Heinz personally paying for everything. He can fund impossible
beginnings; he cannot personally bankroll legitimacy forever.

## Worker Economy

Regular station staff are paid in standard credits. Some housing,
meals, transport, laundry, child services, or medical access may be
subsidized as part of station employment, but Der Himmelskrug should not
function as a trap-like company town. Housing deductions, benefit costs,
overtime rules, festival surge schedules, and hazard premiums are
visible to the Workers' Guild and subject to ordinary review.

Hazard premiums apply to dirty-side work, contaminated cargo handling,
medical surge duty, emergency repair work, festival crowd-control surges,
weapons-storage surge duty, high-risk Flight Control operations,
hazardous recovery, and other defined dangerous assignments.

Workers can raise safety concerns through departmental channels, the
Workers' Guild, and the Independent Safety and Ethics Office. Emergency
exceptions may override normal consultation when life, station safety, or
route safety is immediately at risk, but those exceptions must be logged
and reviewed.

Useful worker-safety line: "If the paycheck only works when the oxygen
alarm stays polite, it is not a paycheck. It is a hostage note."

## Law Enforcement and Jurisdiction

Law enforcement is layered.

Der Himmelskrug Security handles immediate station safety and enforcement
inside the station and its assigned operational zone. The IFP handles
interstellar crimes, route-security threats, sabotage, piracy,
terrorism, diplomatic protection, cross-jurisdiction investigations,
extradition, and major emergency authority. Partner-colony authorities
handle consular protection, citizen notification, local civil claims,
colony-specific criminal follow-up, restitution claims, political
accountability, and culturally specific legal issues when jurisdiction
allows.

Der Himmelskrug is not a sovereign state and not merely Heinz's private
property. It is privately founded, partner-backed, and IFP-recognized
civic and route-safety infrastructure. Its authority must act quickly in
emergencies and remain reviewable afterward.

### Station Security

Station Security, led by Janelle Okonkwo, may detain people on station,
enforce weapons checks, remove people from public areas, secure crime
scenes, lock down corridors, search under station-entry terms, secure or
confiscate illegal weapons, protect residents and visitors, hold
intoxicated or violent persons temporarily, and preserve evidence for
IFP or partner-colony authorities.

Station Security does not operate as a permanent prison system, does not
try major crimes alone, does not ignore IFP review, and does not use
"Heinz owns it" as legal justification. Long-term detention transfers to
IFP or appropriate partner-colony custody.

Useful Janelle line: "We do not run a prison. We run a station. That is
why you are leaving in hand restraints."

### Flight Control

Flight Control, led by Amara Reid, is not ordinary police, but it has
hard operational authority over approach safety, docking, traffic lanes,
fault declarations, emergency vectors, beacon advisories, solar-array
exclusion zones, dirty-side routing, and traffic holds.

Flight Control may deny docking, assign approach lanes, order holds,
prioritize emergencies, require fault declarations, route dirty traffic
below, close approach corridors, demand decloak or transponder
compliance where law allows, and call IFP escalation.

Joy, turnout, elections, celebrity presence, and political pressure do
not modify traffic law.

### Compliance and Customs

Compliance and Customs, led by Mara Venn, handles cargo declarations,
contraband, agricultural and biological imports, clean/dirty
classification, manifest fraud, dirty-side routing documentation,
administrative penalties, costs, bans, reinstatement review, and
evidence packages for prosecution.

### Dirty-Side, Medical, and Other Authority Boundaries

Dirty-Side Operations controls hazardous recovery, decontamination,
artifact containers, quarantine locks, contaminated salvage, drone
handling, and research handoff. Medical controls triage, quarantine for
health reasons, sobering protocols, medical privacy, and emergency care.

Security can support these departments but should not override their
technical authority unless a clear safety emergency requires it.

### Due Process and Review

Station emergency authority can act first to stop danger. Review follows.

Serious uses of authority are logged. Security detentions, major
weapons incidents, dirty-side diversions, Flight Control emergency
orders, transport quarantines, medical diversions, impoundments, bans,
and major fines may be reviewed by the Independent Safety and Ethics
Office, the Governing Board, IFP oversight, partner-colony authorities,
or relevant labor and resident bodies depending on the issue.

Bans, fines, and impoundments should have appeal or reinstatement
processes. Feli's office releases careful public summaries when public
communication is appropriate, but private grief, medical detail, Guild
complaints, school issues, and active investigations are not content.

## Personal Communications and Data Devices

The standard personal device stack is a **data slate** paired with
optional extensions such as a **wristpad**, **earpiece**, or staff badge.
The hypernet is the network, not a separate handheld device. Older
references to a "hypernet browser" should be updated to "data slate"
unless a story deliberately refers to obsolete terminology.

### Data Slate

The data slate is the core personal device: part computer, communicator,
identity wallet, payment tool, map, translator, media terminal,
credential carrier, and work interface.

A slate may handle messages, voice/video calls, hypernet access, media
feeds, research, translation, identity credentials, payment wallet,
tickets, permits, docking records, emergency alerts, local maps, medical
profile access when authorized, menus and allergen data, route
advisories, work orders, ship manifests, legal forms, and authorized
transport requests.

A slate can request access. It cannot magically command everything. It
can open doors, request records, confirm transport routes, submit
manifests, or query station systems only when the user's credentials are
valid and the receiving system accepts the request.

### Wristpad

A wristpad is the wearable extension of the data slate. It is a quick
interface for haptic alerts, identity taps, access confirmations,
payment confirmations, emergency pings, route prompts, transport request
confirmation, short replies, health or suit status, and call control.
It can cache limited emergency credentials and local functions, but it
is not normally a full independent slate.

Most residents, crew, and station staff use ordinary wristpads. Staff
wristpads can receive role-based alerts and work-order prompts when
paired to a valid staff slate or badge.

### Heinz's Custom Wristpad

Heinz's right-wrist device is a custom high-end wristpad rather than a
traditional watch. It preserves the visual language of his earlier watch:
a black band, brushed gold or brass casing, and a ruby-red circular
display face with restrained black-red-gold accents. The design subtly
echoes the German flag without looking like a novelty flag gadget.

The wristpad pairs with Heinz's compact pale silver data slate and red
titanium earpiece. It serves as his quick interface for haptic alerts,
access confirmations, transport requests, payment confirmations,
ship/station status glances, emergency pings, silent messages, and call
control.

Heinz's default incoming-call tone is a solemn instrumental phrase from
the German national anthem. He considers this a normal default tone.
Almost no one else does.

Useful reactions:

- Feli: "Coward. Answer Germany."
- Clara/Klara: "I am not putting the anthem in an incident report."
- Tariq/Teriq: "Brother, your watch salutes itself."
- Janelle: "Captain, either answer Germany or mute Germany."
- Rolf: "The casing tolerances are acceptable. The politics of the
  ringtone are not my department."

### Earpiece

An earpiece is the audio extension of the slate, wristpad, ship, or
station system. It handles calls, comm channels, translation audio,
private alerts, tactical prompts, accessibility audio, noise filtering,
selective amplification, emergency tones, and staff PA relay.

Heinz's discreet red titanium earpiece remains his preferred audio
endpoint. If the slate is confiscated, jammed, dead, or out of range,
the earpiece may still receive local emergency broadcasts, but it loses
most personal functions.

### Staff Badges and Role Credentials

Staff badges may add local role credentials for departmental access,
shift status, emergency authority, medical routing, security presence,
clean/dirty permissions, or visitor-assistance functions. A badge does
not replace a slate; it proves a local role to station systems.

## Ship and Station Consoles

Ship and station consoles remain authoritative infrastructure. A data
slate can authenticate with them, submit requests, receive logs, or
control permitted functions, but the console, station system, ship
system, or receiving destination retains final authority.

A pilot's slate may submit a docking manifest. Flight Control grants or
denies the docking path. Heinz's slate may request transport to Bastion
Meridian. Bastion Meridian may accept, delay, deny, reroute, or lock down
the request.

This preserves the point-to-point transport rule: personal devices carry
authorization data, but they do not create destinations and do not
override receiving systems.

## Hypernet and Relay Communications

The hypernet is fast and normal along settled routes and relay networks,
but it does not make distance irrelevant. Frontier distance, solar
weather, beacon damage, hyperspace corridor problems, jamming, combat,
security lockdowns, dirty-side containment, artifact interference, and
relay outages can delay or degrade communications.

Basic scale:

- Same room or same station: local mesh and station repeaters.
- Ship near station: traffic, docking, and local ship-to-station
  channels.
- Same system: beacon relay and route-local communication.
- Between route nodes: hypernet relay and route beacons.
- Deep frontier: delayed bursts, cached packets, emergency beacons, or
  scheduled relay windows.
- Combat or sabotage zone: degraded, jammed, encrypted, line-of-sight,
  or fallback communications.

Cloaked ships can receive passively, but transmitting may increase
detectability. Normal traffic lanes still require lawful transponder and
traffic-control behavior.

## Translation

Translation systems are useful but imperfect. They handle standard
speech well but struggle with slang, dialect, songs, jokes, legal terms,
ritual language, intoxicated speech, overlapping crowd noise,
culturally loaded phrases, and German ship names such as Lass ma einen
bauen.

Translation failure should remain a source of comedy, policy confusion,
and cultural learning, not proof that competent characters are stupid.

Useful line: "The translator gave up and filed a cultural incident
report."

## Privacy and Surveillance

Der Himmelskrug monitors public safety areas, arrival systems,
traffic-control zones, dirty-side routes, weapons storage, and other
high-risk operational spaces. Private quarters are not casually
monitored. Medical, legal, consular, Guild, school, resident, and family
communications have stronger privacy rules.

Security access to records is possible under emergency authority or
proper process, but serious access is logged. Rebuild-era reforms
require audit trails. The Independent Safety and Ethics Office can
inspect access logs. Unlogged surveillance is treated as a serious
violation, not as cleverness.

Public communication is not merely data. On Der Himmelskrug it includes
soundscape, signage, haptics, alerts, wayfinding, translation, PA timing,
menu/allergen visibility, crowd-flow management, and emotional legibility.
This makes Cultural Atmosphere and Public Experience operationally
important, especially during festivals, emergencies, family traffic, and
public-space confusion.

## Cannabis, Controlled Intoxicants, and Destination Law

Cannabis and related adult-use intoxicants are governed by local jurisdiction, ship authority, station policy, customs status, medical rules, and safety-duty requirements. Der Himmelskrug may permit adult cannabis use under station jurisdiction, but station legality does not override destination law, shipmaster authority, crew contracts, medical restrictions, or safety-critical duty rules.

### Cannabis jurisdiction map

- **Der Himmelskrug:** adult cannabis use is legal under station jurisdiction through a controlled pilot program. Use is limited to licensed designated areas or approved private residential spaces. Public use outside approved areas is prohibited.
- **Zion Drift / Port Irie:** broadly permissive. Adult cannabis use is culturally normal, especially around music, hospitality, rest, and private life. Medical use is accepted. Crew impairment rules remain strict.
- **Tannenfall / Larkspur Landing:** restrictive rather than prohibitionist. Medical use is tightly regulated. Recreational possession/use exists only in licensed zones or declared private-use contexts. Sale is limited, public use is prohibited, and biosecurity rules are strict. Plant material, seeds, residue, oils, molds, unlabeled food products, and uninspected botanical cargo can trigger inspection or decontamination review.
- **New Bavaria:** cautious, especially about public intoxication and cannabis/alcohol pairing. Medical allowances exist, but social norms are conservative around beer culture, public hospitality, and safety-sensitive work.
- **Relay Haven:** pragmatic and tolerant in some zones, but supply controls can be uneven. Safety-duty impairment rules are strict because preventable maintenance mistakes can endanger survival infrastructure.
- **Veyr Compact:** severe prohibition jurisdiction. Certain unauthorized intoxicant or neuroactive violations, especially trafficking, undeclared import, distribution to crew, possession in restricted zones, or repeat smuggling, may carry execution statute or permanent penal exile.

### Der Himmelskrug adult cannabis policy

Hard policy outcomes established through the Green Room pilot:

- **The Green Room** is the public name for the station’s cannabis lounge/pilot space.
- **Grünraum** is the internal, licensing, or technical name.
- Adult use is permitted only under station policy, in designated areas or approved private spaces.
- Public use outside approved areas is prohibited.
- No minors in cannabis use areas.
- No weapons inside cannabis use areas; weapons must be checked, sealed, or locked before entry.
- No access to Restricted Works rearming facilities while impaired.
- No cannabis/alcohol bundle promotions, pairings, “flights,” or cross-selling designed to stack intoxication.
- No unverified vape cartridges, unlabeled edibles, homemade public-sale edibles, mystery concentrates, or products making unsupported medical cure claims.
- Products require clear labeling, dosage guidance, origin records, and medical warnings.
- Dedicated ventilation and filtration are required; corridor odor bleed is an engineering and policy failure.
- Staff assigned to cannabis areas require training, assignment protections, and appropriate pay.
- Legal off-duty adult use is not automatically misconduct.
- On-duty impairment remains a safety issue, especially in safety-critical roles.
- Safety-critical roles require strict sobriety/rest expectations. This includes Flight Control, pilots, docking and tug crews, Security on armed duty, Medical on clinical duty, dirty-side containment, air/water/power systems, heavy maintenance, clean/dirty inspection, weapons/rearming work, and child-supervision roles.
- First self-report before safety-duty contact can be handled as safety removal, Medical assessment, policy counseling, and limited pay protection, with stricter consequences for repeated incidents or system contact.
- Testing should be cause-based, incident-based, or role-required by defined safety policy, not random humiliation fishing.

### Destination-law controls

- Legal here does not mean legal in your luggage.
- Sealed takeaway sales require destination-law screening.
- Consumption-only sales are the default for uncertain destinations.
- No sealed takeaway sales are permitted to Veyr-bound passengers or crew.
- Passengers bound for prohibition jurisdictions must be offered surrender, storage, disposal, or compliant sealed-transfer options before boarding.
- Tannenfall-bound passengers and shippers require biosecurity warnings around plant material, residue, unlabeled edibles, agricultural cargo compartments, seed stock, starter cultures, and food-chain shipments.
- Shipmaster authority can impose additional onboard restrictions even where station use is legal.
