# Transport and Movement Mechanics

Personnel teleportation is point-to-point, not anywhere-to-anywhere. A
valid transport requires an authorized origin point, an authorized
destination point, sufficient power, a clear destination volume, and
accepted identity/route authorization.

Authorized teleportation points may include shipboard wormhole consoles,
station Transit Reception Center pads, docking-bay pads, planetary
landing pads, colony transit halls, IFP pads, medical receiving pads,
secure consular/VIP pads, and temporary field beacons or survey markers
when properly registered.

Personal remotes carry authorization data. They identify the user, store
permitted destinations, request routes, and negotiate with compatible
ship, station, colony, or field systems. A remote does not bypass
transport-point requirements and cannot freely teleport a person from
anywhere to anywhere. Personal remotes authorize access; they do not
create destinations.

Authorization is two-sided. The traveler’s remote may prove that the
traveler is allowed to request transport, but the destination point must
also accept, deny, delay, reroute, or quarantine the arrival according
to local security, safety, customs, medical, dirty-side, or emergency
rules.

Der Himmelskrug does not allow routine direct teleportation into the
taproom, platz, residential corridors, school, brewery production
spaces, crown command areas, or public hospitality areas. Normal
authorized arrivals route through the Transit Reception Center within
the Arrival Control Complex. Staff, medical, VIP, dirty-side, and
emergency arrivals may be routed to appropriate controlled receiving
points.

Dirty, hazardous, undeclared, intoxicated, undocumented, hostile, armed,
legally complicated, or artifact-adjacent arrivals may be denied public
release or diverted to Security, Medical, dirty-side containment,
weapons storage, consular review, or quarantine.

Emergency overrides exist but route to the safest controlled receiving
point available, not necessarily the requester’s preferred destination.
All emergency overrides are logged and reviewed.

Hostile or restricted locations cannot normally be entered by teleport
unless the destination point is known, compatible, and accepting.
Transport denial fields, shields, receiver locks, scramblers, and
security interlocks can block or complicate unauthorized boarding.

Narrative pattern: festival or colony alert -> Heinz uses an authorized
remote/point route to reach Lass ma einen bauen, Der Himmelskrug, or IFP
Bastion Meridian -> Explorer or station systems handle the first
response -> if escalated, he reaches Bastion Meridian and boards the
Decimator, Hammerhead, or Triad.
