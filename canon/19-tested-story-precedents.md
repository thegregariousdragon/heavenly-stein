## Status and use rules

This file preserves system precedents tested during earlier story development. It is **not** active chronology and does not preserve old story plots, scene blocking, or named incidents as canon events.

The continuity rebuild keeps worldbuilding and character canon active while clearing old story/event canon. Use this file for principles about how the setting behaves: safety doctrine, law, economic consequence, labor protection, communications practice, institutional behavior, cannabis policy, and route/station procedure.

When this file conflicts with current station layout, `quick-reference/CANON_LOCKS.md`, `quick-reference/MECHANICS_CHEATSHEET.md`, or numbered worldbuilding canon, the newer/current worldbuilding canon wins.

## Public-space force and nonlethal weapons

- **Nonlethal does not mean harmless.** Nonlethal, stun, restraint, disruptor, and other less-lethal tools can still injure, kill vulnerable people, interfere with implants or medical hardware, trigger falls, disrupt lifts or shields, or cause secondary harm.
- Public-space force is never only a combat choice. It is also a crowd, Medical, infrastructure, legal, communications, accessibility, and liability problem.
- Security owns immediate force escalation in security incidents. Medical can complicate or veto supposedly safe options when bodies, implants, intoxication, panic, gravity/lift conditions, or crowd density make them dangerous.
- Public communications during an incident should be honest containment: no lies, no panic, no propaganda, no using an injured offender or victim to win an argument.
- Valid complaints and dangerous acts can run on separate review tracks. A dangerous act does not erase a possible underlying failure; a possible underlying failure does not excuse endangering people.
- Heinz should not automatically become the center of every emergency. The station has other load-bearing members.

## Flight Control, approach zones, liens, and disabling force

- Flight Control owns controlled approach space. Private enforcement, celebrity, founder status, cargo claims, liens, debt recovery, and corporate pressure do not outrank traffic safety.
- A valid debt or lien is not a weapons permit. Lawful claims must still obey approach orders, weapons restrictions, route-stability rules, and IFP-recognized traffic authority.
- Emergency safety is not debt forgiveness. Protecting a ship, crew, passenger, cargo, or station from imminent harm does not automatically decide debt, lien, cargo ownership, or contract disputes.
- Cargo preservation does not decide cargo ownership. Keeping cargo viable is a safety/economic preservation act, not a legal ruling.
- Disabling is usually harder than destroying. A disabling shot or utility-emitter intervention can cause tumble, cargo loss, rescue-system damage, heat/load cascade, crew injury, or expensive secondary rescue.
- Less destructive is not automatically safer. If force feels clean, someone may have hidden the invoice.
- Utility emitters can become weapons by context. A tool used for grappling, cutting, recovery, pushing, shielding, or manipulation can be a weapon when aimed at a vulnerable ship, system, person, or cargo path.
- Cost allocation is part of the aftermath. Rescue, shield support, crew care, cargo preservation, repair, lost wages, berth handling, and emergency services may be logged and assigned later.

## Cannabis, Grünraum, labor, ventilation, and destination law

- Der Himmelskrug permits adult cannabis only under controlled station rules.
- The public name is **The Green Room**. The internal/licensing/technical name is **Grünraum**.
- Cannabis use is allowed only in designated areas or approved private residential contexts. Public use outside approved areas remains prohibited.
- Cannabis and alcohol cannot be bundled, cross-promoted, or operationally treated as a stacked-intoxication package.
- Legal off-duty use alone is not misconduct. Safety-critical on-duty impairment remains prohibited.
- Staff working cannabis areas require training, assignment protections, appropriate pay, and ventilation/odor safeguards. Legalization without design dumps consequences onto workers.
- Destination-law screening is required for sealed takeaway. “Legal here” does not mean legal in a traveler’s luggage or next port.
- No sealed takeaway sales to Veyr-bound passengers or crew unless a later canon update changes Veyr law.
- Ventilation and filtration must be dedicated and inspected. Corridor odor bleed is an engineering/policy failure, not atmosphere.
- The Green Room succeeds when it is boringly well-run: clear dosage information, water, snacks, quiet-down capacity, no corridor odor, no alcohol bundling, and no forced spectacle.

## Labor, governance, and institutional consequences

- Workers, pilots, crew, residents, victims, visitors, and contractors are not background costs. Their time, safety, grief, wages, exposure, and privacy matter.
- Welcome must be enforceable, not decorative. Events, festivals, Pride-like programming, and civic hospitality require routing, crowd control, staff protection, accessibility, and aftercare.
- Staff privacy is not public content. The Spare Tap is a staff-only decompression space, not a tourist venue or character-display window.
- Incident review should separate safety, legal responsibility, compensation, labor protection, communications, and public narrative rather than pretending one hearing solves all truths.
- Governance becomes more legitimate when responsibility is inspectable: records, route authority, labor protections, independent review, and family/victim privacy all matter.

## Dirty-side trust, clean/dirty routing, and inspectable reform

- Clean/dirty routing is not cosmetic. Dirty-side cargo, salvage, damaged ships, suspicious material, contaminated equipment, artifact-adjacent objects, and hazardous repair must not leak into public, residential, hospitality, school, or clean-garage paths.
- Trusted infrastructure can be exploited without every staff member becoming incompetent. Strong stories should distinguish personal negligence, institutional design flaws, hostile manipulation, and honest limits.
- Tooling, cartridges, firmware, maintenance logs, decon records, custody chains, and access-path changes are story-active systems. They should be inspectable and consequential.
- After serious failures, reforms should appear as procedures people can see and follow, not merely as speeches.

## Communications, media, and public narrative

- Public statements should communicate what is known, what is not known, what people should do now, and who has authority.
- The dead, injured, frightened, accused, grieving, or privately wronged are not content. Media interest does not erase privacy or due process.
- Feli’s communications role is strongest when she refuses both panic and spin.
- Media feeds can be useful, hostile, lazy, funny, neutral to a fault, or exploitative; they should not all behave alike.

## Character and institution behavior reminders

- Character specifics remain active canon in the character files and quick-reference cards. This file does not replace them.
- Heinz can be load-bearing without taking over every room. His restraint is often more interesting than his arrival.
- Feli is public-facing but not merely decorative; she manages civic meaning, language, and accountability.
- Flight Control, Security, Medical, Legal/Cultural Affairs, Engineering, Dirty-Side Operations, Governance/Labor, and Hospitality each have real authority within their lanes.
- Tariq/Teriq’s atmosphere and crowd-reading work can be operationally meaningful without making him less funny.
- Rolf, Nikki, Amara, Janelle, Dr. Santos, Clara/Klara, Sanaa, Greta, and other station professionals should be allowed to solve problems that belong to their role.

## Reusable serious phrases

These phrases may be reused or adapted, but they are not mandatory catchphrases.

- “Nonlethal does not mean harmless.”
- “The station has other load-bearing members.”
- “A valid lien is not a weapons permit.”
- “Emergency safety is not debt forgiveness.”
- “Both truths will be reviewed.”
- “If force feels clean, someone hid the invoice.”
- “Invoices also follow.”
- “Legal here does not mean legal in your luggage.”
- “Adult does not mean consequence-free.”
- “A forbidden corner teaches people to sneak; a designed room teaches people where responsibility lives.”
- “Welcome is not decoration.”

## What these precedents should not over-restrict

- Future stories do not need to recreate earlier test plots.
- Future incidents do not need the same named ships, victims, offenders, companies, routes, or exact scene structures.
- Not every safety story needs Heinz, Tariq, Feli, Flight Control, Medical, Security, and Legal all on-page. Use the roles the scene needs.
- The setting should remain funny, warm, musical, domestic, and absurd. Process and consequence should support that warmth, not flatten it into bureaucracy.
