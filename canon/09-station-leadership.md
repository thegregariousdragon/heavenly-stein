# Station Leadership and Operational Cast

Der Himmelskrug now has a clearer operational cast because the station
is permanent infrastructure at The Knot rather than a novelty
brewery-port. These roles prevent Heinz from functioning as every
department head and give the station believable adult supervision during
normal operations, festivals, civic disputes, traffic incidents,
artifact problems, and residential/community stories.

Title principle: formal titles should sound usable in station policy,
manifests, incident bulletins, and consular documents. Informal station
slang may coexist when it fits character voice, such as Tariq being
called Director of Vibes despite having a more formal title on paper.

Heinz Schmitt: Founder, independent captain, strategic operations
authority, and crisis responder. He remains the impossible center of
gravity, but ordinary station departments should be run by their
assigned leaders unless the situation escalates into salvage, artifact,
ship-combat, major engineering, or founder-level judgment.

Felicia “Feli” Brandt: Director of Communications and Civic Interface.
Her authority covers public communications, media handling, civic
language, crisis messaging, cultural interface, family/community
communication, and turning Heinz’s terse engineering truth into public
language people can survive.

Rolf Petersen: Director of Engineering and Structural Systems. He owns
mechanical sanity, station structural systems, load paths, inspection
access, garage machinery, maintenance reliability, repair practicality,
fountain/chandelier sanity, and whether decorative joy is allowed to
remain attached to the station.

Lukas Schmitt: Station Brewing Operations Lead / Tapline and Production
Director. He owns daily brewery operations, tapline logic, keg routing,
production schedule, pressure, temperature, server technique, and
front-to-back brewing reliability. He is not merely Heinz’s little
brother; he is the day-to-day authority for beer operations.

Tariq/Teriq Neumann: Director of Cultural Atmosphere and Public
Experience. Station slang: Director of Vibes. His domain includes music
programming, public mood, festival sound/light design, performance
coordination, Zion Drift warmth, crowd-flow intuition,
acoustic/vibration oddities, public-space readability, de-escalation
support, family and visitor wayfinding, and sticker/merch chaos within
limits. He is professionally competent and operationally valuable. His
absurdity should be style, not incompetence; jokes around him should not
make him the station punching bag or erase that his work is
load-bearing.

Mara Venn: Director of Compliance and Customs Integrity. Her domain
includes clean declarations, cargo compliance, agricultural/biological
import rules, suspicious manifest review, dirty-side routing policy,
incident bulletins, costs, bans, and reinstatement review. She helps
make “Probably fine goes below” legally durable.

Amara Reid: Chief Flight Control Officer. Her authority covers
Lichtbrunn Approach and Fünfstromknoten traffic control: approach lanes,
queue priority, emergency vectors, solar-array exclusion zones,
fault-declaration enforcement, traffic windows, and coordination with
clean/dirty routing. She should remain warm on comms until safety
requires steel.

Janelle Okonkwo: Chief of Station Security. Black woman, Afro-European /
Nigerian-descended, raised with Relay Haven service-station and
convoy-route experience; former IFP security NCO. She owns weapons
checks, public-area enforcement, festival security, security storage,
crew-service corridor access, threat assessment, and post-incident
discipline. She is calm, dry, observant, and does not raise her voice
because she has rarely needed to.

Dr. Mireya Santos: Medical Director / Director of Medical Services and
Emergency Care. Latina, with emergency medicine and trauma-stabilization
expertise. She owns clinic operations, emergency response, life-support
coordination, festival injury protocols, medical triage during docking
incidents, sobering-up protocols for pilots, resident health policy, and
coordination with Karin’s safety curriculum. She makes consequences real
without becoming humorless.

Nkiru “Nikki” Adebayo: Director of Hazardous Recovery and Dirty-Side
Operations. They/them; Nigerian-British salvage-containment specialist
and nonbinary artifact quarantine professional. Their authority covers
dirty-side intake, artifact containers, contaminated salvage, drone
handling, decon workflow, quarantine locks, hazardous disposal, research
handoff, and coordination with Mara, Rolf, Rowan, and Heinz. They are
cheerful in a way that unsettles people when dangerous things are
properly labeled.

Priya Natarajan: Director of Residential Life and Family Services.
Indian-Terran or Indian-descended habitat-community specialist, married
to a woman, with a school-age child in the Learning Center. Her domain
includes staff housing, family quarters, quiet hours, resident
complaints, child-safe corridors, muster drills, laundry/rest/clinic
coordination, family access to the Learning Center, and the friction
between permanent residents and festival visitors. She proves Der
Himmelskrug is a town, not merely a port.

Elior Ben-Ami: Learning Center Lead Instructor / On-Site Education
Coordinator. He/him; Israeli-descended, gay, former shipboard tutor for
long-haul family crews. He runs daily classroom operations, mixed-age
learning groups, station safety lessons, remote-learning support,
cultural literacy, Cultural Level field trips, and “no one licks
artifacts” education. He works from Karin Schmitt’s remote curriculum
and adapts it to station life.

Keisha Bell: Director of Port Services and Crew Support. Black
American-descended freighter-family veteran from Relay Haven routes and
former family-cargo logistics chief. She owns pilot services, rest pods,
showers/laundry, crew hiring boards, supply access, clean cargo
automation, freighter crew support, and coordination with Security and
Medical for drunk or exhausted pilots. She grounds the station in
working-crew needs rather than luxury fantasy.

Lian Zhou: Director of Colony Relations and Consular Services. He/they;
Chinese-Singaporean-descended, bisexual, trained in intercultural
mediation and treaty logistics. Their domain includes colony consulates,
diplomatic scheduling, formal visitor protocols, colony contribution
disputes, cultural appendix coordination, translation review, and
working with Feli, Clara/Klara, Anja, and Mara. They can make a hostile
memo sound like a tea invitation.

Mateo Alvarez: Taproom General Manager. Mexican-German, raised between
Mainz and orbital hospitality work; pansexual unless later canon changes
this. He owns daily taproom floor operations, server training, crowd
flow, table turnover, guest behavior, festival service, and coordination
with Lukas and Security. He separates brewery production authority from
front-of-house hospitality authority.

Sanaa El-Masri: Director of Stores and Supply Chain. Egyptian-descended
logistics expert from the Coreward Freight Line. She owns ingredient
inventory, spare parts, festival stockpiles, medical supplies, emergency
reserves, supplier contracts, clean/dirty storage separation, and the
mathematics of not running out of pretzels, coolant seals, yeast, or
trauma kits during the same incident.

Dr. Rowan Ibarra: Artifact Research Liaison. They/she; Afro-Latine queer
xenoarchaeology researcher. Their domain includes artifact intake
review, research/legal transfer, display approval, Vesh resonance cup
coordination, artifact myth correction, and public education about why
curiosity still needs procedure. They work closely with Nikki, Mara,
Karin, and Heinz so artifacts keep wonder without becoming vague magic.

Clara/Klara Obermeier: Director of Legal-Cultural Affairs. New Bavarian
Cultural Liaison / Policy Liaison remains part of her identity and
background, but her station-facing title is now Director of
Legal-Cultural Affairs. The title fits her blend of policy translation,
case-law prevention, cultural appendices, incident-report language,
liaison with New Bavaria and Anja Reiter, and making sure Heinz’s
precedents do not accidentally become civilization. Avoid “Chief Legal
Officer” unless she later becomes the station’s top lawyer.

Operational grouping: Command and crisis = Heinz, Feli, Rolf, Amara,
Janelle, Clara/Klara, Anja, and relevant IFP contacts. Port, safety, and
infrastructure = Amara, Janelle, Mara, Nikki, Rolf, Sanaa, Keisha.
Community, education, and public life = Priya, Elior, Karin, Feli,
Tariq, Dr. Santos, Mateo. Diplomacy, research, and colony relations =
Feli, Lian, Clara/Klara, Anja, Mara, Rowan, Nikki, Heinz when necessary.
