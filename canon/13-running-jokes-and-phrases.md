# Running Jokes, Phrases, and Reusable Lines

## Use note

This file preserves reusable lines and tonal anchors. It does not preserve old story events or old chronology. Use phrases sparingly; repeated catchphrases should feel earned, not pasted in.

## Core operational phrases

- “Nobody appears behind the beer.” — arrival-control and transport-safety rule.
- “Labels are life support.” — food, allergy, cargo, and customs safety principle.
- “Probably fine goes below.” — clean/dirty routing shorthand.
- “Nonlethal does not mean harmless.” — force and Medical doctrine.
- “A valid lien is not a weapons permit.” — law/economy/Flight Control principle.
- “Emergency safety is not debt forgiveness.” — rescue and contract disputes remain separate.
- “Legal here does not mean legal in your luggage.” — Green Room / Grünraum destination-law rule.
- “Adult does not mean consequence-free.” — cannabis, alcohol, labor, and safety-duty rule.
- “Welcome is not decoration.” — public events require enforceable safety, accessibility, and staff protection.
- “Trust requires verification.” — mature station safety culture.

## Character-flavored lines

- “Machines do not get excited.” — Rolf.
- “I hate mostly.” — Rolf.
- “Honesty is cheaper before the scan.” — customs/security culture.
- “The station has other load-bearing members.” — founder-restraint principle.
- “Anger is information. It is not orders.” — Annika/Karin family principle.
- “Process first. Joy follows. Music helps.” — Der Himmelskrug hospitality motto.

## Public signage and jokes

- “Don’t bring lasers to beer.” — informal public weapons-safety signage.
- “No artifact cheese exists.” — source-evaluation and smuggling-rumor joke.
- “The cannabis theme is the respect.” — Tariq/Teriq on responsible design.
- “Bad wiring. Worse decisions.” — Tariq/Teriq or maintenance-adjacent commentary.

## Media/governance frames

- “Who owns The Knot?” — a legitimate governance question, especially from skeptical media or resident/labor voices.
- “Private rescue is not public governance.” — skeptical-media frame; sometimes unfair, sometimes useful.
- “Beer kings are still kings.” — Redline-style critique; not automatically true, not automatically bad faith.

## Reuse caution

Do not attach these phrases to removed story events. They are active because the principles remain useful, not because any old plot is active chronology.
