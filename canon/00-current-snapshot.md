# Current Snapshot and Project Overview

Canon navigation: [Home](README.md) | Previous: none | [Next: 01 Style and Continuity Rules](01-style-and-continuity-rules.md)


The Heinz Schmitt stories are a warm, absurd, continuity-heavy space-frontier saga about Heinrich “Heinz” Schmitt: German aerospace engineer, independent captain, salvage/artifact specialist, musician, brewer’s son, reluctant public figure, and founder of Der Himmelskrug.

The central mode remains:

> Ridiculous premise, serious engineering underneath.

Der Himmelskrug, “The Heavenly Stein,” is a tall beer-stein-shaped orbital brewery/free-port/station. It is funny from a distance and serious up close: brewery, port, clean-side/restricted logistics facility, cultural hub, public hospitality space, residential community, learning center, staff-only decompression spaces, and route-safety institution.

## Continuity rebuild status

The project is in a continuity rebuild. Core worldbuilding canon remains active: law, economy, corporations, colonies and locations, Der Himmelskrug station layout, ships, ship systems, weapons and force rules, transport, governance, labor, communications, media feeds, clean-side/restricted-side routing, and established station procedures.

Character-specific canon also remains active. Use the character canon files and quick-reference cards for names, pronouns, relationships, voice, behavior, authority, and scene fingerprints.

Earlier story plots, chronology, scene blocking, and one-off incidents are not active canon unless explicitly re-promoted later. `canon/19-tested-story-precedents.md` remains active only as a system-precedent file.

## Current setting status

- Der Himmelskrug is operational at Fünfstromknoten / The Knot near Lichtbrunn.
- The station serves the New Bavaria Route, Zion Drift Route, Tannenfall Route, Relay Haven Spur, and Coreward Freight Line.
- The station is privately founded, partner-backed, IFP-recognized civic and route-safety infrastructure. It is not a sovereign state and not merely Heinz’s private beer station.
- The IFP remains the major interstellar security, diplomatic, and route-stability authority.
- Der Himmelskrug’s authority at The Knot comes from IFP recognition, partner-colony backing, station competence, and enforceable procedure.

## Station interior layout snapshot

Der Himmelskrug’s current interior redesign is organized as:

1. Crown
2. Observation
3. Entertainment
4. Hospitality Level 1
5. Hospitality Level 2
6. Retail
7. Residential
8. Services
9. Starship Garage
10. Restricted Works

Key layout locks:

- Crown is a four-quadrant civic cap: Command, Administration/Civic, Learning Center, and Museum. Command is strictly restricted; the other Crown quadrants are controlled by purpose.
- Observation remains an open 360-degree viewing/event level with the chandelier and public atrium void.
- Entertainment replaces the old Cultural/exhibit level as the public venue level. Exhibits live in the Crown Museum Quadrant.
- Hospitality Level 1 contains the formal arrival reveal, controlled arrivals route, main taproom, upper brewery face, cafés/restaurants, and press suite.
- Hospitality Level 2 contains the food hall, largest bar, lower brewery, short-term visitor lodging, personal services, and The Spare Tap’s brewery-service adjacency.
- Retail is the final public level, the bottom of the public atrium, and the home of The Green Room / Grünraum.
- Residential is a restricted town-like level with the Wohnhof and residential quadrants. Senior staff housing belongs here, not in the Crown.
- Services is controlled staff/pilot/operational support, not a general public shopping or atrium level.
- Starship Garage and Restricted Works remain physically and procedurally separate. Clean/dirty remain valid staff shorthand; formal maps use Starship Garage and Restricted Works.

## Active worldbuilding pillars

- Arrival Control remains mandatory: nobody routinely appears behind the beer.
- Clean-side/restricted-side routing is a station-wide safety principle.
- Flight Control owns approach safety and controlled station movement where applicable.
- Bubble elevators are iconic public-atrium transport, not the emergency backbone.
- Hard-service cores, emergency lifts, staff routes, Medical routes, residential routes, and dirty-side industrial routes remain separate by purpose.
- The Green Room / Grünraum is a controlled adult cannabis venue with destination-law checks, no alcohol bundling, and dedicated ventilation.
- The Spare Tap is staff-only and not a tourist venue.
- Weapons, force, ship systems, law, economy, corporations, media feeds, governance, labor, and character fingerprints remain active canon in their numbered files.
