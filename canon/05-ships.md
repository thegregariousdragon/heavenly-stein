## Ship Capability Canon

Older exact numerical ship-performance entries are no longer controlling
story canon. Future stories should use narrative capability profiles
instead of exact stat blocks.

The ships retain their established names, classes, roles, basing,
transport compatibility, and relative power hierarchy. Exact shield
totals, hull values, engine values, repair-drone counts, weapon levels,
and recharge timings should not be treated as binding unless a future
story establishes a specific technical fact for dramatic reasons.

All of Heinz’s major ships are advanced by frontier civilian standards
and capable of long-range interstellar operation. Their differences
matter more than exact numbers.

### Lass ma einen bauen — Explorer / Armed Expedition Platform

Lass ma einen bauen is Heinz’s one-pilot Explorer: mobile home,
workshop, expedition platform, salvage craft, artifact-response ship,
and public symbol. It is heavily upgraded, well shielded, and unusually
well armed for an exploration vessel.

It can survive frontier trouble, deter small hostile craft, support
salvage and construction work, operate independently for long periods,
and buy time during a crisis. It is not a dedicated warship and should
not casually defeat major combat vessels. In serious attacks, its best
role is to hold the line, evacuate people, gather tactical data, and
keep the situation alive until Heinz can escalate to a combat ship or
outside support.

### Niemand Wird Dich Vermissen — Decimator / Solo Strike Ship

Niemand Wird Dich Vermissen is Heinz’s compact one-pilot combat ship,
built for fast, precise, overwhelming violence. It has far greater
combat power than the Explorer and is optimized for pursuit, long-range
killing, shield-breaking, and ending isolated hostile threats quickly.

It is not a diplomatic vessel, cargo craft, family ship, or
station-support platform. It solves the narrow class of problems where
the correct answer is “stop that hostile ship now.”

### Komm Her Und Stirb Schnell — Hammerhead / Heavy Command Fortress

Komm Her Und Stirb Schnell is Heinz’s major heavy combat platform: a
mobile fortress, fleet anchor, command ship, deterrent, and strategic
response asset. Its arrival changes the political and tactical meaning
of a crisis.

It can coordinate allied ships, absorb major punishment, project
overwhelming force, support large-scale defense, and serve as the center
of a serious battle response. It should not appear casually. Bringing
the Hammerhead means the situation has escalated beyond ordinary
frontier danger.

### Das Geht Schnell — Triad / Fast-Response Interceptor-Rescue Ship

Das Geht Schnell is Heinz’s three-person fast-response ship. It is built
for rapid interception, emergency rescue, extraction, pursuit, and
precision response.

It is not as symbolically terrifying as the Hammerhead or as narrowly
predatory as the Decimator, but it is often the ship that saves lives
because it arrives first. It is ideal for medical evacuation, pilot
rescue, route incidents, fast boarding response, and situations where
seconds matter.

### Nur Für Den Fall — Armed Expedition Cutter

Nur Für Den Fall (“Just in case”) is a privately owned armed expedition
cutter used by Heinz and trusted companions for long-range off-station
travel, engineering trips, remote-site visits, governance delegations,
family travel in uncertain space, and private transit to Die Werkbank.
It is not a Der Himmelskrug institutional asset and not primarily a
warship.

It fills the gap between Lass ma einen bauen and Niemand Wird Dich
Vermissen: it has Explorer-like range, self-sufficiency, workshop
practicality, strong sensors, shields, cloak, secure comms, compact
repair/fabrication capability, medical alcove, modest cargo bay, and
real but limited weapons. It normally carries 6–7 people comfortably and
more in emergency crowding.

Combat role: it can fight off raiders, contractor cutters, drones,
ambush craft, and small hostile groups, but it is not designed to
replace the Decimator, Triad, or Hammerhead. It has teeth, but its
doctrine is: survive, shield, disable, and escape. Useful line: “It is
not a combat ship.” Rolf’s correction: “It is also not not a combat
ship.”

### Relative Power Rule

The Explorer survives and solves.

The Decimator hunts and ends.

The Hammerhead arrives and changes the war.

The Triad gets there before the disaster finishes happening.

### Hyperspace and Long-Range Travel

Heinz’s major ships have elite long-range hyperspace capability by
frontier standards. They can respond across large interstellar distances
fast enough to matter in emergencies, especially when using known
routes, stable jump corridors, IFP navigation data, or established
colony approach lanes.

Travel time remains narratively flexible. Distance, route quality,
navigation hazards, traffic control, gravity wells, damaged systems,
stealth requirements, escort coordination, and destination clearance can
all affect arrival time.

### Combat Scaling

Combat outcomes should be based on ship role, preparation, crew skill,
tactical position, surprise, terrain, support, and mission objective
rather than numerical stat comparisons.

The Explorer can survive and delay. The Decimator can kill quickly. The
Triad can intercept and rescue quickly. The Hammerhead can dominate a
battlefield but creates political and operational consequences by
showing up.

### Starship Cloak Rules

Starships in this canon can have cloaking systems, but cloaks are
limited. They are not perfect invisibility and do not override physics,
traffic safety, docking procedure, law, or consequences.

Core rules:

- A ship cannot fire starship weapons while cloaked. Beam weapons,
  lasers, missiles, torpedoes, combat drones, and other offensive
  starship-scale weapons require the ship to decloak or break cloak
  enough to become targetable and detectable.
- Shields can remain active while cloaked. Shield impacts or strong
  shield interactions may reveal approximate presence through flare,
  distortion, heat dump, electromagnetic disturbance, or particle
  displacement.
- Cloaks hide or distort visual profile, common sensor returns,
  emissions, transponder visibility when lawfully suppressed, thermal
  output within limits, and drive/hull signatures within limits. They do
  not eliminate mass, gravity effects, collision risk, drive wake,
  heat-management needs, or emergency obligations.
- Normal trade-route travel uses normal transponder behavior. Ships
  should not travel cloaked in traffic lanes, docking queues, port
  approaches, convoy lanes, or regulated arrival/departure corridors
  except under specific emergency, IFP, or authorized stealth protocols.
- Ships do not dock while cloaked. Docking requires local sensor
  confirmation, identity handshake, approach authorization,
  collision-avoidance tracking, alignment, seal verification, and clear
  abort paths. Rolf: “If I cannot see the ship, the docking system
  cannot pretend everything is fine.”
- Responsible cloak systems include emergency decloak triggers and logs
  for collision risk, medical emergency, power/life-support failure,
  rescue request, IFP emergency challenge, proximity to civilian
  traffic, or docking-system failure.

Private cloak transit to Die Werkbank: Heinz or Rolf travels normally on
recognized trade routes to the nearest sensible exit point, departs the
route, verifies safe separation from civilian traffic, activates cloak,
proceeds by private navigation to Die Werkbank’s sector, decloaks
outside Die Werkbank’s local safety perimeter, then completes a normal
authenticated docking approach. Cloak is for privacy and location
security, not for docking, combat, or evading lawful emergency response.

Useful principle: cloaks hide ships. They do not hide consequences.

Current basing update: Lass ma einen bauen is now normally based at Der
Himmelskrug’s Starship Garage, often occupying a highly visible
pride-of-place berth. The combat ships Niemand Wird Dich Vermissen, Komm
Her Und Stirb Schnell, and Das Geht Schnell remain at IFP Bastion
Meridian for rapid combat access.



## Der Himmelskrug berthing guidance

- Lass ma einen bauen normally occupies the Starship Garage pride-of-place berth when in port. Its associated utility craft normally remain docked with or aboard the Explorer unless deployed or under service.
- The pride berth has a controlled public-viewable feed for scheduled civic, museum, educational, founding-anniversary, Heinz birthday, or special celebration events. The feed may be delayed, cropped, replaced by approved exterior angles, or disabled for security/maintenance.
- Nur Für Den Fall may use a controlled Starship Garage long-stay cutter berth when safed, declared, and logged. It is not part of the public pride display.
- Heinz’s dedicated combat ships — Niemand Wird Dich Vermissen, Komm Her Und Stirb Schnell, and Das Geht Schnell — are not normally based at Der Himmelskrug.
- Live consumable starship-weapon rearming uses Restricted Works restricted ordnance/rearming facilities, not ordinary Starship Garage berths.

## Weapons and Shield Loadout Reference

Ship weapons now use standardized narrative families and loadout tiers
rather than exact numerical stat blocks. See
`18-weapons-combat-and-force-rules.md` for point-defense lasers, beam
lasers, pulse cannons, disabling beams, electromagnetic disruptors,
grapple/tractor systems, missiles, torpedoes, armor-piercing kinetics,
combat drones, mines/denial charges, shield families, ship loadout tiers,
and force limits.

Current guidance:

- Lass ma einen bauen is a Tier 2+ upgraded Explorer / armed expedition
  platform: reinforced civilian/combat hybrid shield, point defense,
  light beam lasers, disabling beam, electromagnetic disruptor,
  tractor/grapple, strong sensors, salvage/tool systems, and limited
  missile capability only when a story specifically needs it.
- Nur Für Den Fall is a Tier 3 armed expedition cutter: combat-capable
  reinforced shield, point defense, light/heavy beam mix, disabling beam,
  electromagnetic disruptor, tractor/grapple, limited missiles, strong
  sensors, secure comms, and repair/fabrication support.
- Das Geht Schnell is a Tier 4 fast response combat craft: combat shield,
  point defense, beam lasers, pulse cannons, disabling systems, limited
  missiles, and fast intercept sensors.
- Niemand Wird Dich Vermissen is a Tier 5 dedicated strike ship: combat
  shield, cloak, heavy beam lasers, pulse cannons, disabling beam,
  missiles, armor-piercing kinetic option, and high-end fire control.
- Komm Her Und Stirb Schnell is a Tier 6 heavy command fortress: heavy
  layered shields, point-defense grid, heavy beam batteries, pulse cannon
  batteries, missile and torpedo systems, combat drones, command
  coordination, fleet fire-control support, and emergency rescue/support
  capacity.

Core rule: disabling a hostile ship is usually harder than destroying it.
Precision disablement depends on sensor lock, relative position, target
knowledge, shield state, time, traffic safety, and secondary-effect risk.
