# Opening the Stein — Beat Sheet v1

Status: intended canon / draft-only until complete and promoted.

This beat sheet does not create active canon. It is a story-development structure for the official public opening story of Der Himmelskrug.

## Core shape

**Form:** serialized episode in four parts.
**Primary lens:** Feli Brandt.
**Structure:** Feli anchor scenes, limited department cutaways, light media-frame spine.
**Consequence level:** inconvenient but safe, with one public scandal risk that Feli prevents from becoming the headline.
**Ending image:** first public visitors entering through Arrival Control into the Hospitality Level 1 reveal, exhausted staff seeing the miracle hold, a quick exterior media shot, a successful first pour, and a final private Heinz/Feli button.

## Story promise

Der Himmelskrug does not open because nothing goes wrong.

It opens because the people running it catch the problems before the public pays for them.

## Part 1: Three Hours

### Purpose

Introduce the station through Feli's pressure map: public meaning, media framing, staff exhaustion, Heinz's missing speech, Flight Control crowding, and taproom panic.

### Opening image

Feli watches the official countdown clock drop under three hours while receiving too many updates from too many systems.

There are official countdowns on public-facing displays, operations countdowns on staff channels, and one private countdown Feli refuses to name: how long Heinz will pretend the missing speech is a file-management problem instead of an emotional problem.

### Beat 1: Feli sees the whole station before the public does

Feli is in a public-facing coordination space, likely near Hospitality Level 1 or a communications/event operations station, watching media feeds, opening-sequence timing, and staff alerts.

The station is almost ready. It is not calm.

Sensory notes:

- Warm public lighting is already on, but staff channels are sharp with alerts.
- The public areas smell controlled and clean, with brewery warmth held where it belongs.
- Sound is layered: countdown tones, comms pings, distant taproom prep, soft public music not yet released to visitors.

Feli's job in the scene is not to panic. Her job is to know what kind of panic each department is having.

### Beat 2: Flight Control pressure intrudes

A Flight Control update comes through. Opening-day traffic is heavier than planned, not because of danger but because everyone wants to be early, visible, or first.

Amara is calm, courteous, and terrifyingly clear.

Possible line direction:

> “You may be excited after you stop moving.”

Function:

- Establish Flight Control authority.
- Show that public excitement does not modify traffic law.
- Introduce the external scale: ships are waiting outside the stein-shaped station.
- Give media a visual hook without letting media dictate safety.

### Beat 3: The taproom reports the clogged tap

A main taproom alert arrives.

A tap is clogged.

On any other station this would be inconvenient. On Der Himmelskrug, three hours before the first public opening, it sounds spiritually illegal.

Mateo treats it as a guest-flow and hospitality timing problem. Lukas treats it as a process, pressure, quality, and first-pour integrity problem.

Function:

- Introduce the brewery heart of the station.
- Make the problem funny without making the staff foolish.
- Establish that process matters more than spectacle.
- Seed the first-pour payoff.

### Beat 4: Heinz has lost the speech

Heinz appears, or Feli finds him through comms. He is doing something useful and unrelated, which is how Feli knows the speech problem is real.

Possible versions:

- He has lost the final speech file.
- He has found five versions and hates all of them.
- He has the speech but has renamed it something absurdly technical.
- He has accidentally opened an old brewery maintenance note instead.

Feli recognizes the real issue: Heinz is afraid of saying what the station means.

She does not solve it for him yet.

Function:

- Establish Heinz as anxious, useful, funny, and avoidant.
- Establish Feli as emotionally perceptive but not responsible for carrying him.
- Set up later speech repair.

### Beat 5: First media framing

A live pre-opening media segment runs in the background.

The coverage is mixed but fair:

- one feed admires the station's ambition;
- one feed leans hard into “giant beer stein in orbit”;
- one accountability-minded reporter asks who benefits, who governs, and how safety authority works.

Feli does not resent the joke. She resents the possibility that the joke will become the whole story.

Function:

- Establish media as pressure, not villain.
- Show Feli's central conflict: protect the station's meaning without pretending it is not absurd.

### Part 1 turn

Feli receives three updates nearly at once:

1. The Flight Control queue is still growing.
2. The tap is not cleared yet.
3. Heinz's speech situation is worse than he admitted.

Then a fourth message arrives from Residential: Priya is being asked to intervene in a staff housing dispute before it becomes loud enough to matter.

Feli realizes the opening is no longer a countdown. It is a stress test.

## Part 2: Two Hours

### Purpose

Multiply the problems, broaden the station, and show each department solving within its lane.

### Beat 1: Hospitality Level 2 food-prep issue

Cut to Hospitality Level 2.

A food-prep problem appears in a restaurant, food hall station, or colony specialty prep line.

Best type of issue:

- a sauce or garnish is staged on the wrong line;
- a substitution creates an allergen-label mismatch;
- a colony specialty is correctly prepared but incorrectly labeled for general visitors;
- a supplier tag does not match the guest-facing label;
- a dish meant for a restricted dietary line is nearly routed to public service.

The problem is caught before guests are affected.

Function:

- Show food safety seriousness.
- Introduce cultural/colony food variety.
- Show staff under pressure but competent.
- Reinforce that hospitality is a safety system, not just friendliness.

Scene result:

The item is isolated, relabeled, replaced, rerouted, or pulled. Someone is unhappy about timing. Nobody argues with safety.

### Beat 2: Feli receives the H2 update

Feli gets the food-prep update in compressed operational language.

She understands the media version would be “opening day kitchen chaos,” but the truth is better: the system caught the problem.

She may make a note that no public language should turn food safety into a cute blooper.

Function:

- Keep Feli centered.
- Show her translating internal events into truthful public meaning.
- Reinforce that she protects proportion.

### Beat 3: Taproom escalation

Cut to Mateo and Lukas.

The clogged tap is not catastrophic, but it has become symbolic. The first pour is scheduled as a public-facing moment. Cameras may be expecting it.

Mateo worries about timing and guest experience. Lukas refuses to rush a compromised line.

Possible line direction:

> “The first glass is not a hostage.”

They isolate the tap, check pressure, verify the line, and create a backup pour plan if needed.

Function:

- Make the tap problem funny and meaningful.
- Show brewery competence.
- Let Lukas carry family/process authority.
- Prevent Heinz from becoming the fixer.

### Beat 4: Flight Control cutaway

Amara manages impatient ships.

Opening-day traffic includes:

- early tourists;
- partner-colony visitors;
- route crews;
- VIP-adjacent traffic;
- media-cleared vehicles;
- pilots who believe “we're part of the first wave” is a clearance category.

Amara keeps it polite and immovable.

Function:

- Show the exterior scale.
- Give a quick shot of the beer-stein silhouette surrounded by orderly traffic.
- Reinforce that the station is safe because traffic law held.

Media may show a beautiful external shot while Amara is busy preventing that beauty from becoming a collision problem.

### Beat 5: Residential dispute begins

Cut to Residential.

A staff apartment dispute starts over something small but meaningful:

- a quiet-wall promise;
- shift timing and sleep needs;
- a family assignment;
- storage or temporary lodging;
- a misunderstood move-in schedule;
- a staff member feeling displaced by opening-day priorities.

Exhaustion turns it loud.

Priya arrives or is called.

Function:

- Show Residential is restricted, private, and town-like.
- Show fatigue as a real pressure.
- Establish Priya's authority.
- Show the station is already a place where people live, not just perform.

### Beat 6: Feli checks on Heinz

Back to Feli.

Heinz has found a speech, but it is wrong.

Possible wrong speech:

- too technical;
- too long;
- too self-deprecating;
- too full of thanks without meaning;
- too polished in a way that sounds nothing like him;
- a file that starts with a joke and never stops hiding.

Feli does not rewrite it. She asks what he is afraid to say.

He deflects.

She lets him deflect once. Not twice.

### Part 2 turn

A media producer or reporter asks for pre-speech access or a human-interest angle. At almost the same time, Tariq/Teriq sends Feli a private warning:

The guest performer may not be camera-safe.

The near-scandal enters.

## Part 3: One Hour

### Purpose

Bring the pressure to its peak. Feli must prevent the performer issue from becoming the headline while still helping Heinz find the speech's truth.

### Beat 1: Performer near-scandal appears

Tariq/Teriq has noticed the performer is too tipsy.

Not dangerous in a villainous way. Not malicious. But loose, over-charming, emotional, possibly unsteady, and minutes away from being caught by a camera.

The unfair headline is obvious:

> Giant beer station opens with drunk performer.

Function:

- Create the main public scandal risk.
- Let Tariq/Teriq's atmosphere work be operationally meaningful.
- Give Feli a problem that is exactly in her lane: truth, dignity, proportion, and public framing.

### Beat 2: Feli triages the performer issue

Feli coordinates quickly:

- Tariq/Teriq manages event flow and performer access.
- Hospitality provides water, food, quiet, or a delay.
- Medical may quietly advise if impairment matters.
- Security is nearby only if needed, not as humiliation.
- Media access is redirected honestly toward the opening sequence.

Feli does not lie. She does not say the performer is fine if they are not. She reframes without making the person into content.

Possible truthful line:

> “We are adjusting the opening program. The first public arrival is the story. Let's not turn a tired performer into a souvenir.”

Function:

- Feli's major public-facing victory.
- The station protects a person and its own meaning at the same time.
- The story avoids cruelty.

### Beat 3: Residential dispute peaks

Cut to Priya resolving the dispute.

The shouting is not about the apartment anymore. It is about fatigue, fairness, being invisible, and whether opening day matters more than the people who make the station work.

Priya does not sentimentalize it.

She sets a boundary: Residential is not backstage. Residents are not props. Opening day does not erase livability.

Possible line direction:

> “A home is not overflow storage for a miracle.”

Resolution:

- temporary quiet accommodation;
- revised assignment time;
- written follow-up;
- shift-rest protection;
- no public/media exposure.

Function:

- Show Priya as essential.
- Show the station-as-town theme.
- Keep the dispute contained and safe.

### Beat 4: Taproom solution

Mateo and Lukas solve the clogged tap or activate the backup plan.

The fix protects quality.

They may not use the originally planned tap, but the first public pour can still happen honestly.

Optional family legacy touch:

- Lukas repeats a Matthias-style process line.
- Heinz hears about the fix and feels the emotional weight but does not interfere.
- Feli recognizes that to outsiders it is just a tap; to the Schmitts it is family craft under public lights.

Function:

- Set up first-pour payoff.
- Keep brewing meaningful without turning into family backstory.

### Beat 5: Flight Control reaches controlled order

Amara has the queue under control.

Not empty. Not easy. Controlled.

A media feed shows the exterior image: the stein-shaped station, lights coming up, traffic lanes disciplined around it.

A commentator starts to make a joke, then catches the precision of the movement.

Function:

- Visual awe.
- Serious engineering beneath spectacle.
- Public-facing proof that Flight Control held.

### Beat 6: Feli and Heinz speech repair

Feli finds Heinz, or Heinz finds Feli, with time running out.

She has solved enough public problems to see the real one clearly: Heinz is still trying to make the speech useful instead of true.

She refuses to carry his feelings for him.

Possible exchange shape:

- Heinz says the speech is not ready.
- Feli says he is not ready to mean it.
- He argues with process.
- She makes him name who the station is for.
- He starts listing departments, routes, staff, families, pilots, visitors.
- She lets the list become the speech's emotional spine.

She may remove one defensive joke but leave the awkwardness because that is him.

Function:

- Emotional peak before public opening.
- Heinz trusts Feli's judgment.
- Feli does not ghostwrite him.
- The speech becomes awkwardly funny, then sincere.

### Part 3 turn

The countdown reaches the final opening sequence.

Problems are not perfect. They are controlled.

Feli has to step out of problem-solving mode and let the public meet the station.

## Part 4: Zero

### Purpose

Deliver the opening, public reveal, staff relief, first pour, and final Heinz/Feli button.

### Beat 1: Final countdown

The station enters official opening sequence.

Feli watches:

- Arrival Control ready;
- Flight Control green enough to proceed;
- Hospitality ready;
- taproom ready;
- H2 corrected;
- Residential contained;
- performer program adjusted;
- media pool positioned where it is allowed to be;
- Heinz present with a speech he can actually say.

The countdown hits zero.

### Beat 2: Arrival Control releases first public visitors

The first public visitors do not teleport into the taproom. They come through clearance and release properly.

The release into Hospitality Level 1 is controlled, public, and emotional.

Visitor mix:

- families;
- route crews;
- curious tourists;
- workers in transit;
- skeptical regulars;
- partner-colony visitors;
- media-cleared guests;
- people who came for beer, view, route convenience, or curiosity.

Function:

- Teach the station's access logic.
- Make the public opening feel real.
- Show ordinary people, not just VIPs.

### Beat 3: The public reveal

Visitors enter Hospitality Level 1 and see the public heart of Der Himmelskrug.

They react in mixed ordinary ways:

- awe at the atrium;
- laughter because yes, it is a stein;
- practical questions about routes, restrooms, crew recovery, food, lifts, and taproom access;
- a child noticing bubble elevators;
- a tired pilot relaxing because the arrival process made sense;
- a skeptical traveler realizing the place works.

Function:

- Public payoff.
- Moderate world introduction through action.
- The station becomes real because people start using it.

### Beat 4: Heinz's speech

Heinz gives the opening speech.

It begins awkwardly funny. He may make a dry joke about the station not being a mug, not letting anyone appear behind the beer, or the danger of naming files under emotional stress.

Then he finds sincerity.

Speech content should be brief and human:

- thanks the people who built and operate the station;
- names safety and welcome without turning into a manual;
- acknowledges the absurd shape without apologizing for it;
- says the station is not his alone;
- frames Der Himmelskrug as a place where routes, crews, families, workers, visitors, and strangers can arrive safely and be treated like people.

Function:

- Emotional release.
- Heinz owns his words.
- Feli's influence is felt but not visible as ghostwriting.
- The station is defined as promise, not vanity.

### Beat 5: The first pour

The first public pour happens.

Because the taproom protected process, the pour works.

Keep it quick, tactile, and symbolic:

- foam line right;
- glass steady;
- staff watching too carefully;
- Mateo smiling only after the pour settles;
- Lukas noticing the technical correctness before the emotion hits.

Feli sees that this moment matters to Heinz and Lukas even if the media mostly sees the image.

Function:

- Pay off clogged tap.
- Carry family brewing legacy lightly.
- Make the station's heart tangible.

### Beat 6: Media coverage lands

The media frame shifts.

Some coverage still jokes about the stein shape. That is fine.

But the dominant image becomes more complex:

- orderly traffic outside;
- first visitors entering safely;
- staff competence;
- the public reveal;
- Heinz's awkward sincerity;
- the first pour;
- Feli's steady public handling.

The performer issue does not become the headline.

Feli has not hidden the truth. She has protected the proportion of the truth.

Function:

- Feli's public victory.
- Mixed-but-fair media tone confirmed.
- Comic spectacle remains, but with load-bearing truth.

### Beat 7: Staff relief

Behind the public shine, staff exhale.

Possible quick moments:

- Amara allows herself one small breath after the first wave docks cleanly.
- Mateo stops pretending he was never worried.
- Lukas checks the foam line twice.
- Priya receives confirmation Residential is quiet.
- Tariq/Teriq watches the adjusted program work and lets the room feel like celebration.
- H2 staff see guests safely enjoying food that was corrected before service.

Function:

- Honor labor.
- Show the station opening as ensemble achievement.
- Reinforce that warmth rests on work.

### Beat 8: Final Feli/Heinz button

End with Feli as emotional lens and Heinz beside her or in her orbit.

Options:

1. Private look across public noise.
2. A short line from Heinz that is almost a joke but not quite.
3. A one-word slate message from Feli.
4. A small hand touch out of camera view.
5. Feli quietly correcting Heinz's instinct to deflect.

Possible final-button direction:

Feli watches the first visitors stop looking at the station as an object and start moving through it as a place.

Heinz asks, quietly, whether the speech was stupid.

Feli says something like:

> “Parts of it. The right parts.”

Or:

> “No. It sounded like you finally stopped trying to open a station and welcomed people into one.”

Final emotional meaning:

The station stopped being a dream, project, joke, or speech.

It became a place.
