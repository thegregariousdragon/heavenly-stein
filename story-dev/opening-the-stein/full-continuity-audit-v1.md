## Revision issue 1: Repeated key phrasing

Several phrases are effective but may be slightly overused across Parts 1–4:

- `green enough`
- `controlled`
- `held / holding`
- `not perfect`
- `load-bearing`
- `not a prop / not a novelty / not just a joke`
- `truth / proportion / public language`

These are thematically right, but a revision pass should decide which ones are intentional refrains and which ones can be varied.

### Suggested fix

Keep these as deliberate refrains:

- `green enough`
- `hold law / warmth / quiet / quality / labels / room / speech`
- `not perfect; controlled`

Trim or vary these when they cluster too closely:

- `load-bearing`
- `remove shrapnel`
- `not a prop`
- `truth has parts`

## Revision issue 2: Heinz’s speech may be slightly long

The speech works emotionally, but it could likely tighten by 10–15%.

The key material to preserve:

- not a mug joke;
- laughter is allowed;
- stupid-looking answer to a serious question;
- station is not Heinz’s alone;
- rules and welcome are both real;
- welcome to people who arrive tired.

### Suggested fix

Cut one sentence from the “many people built the answer” list or compress it.

Possible trim:

> “Engineers, brewers, controllers, cleaners, cooks, medical staff, residents, inspectors, pilots who complained usefully, partners who argued before signing anything, and workers who told us when the beautiful plan was stupid in practice.”

This keeps breadth while reducing list fatigue.

## Revision issue 3: Part 3 greenroom wording

Part 3 opens with:

> “outside a greenroom that was not green”

This is a funny line, but the project has a specific Green Room / Grünraum concept elsewhere. Even though this is clearly a performance holding room, the word `greenroom` may create avoidable ambiguity.

### Suggested fix

Replace `greenroom` with one of:

- `performer lounge`
- `performance lounge`
- `holding room`
- `performer holding room`

Recommended revision:

> At one hour and seventeen minutes to public opening, Tariq Neumann stood outside a performer lounge, watched the air change shape around a famous person, and decided the room had become more honest than the schedule.

This avoids accidental Green Room / Grünraum collision while preserving the line’s function.

## Revision issue 5: H2 food issue is good but dense

The food-safety sequence is strong, but the opening explanation of the label mismatch is dense. It is correct, but a prose pass could make it easier to follow without weakening safety seriousness.

### Suggested fix

Keep the logic:

- supplier tag says one thing;
- prep sheet says modified batch;
- public label does not match substitute;
- trade name does not map cleanly;
- line server catches smell difference;
- no guest exposure.

But consider breaking the coordinator’s explanation into shorter beats so readers can process it as Feli does.

## Revision issue 6: Part 4 is emotionally satisfying but could use one fewer media line

The media landing works and fulfills the mixed-but-fair media frame. However, Part 4 has three media headline examples plus Starlight commentary. It is useful, but slightly busy after the speech and pour.

### Suggested fix

Keep all three outlets if the goal is to show mixed media framing.

If tightening, cut or compress one lower-third line, likely Civic Meridian, because Galaxy News and Starlight carry stronger contrast.

## Revision issue 7: `Da` line should stay

The correction from `Matthias would say...` to `Da would say...` is the right fix.

It gives Lukas warmth and character while still letting narration name Matthias Schmitt for reader clarity.

### Suggested fix

Keep:

> “Da would say the line losing an argument is still better than beer losing one.”

Then keep the next narration sentence naming Matthias Schmitt.

## Revision issue 8: Part transitions may need smoothing in compiled form

As separate serialized parts, each opening works.

If compiled into one story, the part openings may feel slightly recap-heavy:

- Part 2 begins by reestablishing H1 and media pressure.
- Part 3 begins by jumping to Tariq and the performer.
- Part 4 begins with a status list.

All are functional for serialization.

### Suggested fix

If the story remains serialized, leave them.

If compiled into one long chapter, reduce recap at the top of Parts 2–4 and let the transitions carry more momentum.
