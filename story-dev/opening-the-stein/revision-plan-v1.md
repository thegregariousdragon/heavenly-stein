# Opening the Stein — Revision Plan v1

Status: draft-development planning. No canon promotion.

This plan follows `full-continuity-audit-v1.md` and prepares the actual prose revision across Parts 1–4.

## Revision goal

Produce a cleaner revised story draft that preserves the current continuity wins while improving rhythm, density, and canon triage.

The story should still feel like:

- absurd Bavarian orbital beer-stein premise treated as real civic infrastructure;
- warm comedy with consequences;
- public opening as ensemble labor;
- Feli as emotional/public-language center;
- Heinz as awkward founder who finally means the speech;
- Arrival Control, Flight Control, Hospitality, Residential, H2, Taproom, Entertainment, Medical, and media all remaining in their lanes.

## Non-negotiables to preserve

- Public visitors enter through Arrival Control, not direct transport into H1 or the taproom.
- Press stays on assigned marks and approved feeds.
- Flight Control authority stands.
- Residential remains private and not backstage.
- H2 food issue remains a serious safety catch before guest exposure.
- Taproom process protects first-pour meaning.
- Performer dignity is protected without lying.
- Feli does not ghostwrite Heinz or carry his feelings.
- Heinz’s speech remains awkwardly funny, then sincere.
- `Tariq Neumann` appears on first use; `Tariq` afterward.
- Lukas’s family line stays `Da would say...` unless later canon establishes a different family address style.

## Required revision fixes

### 1. Avoid Green Room / Grünraum ambiguity

Replace Part 3 opening wording:

> outside a greenroom that was not green

Recommended replacement:

> outside a performer lounge

or:

> outside a performer holding room

Preferred: `performer lounge`, because it is compact and avoids accidental conflict with the project-specific Green Room / Grünraum.

### 2. Smooth repeated phrases

Keep as intentional refrains:

- `green enough`
- the final `holding` cadence
- `not perfect; controlled` as a thematic idea

Vary or reduce:

- `load-bearing`
- `remove shrapnel`
- repeated `truth` phrasing
- repeated `not a prop / not a novelty / not only a joke`
- dense clusters of `not X / not Y`

### 3. Tighten H2 food explanation

Preserve all safety logic but break the first explanation into easier beats.

Keep:

- supplier tag mismatch;
- prep sheet modified batch;
- public label mismatch;
- trade name not mapping cleanly;
- Naya catching smell difference;
- no guest exposure;
- item isolated / corrected / relabeled.

### 4. Trim Heinz’s speech by 10–15%

Preserve:

- not-a-mug joke;
- laughter allowed;
- stupid-looking answer to serious question;
- not mine / not alone;
- rules and welcome are real;
- welcome to tired arrivals and workers.

Likely trim area:

- the long list of people who built the station.

### 5. Decide media density in Part 4

Options:

1. Keep all three outlets for mixed-but-fair media proof.
2. Compress Civic Meridian lower-third because Starlight + Galaxy News carry stronger contrast.
3. Keep the headlines but shorten surrounding narration.

Recommended default: option 3.
