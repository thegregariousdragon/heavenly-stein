## Premise

Three hours before Der Himmelskrug opens to the public for the first time, the staff is exhausted, proud, and fraying at the edges. A series of minor mishaps across Hospitality Level 2, the main taproom, Flight Control, Residential, and opening-day festivities slowly ratchets up pressure until the countdown hits zero.

The station opens successfully, the visitors react with awe, laughter, practical questions, and trust, and live media coverage frames Der Himmelskrug as both absurd spectacle and serious civic infrastructure.

## Core story mode

Ridiculous premise, serious engineering underneath.

The story should introduce the world through pressure rather than exposition. The public sees a beer-stein-shaped orbital station. Feli sees the civic promise, the media risk, the staff labor, and the emotional truth of letting the station become real in public.

## Finished form

Serialized episode, broken into 3 to 5 scenes or chapters.

Recommended structure: 4 parts.

### Part 1: Three Hours

Feli anchors the opening pressure. Heinz's speech problem appears. Flight Control, Hospitality, taproom, and media pressure are introduced.

### Part 2: Two Hours

Small problems multiply. Food prep goes wrong on Hospitality Level 2, the main taproom tap clogs, Flight Control manages the arrival queue, and Residential tension begins.

### Part 3: One Hour

The performer near-scandal forms. The Residential dispute peaks. Feli coordinates with Tariq/Teriq, Priya, Hospitality, and media handling while helping Heinz find the real shape of the speech.

### Part 4: Zero

Flight Control clears the opening wave. Arrival Control releases the first public visitors. Heinz gives the speech. The public reacts, the staff exhales, and the final emotional button belongs to Heinz and Feli through Feli's lens.

## Emotional center

Feli Brandt carries the emotional center of the story.

Function: Feli is the reader's main anchor for what the opening means publicly, emotionally, and institutionally. She tracks the station's public language, media risk, staff dignity, visitor trust, and Heinz's avoidance patterns.

Heinz remains central as founder and public face, but he is not the sole fixer. His missing speech is a comic and emotional pressure point. He can do his thing — engineering logic, dry panic, practical competence, avoidance by usefulness — while Feli understands what his behavior means.

The opening becomes less about whether Heinz can personally hold the station together and more about whether Feli can help the station speak honestly as it becomes real in public.

## Reader orientation

Moderate explanation, supported by media-assisted and character-guided orientation.

The story should introduce enough of Der Himmelskrug for new readers to understand the opening-day pressure, but most explanation should arrive through action, public signage, staff communications, media commentary, visitor reactions, and Feli's perspective.

Use:

- Arrival Control and public release to show how visitors enter.
- Flight Control comms to show station traffic authority.
- Hospitality Level 1 reveal to orient readers to the public station experience.
- Hospitality Level 2 food/taproom issues to show hospitality operations.
- Residential dispute to show the station is also a town, not just a venue.
- Media coverage to explain the external public framing.
- Feli's observations to connect systems, people, and public meaning.

Avoid:

- Long lore dumps.
- Explaining every station level in one block.
- Making characters tell each other things they already know.
- Letting media commentary replace lived scene experience.

## Media tone

Mixed but fair, with a strong risk of comic spectacle.

The media coverage should recognize that Der Himmelskrug is genuinely impressive and newsworthy, while also being tempted by the easy image: a giant Bavarian beer stein in orbit.

Coverage style:

- Some anchors and feeds emphasize awe, first arrivals, hospitality, and civic promise.
- Some coverage leans into jokes about beer, the stein shape, and Heinz as founder.
- Accountability-minded reporters ask fair questions about safety, governance, labor, traffic control, public intoxication, and whether the station is more than one rich engineer's vanity project.
- Feli's job is not to kill the joke. Her job is to make sure the joke has load-bearing truth under it.

Do not make the media cartoonishly hostile. Do not let media physically enter restricted areas for a better shot. Do not let public excitement erase staff privacy, Residential boundaries, or Flight Control authority.

## Consequence level

Inconvenient but safe, with one public scandal risk that Feli prevents from becoming the headline.

Function: the story should show real operational pressure without turning opening day into disaster. Problems are caught, routed, corrected, softened, or honestly reframed before anyone is harmed.

Pressure scale:

- Food prep problem: inconvenient, corrected before guest impact.
- Clogged tap: embarrassing and time-sensitive, solved by taproom/brewery staff.
- Flight Control congestion: tense but professionally managed.
- Residential dispute: private, emotionally messy, contained by Priya before it becomes public content.
- Tipsy performer: main public scandal risk.
- Missing speech: comic/emotional pressure, not operational danger.

Safety boundary:

- No serious injuries.
- No major docking incident.
- No public stampede.
- No food-allergy harm.
- No uncontrolled intoxication in a safety-sensitive role.
- No breach of Residential privacy.

Emotional result: the station does not open perfectly. It opens honestly, safely, and humanly.

## Feli and Heinz relationship beat

Feli refuses to carry Heinz's feelings for him, Heinz trusts her judgment, and the story ends with a tiny romantic/private button.

Function: Feli can help Heinz find the real shape of the speech, but she will not perform his vulnerability for him. Heinz must trust her public judgment and then own his own words.

Story use:

- Feli sees that Heinz is hiding inside speech logistics.
- She cuts through the missing-file problem to the real problem: he is afraid of naming what the station means.
- Heinz accepts her read instead of arguing, deflecting, or trying to solve another department's issue.
- During the opening, he lets her manage the public/media frame.
- After the station opens, they share a small private moment that confirms trust, relief, and affection without becoming a proposal-level scene.

Do not make Feli ghostwrite his feelings. Do not make Heinz helpless without her. Do not make the romance eclipse the station opening.

## Heinz opening speech

Awkwardly funny, then sincere, with Feli helping him find the real emotional shape of it.

Function: the missing speech begins as a comic logistics problem but becomes an emotional pressure point. Heinz has prepared words, lost them, found the wrong version, or found too many versions, but the real issue is that he is frightened of saying what Der Himmelskrug means out loud.

Feli helps him identify the truth of the speech without replacing his voice. She may cut away polish, remove defensive jokes, or ask the question that makes him stop treating the opening like an engineering handoff.

Speech effect: Heinz starts awkwardly or dryly funny, then becomes sincere enough to let the public see the station as a promise rather than a vanity project.

Do not make Heinz suddenly smooth. Do not make Feli ghostwrite his feelings. Do not let the speech become a lore dump. Do not have Heinz take credit for everyone's work.

## Performer problem

The guest performer is too tipsy and about to go on camera. The emerging media angle is unfairly harsh because the performer is nervous, exhausted, and not malicious.

Function: this is the story's main public scandal risk. Feli prevents it from becoming the headline by moving quickly, coordinating with Tariq/Teriq, protecting the performer's dignity, and reframing the opening coverage toward the actual public event.

Likely scene use:

- Tariq/Teriq notices the performer's timing, balance, emotional looseness, or over-charm before the cameras fully commit.
- Feli recognizes the headline risk immediately.
- Medical may be consulted quietly if impairment affects safety.
- Hospitality or event staff substitute, delay, rearrange, or convert the performance into something safer.
- The media does not need to be villainous; they may simply be following the easiest visible story.
- Feli's intervention should be honest, not a cover-up.

Do not humiliate the performer for comedy. Do not let the performer enter a safety-sensitive role while impaired. Do not make intoxication consequence-free. Do not make Feli lie to the media. Do not turn the performer into the defining story of opening day.

## Apartment dispute

The apartment dispute reveals that Residential privacy matters, fatigue is making people brittle, Priya is essential, and the station is already becoming a town.

Function: the dispute should show that staff housing is not backstage for public spectacle. Even on opening day, Residential is a lived-in restricted space with real needs, fairness concerns, family pressures, quiet requirements, and emotional limits.

Scene use:

- The dispute starts over something small but meaningful: assignment timing, room readiness, quiet access, family needs, shift timing, storage, or a misunderstood promise.
- Exhaustion turns a solvable issue into a shouting match.
- Priya intervenes with calm authority and practical compassion.
- The scene shows Residential Life as operationally essential, not decorative care work.
- The dispute is contained privately and should not become media content.

Do not make Residential publicly accessible. Do not make the dispute a tourist spectacle. Do not make Priya merely nice; she has authority. Do not create a major policy gap unless the story intentionally wants a future governance thread. Do not let opening-day excitement override residents' right to livable space.

## Food-prep problem

The Hospitality Level 2 food-prep problem reveals food safety seriousness, opening-day pressure, hospitality competence, and cultural/colony food variety.

Function: the problem should show that food service on Der Himmelskrug is part of safety culture, not just atmosphere. Labels, allergens, substitutions, route-specific ingredients, colony food traditions, and staff process all matter.

Scene use:

- A dish, sauce, garnish, packaged ingredient, or colony specialty is mislabeled, delayed, substituted, cross-staged, or nearly sent to the wrong service line.
- The issue is caught before guests are affected.
- Staff are rushed and tired, but the correction process works.
- The scene can introduce Hospitality Level 2's food hall or restaurant variety across partner colonies and routes.
- The fix shows competence: isolate, verify, relabel, replace, reroute, or temporarily pull the item.
- The problem may be funny in presentation, but not careless in consequence.

Do not cause actual guest harm. Do not make allergen or label safety a harmless gag. Do not make food staff look incompetent. Do not imply supplier documentation alone is enough if front-line staff and guests need usable information. Do not turn cultural food variety into caricature.

## Clogged tap

The clogged main taproom tap reveals brewery competence, symbolic pressure, comic panic, process over spectacle, and family/brewing legacy.

Function: the clogged tap should be a small technical problem with huge emotional weight. It threatens the first-pour image, but the taproom and brewery staff solve it by respecting process rather than forcing spectacle.

Scene use:

- Mateo treats it as a hospitality timing problem.
- Lukas treats it as a brewing/process problem.
- Staff around them may react as if the station's soul has lodged itself in a valve.
- The fix is competent: isolate the line, check pressure, protect quality, reroute service, verify the pour, and refuse to serve a bad first glass just because cameras are waiting.
- The moment can lightly evoke Schmitt brewing legacy through Lukas's standards, Matthias's influence, or a remembered process principle.
- The first pour later works because staff chose correctness over panic.

Do not make the taproom team incompetent. Do not serve bad beer for the sake of the countdown. Do not let the first pour become more important than guest safety or quality. Do not over-lore the Schmitt family brewing tradition in this scene. Do not make Heinz personally solve the tap unless the scene needs him only as comic interference.

## Opening scene

Open on Feli with the countdown clock, then quickly let Flight Control pressure, the clogged main taproom tap, and Heinz's missing speech intrude.

Function: the reader meets Der Himmelskrug through Feli's awareness of public meaning and operational pressure. The station is almost ready, but not quiet. Every system is technically doing its job; every person is one small problem away from saying something regrettable.

Opening ingredients:

- Countdown clock under three hours.
- Feli tracking media language, public messaging, and opening sequence timing.
- Flight Control queue pressure audible through comms, alerts, or Amara's controlled updates.
- Main taproom clogged tap reported as a small crisis with ridiculous emotional weight.
- Heinz either cannot find the speech, has found the wrong speech, or has too many versions and no confidence in any of them.

Opening effect: the story begins with comedy, pressure, and competence all at once. Feli is not watching chaos; she is watching a station prove whether its systems can absorb stress before the public ever sees it.
