# Opportunity Knocks — Beat Sheet v1

Status: draft-only story-development beat sheet.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology. It should be read with `intake-v1.md` and `candidate-canon-v1.md`.

## Core story shape

Sven Andersen, Freya Andersen, Nora Andersen, and Ingrid Andersen arrive at Der Himmelskrug aboard *Last Good Haul* with the legendary racing ship *Opportunity Knocks* in the freighter's docking bay. A disputed lien and Mirador pressure make the Himmelskrug Run a lawful path out of bankruptcy and coercive seizure.

## Beat 1: Boarding Last Good Haul

Location: Kestrel Minor, Andersen family berth or small-hauler dock.

The Andersen family boards *Last Good Haul*. Sven checks the freighter and then *Opportunity Knocks*. Freya checks documents, notices, costs, and remaining money. Ingrid watches the racing ship with grief and pride. Nora knows the ship matters, but not why everyone is afraid.

Emotional beat: the family is not going on an adventure. They are taking one last chance.

## Beat 2: Mirador's tracker

Location: Kestrel Minor dock exterior, service corridor, or nearby departure zone.

Callum Dresk and Ilya Voren confirm that the tracker planted on *Last Good Haul* is active. They intend to follow Sven, isolate him, and force surrender or signature under Northstar's disputed lien claim before legal review can catch up.

Emotional beat: predation disguised as paperwork.

## Beat 3: Himmelskrug Run announcement prep

Location: Der Himmelskrug, Crown Admin / Civic area, with Flight Control input.

Der Himmelskrug finalizes the Himmelskrug Run announcement. The Board-approved prize structure is confirmed. Sponsors and media rights support the purse, but Flight Control remains the authority over the race.

Emotional beat: the station is joyful, but the joy has paperwork and traffic authority.

## Beat 4: Public announcement

Location: public feed from Der Himmelskrug, station displays, external media feeds.

Heinz and the Governing Board jointly announce the Himmelskrug Run, its purse, and the Knotward Run course. Across the route network, racing fans react. On *Last Good Haul*, Freya hears the number and starts calculating.

Emotional beat: a public festival announcement becomes one family's possible lifeline.

## Beat 5: Freya sees the chance

Location: aboard *Last Good Haul*, en route to Der Himmelskrug.

Freya realizes first prize would clear the family debt and leave enough to start over. Sven resists because the ship needs repairs, he is publicly unproven, and the Andersen name is heavy. Freya pushes back: shame is not a plan.

Emotional beat: Freya refuses to let Sven confuse shame with strategy.

## Beat 6: Mirador adapts

Location: commercial transport route or Mirador staging point.

Callum and Ilya hear the same announcement. If Sven wins, Northstar loses leverage. They book passage to Der Himmelskrug under false identities.

Emotional beat: hope creates motion; predators move too.

## Beat 7: Approach to Der Himmelskrug

Location: approach zone near Der Himmelskrug.

*Last Good Haul* approaches the station. Sven declares the freighter's faults and the stored racing craft. He does not lie to Flight Control. Flight Control assigns cautious routing.

Emotional beat: Sven is broke and scared, but he is not dishonest.

## Beat 8: Commercial passenger arrival

Location: Arrival Control, then public station levels.

Callum and Ilya arrive by commercial transport using false identities. They clear ordinary Arrival Control and enter public station areas. They do not have free access to Services, Starship Garage, Restricted Works, Residential, or Command.

Emotional beat: Der Himmelskrug is safe, not omniscient.

## Beat 9: Garage intake and reveal

Location: Starship Garage intake, with controlled handling from the freighter.

*Opportunity Knocks* is unloaded from *Last Good Haul*. Garage staff recognize her. The intake briefly fills with barely contained ship-nerd awe: whispered race memories, someone muttering that she is not a replica, and at least one staffer trying very hard not to grin.

Oskar Teague shuts down gawking and turns the moment into intake. Fame does not replace inspection.

Possible Oskar line:

> If anyone drools on my diagnostic pad, they clean the coolant trench.

Emotional beat: a legend arrives on handling pads and still has to pass diagnostics.

## Beat 10: Heinz hears what is in the garage

Location: Crown / staff comms / race administration route.

Senior staff quietly notify leadership that *Opportunity Knocks* is in the garage. Heinz freezes, then tries very hard to act like a dignified station founder. Feli or Rolf may quietly note that he is gripping the table.

Heinz badly wants to see the ship, but he does not override protocol. He lets the right people work.

Possible Heinz line:

> I am being very normal about this.

Emotional beat: Heinz's restraint shows that the station is bigger than its founder's enthusiasm.

## Beat 11: First public station experience

Location: Hospitality levels or other public areas.

The family gets a modest meal and sees public station life. Nora reacts to the station's warmth and scale. Ingrid begins to see it as more than another port. Freya notices how survival is organized.

Emotional beat: the station offers ordinary dignity before it offers rescue.

## Beat 12: Race registration

Location: race registration point.

Sven registers *Opportunity Knocks* for the Himmelskrug Run. The paperwork confirms inspection conditions and Flight Control authority. Heinz meets Sven and treats him like a pilot who deserves process, not charity.

Heinz briefly lets the ship-nerd joy leak through, catches Sven's discomfort, and pulls himself back. He makes clear that the station will treat *Opportunity Knocks* as a registered craft under garage protocol, not as a trophy.

Possible Heinz line:

> I will now say one historically undignified thing, and then we will return to paperwork.

Emotional beat: Sven is seen without being exposed.

## Beat 13: The repair problem

Location: Starship Garage diagnostic bay.

Sven says *Opportunity Knocks* is "answering late." Ordinary diagnostics make the issue look like age, tolerance drift, or pilot anxiety. Oskar does not dismiss him. Sven and Oskar reproduce the issue: old frame resonance feeding tiny control lag into the pilot-feedback interface under specific high-load race profiles.

Emotional beat: Sven's family knowledge becomes evidence.

## Beat 14: Rolf confirms

Location: Starship Garage / engineering diagnostic station.

Oskar escalates the issue to Rolf. Rolf confirms that the resonance / feedback lag could become dangerous during Kaltmond's gravity-assisted slingshot, the Twins' Teeth pitch/yaw/roll passage, or the repeated corrections of The Gauntlet. The repair plan changes. Sven collaborates with the team.

Emotional beat: Sven earns respect before he earns applause.

## Beat 15: Mirador closes in

Location: public station levels and controlled-route edges.

Callum and Ilya track Sven's public movement and infer his route toward pilot / crew support. They prepare to corner him before he can stabilize his position.

Emotional beat: the station's warmth is not weakness.

## Beat 16: Threat near Services

Location: pilot / crew waiting area near Services.

Callum and Ilya confront Sven and invoke Northstar's lien claim. They use intimidation, legal pressure, and blocked movement to push him toward surrendering or signing over *Opportunity Knocks*.

Freya recognizes the paperwork language. Calder Yim notices the access irregularity and the way the agents are controlling the space. Pia Stern notices the coercive tone, blocked movement, and threat pattern. Calder starts the report chain. Pia corroborates the intimidation and safety threat.

Emotional beat: private debt becomes public danger.

## Beat 17: Security response

Location: Services-adjacent routes, Arrival Control exits, public movement nodes.

Security moves the Andersens to safety and locks down relevant controlled routes. Arrival Control and Flight Control are alerted as needed. Security reviews entry data, credential mismatches, route logs, witness statements, and sensor records. The hidden tracker becomes evidence.

Emotional beat: Der Himmelskrug's kindness has teeth because the systems are real.

## Beat 18: Family support

Location: Crew Support lodging, family-safe rest area, or controlled temporary quarters.

The Andersens are not sent back to *Last Good Haul*. They receive temporary lodging, privacy protection, Security support, and check-ins as needed. Nora is protected from public exposure. Ingrid begins functioning as the family's home anchor in the new environment.

Emotional beat: relief can feel humiliating before it feels safe.

## Beat 19: Agents detained

Location: controlled station route, attempted exit path, or transit point.

Security tracks Callum and Ilya through access records, route logs, and witness reports. The agents are detained before they can leave the station or reach controlled ship areas. The incident becomes serious enough for IFP handoff.

Emotional beat: the predators thought paperwork made them untouchable. It made evidence.

## Beat 20: Repair days

Location: Starship Garage and family support spaces.

Over several days, Sven works with Oskar and the garage team to repair and retune *Opportunity Knocks*. Freya handles legal updates, financial records, and family needs. Ingrid helps Nora settle and privately keeps the family racing history alive.

The garage levity continues in controlled doses: staff remain professional, but quiet awe follows *Opportunity Knocks* through the repair bay. Oskar keeps reminding everyone that legends still need torque checks.

Emotional beat: the ship is not restored by nostalgia. It is restored by labor.

## Beat 21: Race clearance

Location: Starship Garage / Flight Control race systems.

*Opportunity Knocks* passes race inspection. Flight Control confirms race status. Sven receives clearance not as a favor, but because the ship, pilot, and repair work meet the requirements.

Emotional beat: permission matters because it was earned.

## Beat 22: Pre-race public framing

Location: public broadcast, station viewing areas, family-safe viewing space.

The race broadcast uses rotating feed voices. Ren Calder is the primary Himmelskrug Run feed host. Maude Kess is the retired racer voice. Ren keeps the public oriented. Maude recognizes racing lines, ship behavior, and the Andersen legacy.

Commentators recognize *Opportunity Knocks* but question Sven. Freya, Nora, and Ingrid watch from a protected family-safe space.

Emotional beat: the world knows the name. It does not yet know Sven.

## Beat 23: Race start

Location: Knotward Run course around Der Himmelskrug.

The Himmelskrug Run begins. Sven starts conservatively, testing the repaired response of *Opportunity Knocks*. Some commentators mistake this for fear or overcaution. Maude understands he is listening to the ship.

Emotional beat: Sven is not timid. He is listening.

## Beat 24: Opening chicanes

Location: early Knotward Run course.

The opening chicanes establish the race rhythm. Sven stays legal, avoids unnecessary duels, and feels for the repaired response before committing to the more dangerous course features.

Emotional beat: control comes before spectacle.

## Beat 25: High-speed Kaltmond slingshot

Location: Kaltmond gravity corridor.

Sven commits to a high-speed gravity-assisted slingshot around Kaltmond. The maneuver requires entering the moon's legal gravity corridor at a precise angle. Too shallow and he loses the speed boost. Too wide and Flight Control disqualifies him for leaving the corridor. Too deep and the barren moon becomes a wall.

*Opportunity Knocks* answers cleanly, and Kaltmond flings Sven toward the exit chicane with more speed than the field expects.

Possible Maude line:

> Too shallow, he loses the boost. Too wide, Flight Control throws the flag. Too deep, Kaltmond keeps him.

Emotional beat: the old legend flickers awake in a new pilot's hands.

## Beat 26: Kaltmond exit chicane

Location: Kaltmond exit corridor.

Sven must convert the slingshot's speed into a legal exit line. The exit chicane punishes pilots who leave Kaltmond too hot, too wide, or too proud to bleed a little speed. Sven carries the advantage without losing the setup for the Twins' Teeth.

Emotional beat: speed only matters if he can keep using it.

## Beat 27: The Twins' Teeth

Location: asteroid gate on the Knotward Run.

Sven aims for the Twins' Teeth. The two asteroids do not form a straight slot. The legal line is an angled, offset gap that requires coordinated pitch, yaw, and roll so *Opportunity Knocks* presents her narrowest safe profile while carrying speed.

Most pilots avoid the gate or slow enough to lose time. Sven trusts the repaired response and threads the gap as a shape problem, not a straight-line dare.

Possible Ren line:

> That is not a straight gap. Viewers, watch the attitude change. He has to pitch, yaw, and roll the ship through the line, not merely fly between the rocks.

Possible Maude line:

> He is showing the Teeth her narrow side.

Emotional beat: Sven stops being only Lars's son.

## Beat 28: Teeth-to-Gauntlet chicane

Location: course segment between the Twins' Teeth and The Gauntlet.

After the asteroid gate, Sven has to recover angle before entering The Gauntlet. The chicane prevents the Twins' Teeth from becoming a simple slingshot into the next feature. A pilot who exits the Teeth badly enters The Gauntlet compromised.

Emotional beat: survival is not enough. The next line is already asking a question.

## Beat 29: The Gauntlet

Location: sanctioned high-precision corridor on the Knotward Run.

The Gauntlet is a dense, Flight-Control-monitored corridor of rapid gates, tight turns, sensor-shadow pockets, and course pylons. It is legal, famous, and hated because it punishes sloppy handling after pilots are already tired and overcommitted.

Sven has to make repeated pitch, yaw, roll, and thrust corrections under load. The repaired frame resonance does not return. *Opportunity Knocks* answers fast enough to make the impossible look practiced.

Possible Ren line:

> He carried Kaltmond speed through the Teeth, and now he has to carry precision through The Gauntlet.

Emotional beat: the repair holds under sustained pressure.

## Beat 30: Final acceleration recovery

Location: final Knotward Run approach corridor.

After The Gauntlet, *Opportunity Knocks* needs to recover acceleration quickly. The ship answers cleanly. Sven takes the lead or extends it, but the final approach still requires composure.

Emotional beat: the ship answered him.

## Beat 31: The Last Pour

Location: final kink before the finish corridor.

The Last Pour is the named final kink heading toward the finish line. It looks simple compared with Kaltmond, the Twins' Teeth, and The Gauntlet, but it punishes pilots who arrive with too much speed, too little patience, or a bad exit angle.

Sven takes The Last Pour cleanly and enters the finish corridor.

Possible Ren line:

> Andersen is through The Last Pour and into the finish corridor.

Emotional beat: the final test is not bravery. It is not spilling the victory at the lip of the stein.

## Beat 32: Finish

Location: race finish corridor, Flight Control feeds, public viewing areas, Starship Garage, protected family viewing space.

Sven crosses the finish first. Flight Control confirms the legal finish. Public celebration erupts. Garage staff react with relief as much as joy. Freya and Ingrid break down in different ways. Nora understands that her father won, even if she does not yet understand what was saved. Heinz is delighted, but the moment stays Sven's and the family's.

The story does not resolve every administrative consequence immediately. The race gives emotional release. The final chapter comes weeks later.

Emotional beat: the family gets to breathe.

## Beat 33: Prize processing and debt resolution

Timing: weeks later.

Location: private administrative setting, family lodging, or station legal / financial office.

The first-place prize has been processed. The Andersen debt is paid off and documented. Freya makes sure the records are correct and that Northstar cannot pretend payment, lien status, or ownership boundaries are unclear. The disputed lien is resolved enough to protect *Opportunity Knocks*.

Emotional beat: the family does not just win. They become legally free.

## Beat 34: IFP transfer and Northstar investigation

Timing: weeks later, or summarized during the final chapter.

Location: private administrative setting, family lodging, or station legal / financial office.

Callum Dresk and Ilya Voren are transferred to IFP custody. Evidence includes false identities, tracker use, intimidation, disputed lien abuse, station access misconduct, and witness statements from Calder Yim and Pia Stern.

Northstar Collateral Services comes under investigation for lien abuse, predatory refinancing, and possible coordination with coercive seizure tactics. Mirador's wider operation remains unresolved.

Emotional beat: justice begins; it does not pretend to be finished.

## Beat 35: Public story by consent

Timing: weeks later, after the family sets boundaries.

Sven and Freya decide what the family will share. Feli shapes a blended statement that protects the family's dignity, celebrates the race, credits station procedure, and acknowledges the investigation without trying the case in public.

Ingrid gives the brief Lars quote, with family approval:

> Lars never taught Sven that *Opportunity Knocks* belonged to the fastest hand. He taught him she belonged to the hand that listened.

Emotional beat: the family owns its story.

## Beat 36: Job offer finalized

Timing: weeks later.

Sven is offered a probationary senior-track Starship Garage engineering role specializing in high-performance craft diagnostics. The offer is based on repair collaboration, safety-critical diagnosis, respect for station inspection standards, race conduct, Oskar's support, and Rolf's confirmation.

Sven does not accept as if alone. He brings the offer to Freya and Ingrid.

Emotional beat: the job is not rescue by pity. It is recognition.

## Beat 37: Full relocation

Timing: weeks later.

Sven, Freya, Nora, and Ingrid relocate fully to Der Himmelskrug. Sven begins the practical steps toward his new role. Nora starts or begins intake for the Learning Center. Ingrid becomes the family's home anchor. Freya does not immediately take a station job; her ending focuses on safety, legal cleanup, recovery, and options.

Emotional beat: the family's future is not only employment. It is safety, time, and choice.

## Beat 38: Last Good Haul's next life

Timing: weeks later.

*Last Good Haul* is repaired enough to be safe, honest, and useful. The Andersens sell it at a deliberately reasonable cost to Tamsin Orr, a nonbinary parent from Brindlehook Junction, and Tamsin's teenage child Keo Orr.

Tamsin and Keo rename the ship **Second Breakfast**. The Andersens refuse to pass on the same kind of burden that nearly crushed them.

Emotional beat: they let go without becoming what hurt them.

## Beat 39: Opportunity Knocks receives her berth

Timing: weeks later.

*Opportunity Knocks* receives a protected Starship Garage berth. She remains private Andersen family property. Physical access is controlled. A public-viewable feed or scheduled display may exist only by family consent.

Emotional beat: the ship is no longer collateral. She is home.

## Beat 40: Sven's first official shift

Timing: weeks later, final braided sequence.

Sven reports for his first official Starship Garage shift. He is not treated as a miracle hero forever. He puts on work gear, follows procedure, and joins the maintenance life of the station.

Emotional beat: the future is earned one shift at a time.

## Beat 41: Final braided image

Timing: final pages or final scene sequence.

First, **Second Breakfast** departs Der Himmelskrug with Tamsin and Keo Orr. The former *Last Good Haul* leaves under a new name, with a new family crew, free of the predatory burden the Andersens refused to pass on.

Second, Sven begins his first official Starship Garage shift.

Third, Nora sees *Opportunity Knocks* resting in her protected berth. The ship is safe, private, and still alive.

Nora is not promised to the ship. She is not forced into destiny. The legacy waits as a choice.

Possible final line:

> *Opportunity Knocks* waited in her berth, not retired and not claimed, listening to the station breathe around her. Someday, Nora might ask what it felt like to fly her. Someday, Sven might answer. For now, the ship rested, and the family did too.

Emotional beat: the legacy survives because it becomes a choice.
