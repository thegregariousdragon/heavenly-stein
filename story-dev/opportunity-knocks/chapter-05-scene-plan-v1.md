# Opportunity Knocks — Chapter 5 Scene Plan v1

Status: draft-only planning.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology.

## Chapter 5: The Legend on Handling Pads

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 5 introduces *Opportunity Knocks* to Der Himmelskrug's Starship Garage without turning the moment into public spectacle. The ship is famous enough that garage staff briefly fail to be normal, but the station's clean-side handling rules still govern everything. Oskar Teague establishes the chapter's key principle: awe is allowed after the ship is safe, logged, and inspected.

## Scene 1: No-Power Unload

Location: Starship Garage clean-side intake berth.

*Last Good Haul* is held at the clean-side garage-adjacent berth. Garage Ops prepares a passive tow cradle, handling pads, scan lanes, and no-power inspection route. Sven is present as shipmaster and owner-representative, but he does not command the station team. Freya, Ingrid, and Nora are routed toward crew/passenger clearance or a controlled family waiting area rather than the garage floor.

Function: show that Sven's honest declaration produced a careful process.

Ending turn: the cargo bay opens under Garage Ops authority, not family drama.

## Scene 2: The Name Reaches the Floor

Location: Starship Garage intake lane.

The sealed manifest reaches the correct garage staff. The ship's identity becomes known inside the controlled team: *Opportunity Knocks*. A few techs recognize the name immediately. One knows the old race stats. Another tries to pretend they are not staring. Someone almost says something unprofessional and stops.

Function: reveal the ship's reputation through people who care about ships.

Ending turn: the ship appears on handling pads.

## Scene 3: Oskar Says "Show Me"

Location: same intake berth.

Oskar Teague arrives or steps forward from the intake team. He notices the awe and shuts down the drift without mocking it. He recognizes the ship, but refuses to let fame replace inspection. He asks Sven for the fault in operational terms and listens when Sven says she is answering late.

Function: introduce Oskar's authority, competence, and evidence-driven respect.

Ending turn: Oskar orders diagnostics based on Sven's declaration rather than the ship's legend.

## Scene 4: Controlled Fan Failure

Location: intake berth / diagnostic staging.

Garage staff perform safed diagnostics, scan cradle restraints, verify clean hull status, and prepare no-power tow/inspection. While working, they show small, controlled fan reactions: whispered old race names, someone grinning at the stripe, someone nearly touching the hull and catching themselves, Oskar threatening coolant-trench cleanup if anyone drools on diagnostic equipment.

Function: warmth and levity without breaking safety process.

Ending turn: the team finds the first signs that Sven's concern deserves deeper investigation.

## Scene 5: The Owner Who Told the Truth

Location: diagnostic station beside the handling pads.

Oskar asks Sven why he declared a fault that ordinary intake might not have caught. Sven admits he felt it and could not prove it. Oskar respects that. Freya's safety-gate list may be referenced through Sven's slate or a family note, showing that this was deliberate, not panic.

Function: begin Sven earning credibility with the garage.

Ending turn: Oskar tells Sven the garage will test the ship as a machine, not worship her as a story.

## Scene 6: Quiet Notification

Location: Garage Ops internal channel / Oskar's station.

Oskar sends a controlled internal notification up the proper chain: Rolf Petersen for Engineering risk, Flight Control for race implications, Port Services/Crew Support for family handling, and possibly Heinz/Feli only because the ship's public reputation and privacy risk require leadership awareness. The message is not gossip.

Function: set up Chapter 6 without making Heinz the solution.

Ending turn: somewhere above, the name *Opportunity Knocks* reaches leadership, and the station quietly becomes less normal about the day.
