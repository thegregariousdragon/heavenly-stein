# Opportunity Knocks — Chapter 4 Scene Plan v1

Status: draft-only planning.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology.

## Chapter 4: Arrival Control

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 4 brings both tracks toward Der Himmelskrug: the Andersens aboard *Last Good Haul* and the Mirador agents aboard ordinary commercial passenger transport.

The chapter should show Der Himmelskrug as safe and procedural, not omniscient. Sven does the right thing: he declares the freighter's faults, the stored racing craft, and the possible response-lag concern. Flight Control responds with controlled routing. Separately, Callum and Ilya arrive through ordinary passenger channels under false identities and are released into public areas because they do not yet ask for restricted access or trigger a clear station-side violation.

## Scene 1: Approach Call

Location: aboard *Last Good Haul*, Der Himmelskrug approach corridor.

Sven contacts Flight Control before the station becomes a place instead of a destination. He declares *Last Good Haul*'s known faults: cargo-bay seal wear, unstable docking-bay power, old coolant routing, outdated maintenance records, rough handling cradle, and stored racing craft. He also declares that the stored craft has a suspected low-power control-response delay and should not be powered or released from cradle without diagnostic review.

Function: prove Sven is frightened but honest.

Ending turn: Flight Control assigns a controlled approach profile and opens a clean-side intake review with Starship Garage.

## Scene 2: The Station Answers Like a System

Location: Flight Control / approach channel, mostly heard from Sven's cockpit.

Flight Control asks precise questions and does not become impressed, suspicious, or sentimental. The controller separates issues: freighter approach safety, cargo-bay/stored craft status, clean-side versus restricted-side routing, family passenger status, and garage intake requirements.

Function: show Der Himmelskrug as process with warmth at the edges.

Ending turn: *Last Good Haul* receives a cautious lower-handle/garage-adjacent berth assignment or hold path, with public passenger release handled separately after clearance.

## Scene 3: First Sight

Location: *Last Good Haul* cockpit and passenger compartment.

The family sees Der Himmelskrug in person. Nora reacts to the beer-stein scale. Ingrid sees the station's seriousness beneath the joke. Freya watches Sven choose honesty again. Sven resists the urge to minimize the problem and confirms all cautions.

Function: emotional arrival without triumph.

Ending turn: the station grows large enough that the family must stop imagining it and start obeying it.

## Scene 4: Passenger Arrival

Location: Der Himmelskrug public Arrival Control, upper-handle arrival route.

Callum and Ilya arrive as ordinary passengers under false identities. Arrival Control runs identity, security, decontamination, customs, weapons, and clean/dirty declaration process. They carry no weapons, no declared restricted tools, and no access request beyond public event attendance. They pass because the system catches known problems, not future intent.

Function: show that station procedure is strong but not magical.

Ending turn: they are released through the public gate into the controlled entrance corridor toward Hospitality Level 1.

## Scene 5: Clean Public, Controlled Below

Location: Arrival Control release path / Hospitality Level 1 threshold.

Callum and Ilya separate naturally among event arrivals. They see enough station signage and routing to understand that public areas are not the same as operational access. Services, Starship Garage, Residential, and Restricted Works are visibly not public. This confirms Chapter 3's plan: find Sven before the station fully wraps him in support.

Function: position the agents for later public-area tracking.

Ending turn: Callum spots route information that suggests pilots and crews will eventually pass toward controlled lower services, but not through public tourist routes.

## Scene 6: The Andersen Intake Split

Location: *Last Good Haul* docking/intake channel and family release planning.

Flight Control and Arrival/Port-side procedure split the family's situation properly: the freighter and stored craft are held for clean-side garage intake; the family can clear as passengers/crew under ordinary arrival processing; the ship's name and racing significance stay inside operational logs rather than becoming public gossip.

Function: protect the ship and family without special treatment.

Ending turn: *Opportunity Knocks* is cleared only for controlled garage handling, not public reveal or powered movement.

## Chapter ending

The Andersens have arrived honestly. The agents have arrived cleanly. Der Himmelskrug has logged both arrivals, but does not yet know how they connect.

Final turn candidate:

> Der Himmelskrug had accepted two arrivals that morning.
>
> One had declared its damage.
>
> One had not.
