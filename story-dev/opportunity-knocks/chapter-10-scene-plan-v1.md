## Chapter 10: Systems With Teeth

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 10 shows that Der Himmelskrug’s kindness is not softness. It is backed by procedure, trained labor, route control, Security, Crew Support, evidence handling, and interjurisdictional handoff.

The chapter begins exactly where Chapter 9 ends: Security has arrived in the Services-adjacent Crew Support corridor. The Andersens are at the marked blue line. Callum/Marek and Ilya/Tilen are visible, contained, and still trying to turn the incident into paperwork. Calder and Pia remain important witnesses.

The chapter should not make the station omniscient. Security succeeds because the agents have already been logged by Arrival Control, recorded in controlled-adjacent space, observed by trained staff, named in witness reports, constrained by route controls, and connected to a tracker and creditor-pressure pattern.

## Authority lanes to preserve

- **Station Security / Janelle Okonkwo:** immediate station safety, controlled-route enforcement, detention, evidence preservation, incident command.
- **Crew Support / Keisha Bell:** Andersen family safety, lodging, privacy, child protection, practical care, witness-support language.
- **Compliance / Mara Venn:** legal/administrative handling of false purpose, creditor-contact posture, station-entry terms, evidence packaging.
- **Garage Ops / Oskar:** *Opportunity Knocks* remains powered down and in Garage Ops custody; no creditor access, no movement, no public status.
- **Flight Control / Amara:** confirms no traffic, docking, or ship-release authority has been granted or altered.
- **IFP liaison:** receives the formal evidence package for cross-jurisdictional review, identity fraud questions, coercive creditor enforcement, and possible broader Mirador/Northstar investigation.

## Scene 1: Security Sorts the Room

Location: Services-adjacent Crew Support corridor.

Security arrives without running. The first officers do not tackle anyone. They stabilize the space:

- Andersen family stays by the blue line.
- Public visitors keep hands visible.
- Calder and Pia remain in witness positions.
- Corridor movement pauses.
- Crew Support staff keep Nora shielded from the corridor.

Marek/Callum tries to frame the encounter as a private legal matter. Security refuses that frame because the issue is controlled-route interference and safety first.

Function: show procedural teeth without melodrama.

Ending turn: Janelle Okonkwo arrives or enters command through comms and takes incident control.

## Scene 2: Janelle Names the Problem

Location: same corridor.

Janelle identifies the categories without overclaiming:

- public visitors in a controlled-adjacent crew-support route;
- disputed creditor language used against a vulnerable crew-family party;
- possible false purpose at Arrival Control;
- blocked movement / coercive posture;
- private financial details spoken in support space;
- potential link to a protected ship under Garage Ops custody.

She does not argue morality. She names safety and evidence.

Function: show Janelle’s calm, dry authority.

Ending turn: she separates people into safe positions and starts the evidence chain.

## Scene 3: Move the Family First

Location: from corridor to protected Crew Support / temporary lodging route.

Keisha’s team moves Sven, Freya, Ingrid, and Nora through a controlled route. Nora is not exposed to interrogation or hallway spectacle. Ingrid sees more than anyone wants her to. Freya wants to stay and fight the paperwork; Keisha reframes: protecting Nora and giving a statement in the right room is not retreat.

Sven is torn because the ship is below and Mirador is right there. Security confirms *Opportunity Knocks* remains in Garage Ops custody, powered down, with no creditor access and no movement authority.

Function: protect victims/witnesses while keeping agency.

Ending turn: the Andersens are moved to temporary Crew Support lodging / protected interview space.

## Scene 4: The Agents Try to Become Paper Again

Location: controlled corridor / security holding position.

Callum/Marek tries several frames:

- private legal communication;
- no physical contact;
- no threshold crossing;
- voluntary conversation;
- public visitors with no restricted goods;
- creditor dispute outside station jurisdiction.

Ilya/Tilen stays quiet and watches routes, but route lights, staff positions, and Security bodies remove her geometry advantage.

Janelle and Security do not argue every point. They say the issue is station safety and controlled-route interference, then preserve the legal questions for Compliance and the IFP liaison.

Function: prevent antagonist cleverness from becoming dominance.

Ending turn: their public identities are checked against Arrival Control logs.

## Scene 5: Logs Have Memory

Location: Security command support / corridor evidence overlay.

Security and Compliance start assembling the incident picture:

- Arrival Control records for Marek Sova and Tilen Voss.
- Purpose of visit: race / public attendance.
- No contractor purpose declared.
- Route history through public levels.
- Loitering near controlled Services edges.
- Corridor camera showing Ilya/Tilen positioning.
- Calder’s alert timestamp.
- Pia’s corroborating statement.
- Crew Support route lights and door-state logs.
- Audio snippets / transcript fragments where legal pressure, debt number, and “Ilya” name slip occur.
- No authorization from Compliance, Northstar, Mirador, or any station office for creditor contact.

Function: show station systems catching patterns after trained humans notice them.

Ending turn: the false identities begin to look false or incomplete.

## Scene 6: The Hidden Tracker

Location: Security / Garage Ops / Compliance coordination.

Security asks the practical question: how did two public visitors know where and when to find Sven? That leads to the tracker.

Possible sequence:

- Calder’s report notes they knew too much.
- Garage Ops confirms *Opportunity Knocks* and *Last Good Haul* were under privacy/operational locks.
- Security checks whether any public feed exposed Sven’s location. It did not.
- A sweep or log review finds a non-station tracker ping associated with the freighter or cargo route.
- The tracker is not enough by itself to convict everyone of everything, but it becomes evidence of pre-arrival tracking and coordinated pressure.

Function: connect Chapter 1’s tracker to station procedure.

Ending turn: Janelle escalates from corridor incident to suspected organized coercive asset-pressure operation.

## Scene 7: No Private Enforcement Here

Location: security holding / controlled corridor.

Janelle tells Callum and Ilya that whatever Northstar thinks it owns, no creditor, contractor, or asset-protection group gets to use station corridors to coerce signatures, move ships, interfere with Garage Ops custody, or pressure crew families.

Marek/Callum asks if they are under arrest. Janelle gives the station-accurate answer: detained under station authority pending identity, safety, and transfer review with the IFP liaison.

Possible Janelle line:

> “We do not run a prison. We run a station. That is why you are leaving in hand restraints.”

Function: make law and jurisdiction clear.

Ending turn: Security places them in restraints or controlled escort for holding.

## Scene 8: Privacy Is Also Infrastructure

Location: protected Crew Support room.

The Andersens receive the immediate outcome in plain language:

- The agents are detained.
- The family is safe for now.
- *Opportunity Knocks* remains under Garage Ops custody.
- No creditor access has been granted.
- Their private financial and medical details are not public content.
- Statements will be taken with support.
- Nora does not need to hear the hallway version.

Freya asks what happens next. Keisha/Janelle explains that this becomes station evidence, Compliance review, and an evidence package for the IFP liaison.

Sven feels shame trying to return and fails to find its old seat because the station has made the danger visible.

Function: give emotional landing after threat.

Ending turn: Mirador agents are formally routed for IFP handoff, while the Andersens are moved into protected temporary lodging.
