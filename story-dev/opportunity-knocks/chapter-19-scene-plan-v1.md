# Opportunity Knocks — Chapter 19 Scene Plan v1

Status: draft-only planning.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology.

## Chapter 19: Second Breakfast

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 19 lets the Andersens release *Last Good Haul* without erasing what it carried.

The freighter is no longer the family’s home, crisis vehicle, business burden, or hiding place for *Opportunity Knocks*. It has been repaired enough to be safe, honest, and useful. It is still old. It still has limits. Those limits are documented plainly.

The chapter introduces Tamsin Orr, a nonbinary parent from Brindlehook Junction, and their teenage child Keo Orr. They need a modest, reliable-enough freighter they can afford without being trapped by another family’s hidden disaster. The Andersens deliberately sell *Last Good Haul* at a reasonable price with full disclosure, refusing to pass on the same kind of burden that nearly crushed them.

Tamsin and Keo rename the ship **Second Breakfast**. The chapter ends with *Second Breakfast* departing.

## Authority lanes to preserve

- **Freya:** owns the sale paperwork, disclosure language, price fairness, and refusal to hide defects.
- **Sven:** explains the ship’s mechanical truth without romanticizing or over-apologizing.
- **Ingrid:** understands the emotional release and watches whether the family is letting go cleanly.
- **Nora:** processes the ship’s departure in child-safe terms; she may like the new name.
- **Oskar / Garage Ops:** confirms the ship’s repair and inspection status but does not sell it for the family.
- **Tamsin Orr:** buyer, nonbinary parent, practical and cautious; not a charity case.
- **Keo Orr:** teenage child, mechanically curious, hopeful but not naive.
- **Keisha / Crew Support:** may provide transition logistics, not pressure.

## Scene 1: The Packet Is Too Honest to Sell Fast

Location: Starship Garage / controlled buyer review room.

Open with Freya looking at the sale packet for *Last Good Haul*. It is brutally honest:

- repaired power instability;
- cargo-bay door repaired and tested;
- remaining age-related limitations;
- fuel-efficiency notes;
- maintenance schedule;
- safe-use envelope;
- what the ship should not be used for;
- clear price rationale.

Freya approves the honesty and worries the honesty will scare off buyers. Sven says it should scare off the wrong buyers.

Function: make the sale ethical before emotional.

Ending turn: Keisha or Garage Ops confirms a prospective buyer has requested a review.

## Scene 2: Tamsin and Keo Orr

Location: buyer review room / berth viewing.

Introduce Tamsin Orr and Keo Orr.

Tamsin is practical, tired, cautious, and protective. Keo is excited but trying to behave older than they are. They are not looking for a legend. They are looking for a ship that will not lie to them.

They have read the packet before arriving, which makes Freya respect them immediately.

Function: show the buyers are not fools and not marks.

Ending turn: Tamsin asks to see the ship.

## Scene 3: The Walkaround

Location: *Last Good Haul*’s berth.

Sven gives the walkaround:

- what was fixed;
- what is old;
- what sounds bad but is normal;
- what sounds normal but requires attention;
- what must be inspected before long hauls;
- what the freighter can safely do.

Keo asks sharp questions. Sven answers plainly. Oskar may interrupt once to correct a phrase that sounds too sentimental or too vague.

Function: transfer knowledge, not burden.

Ending turn: Tamsin asks why the price is lower than market for a recently repaired ship.

## Scene 4: Freya Names the Price

Location: berth or review room.

Freya explains the price:

- not desperation pricing;
- not charity;
- not market extraction;
- fair cost for an old ship with known limits;
- enough to respect the ship and buyers;
- not enough to trap Tamsin and Keo in debt the way the Andersens were trapped.

Tamsin recognizes the choice. They do not want pity. Freya makes clear it is not pity. It is clean math with ethics attached.

Function: restitution in miniature.

Ending turn: Tamsin accepts the price, pending final inspection and title transfer.

## Scene 5: Keo Names What They See

Location: cargo bay / cockpit threshold.

Keo has a private or semi-private moment with Nora near the cargo bay or viewing line. Keo says the ship looks like it has been through things. Nora says it has, but it tells the truth now.

Keo may float possible new names. Nora reacts honestly.

Function: child/teen bridge between old burden and new life.

Ending turn: Keo says they think the ship needs a hungry name.

## Scene 6: The Title Transfer

Location: administrative office / Garage Ops desk.

Freya handles the paperwork with the same care she brought to Northstar. Tamsin reads carefully. Sven signs where needed. Ingrid watches.

Oskar or Garage Ops confirms inspection status. The title transfer includes the disclosure packet. There are no hidden side promises, no hand-wave language, no implied warranty beyond stated work.

Function: make the ethical sale legally durable.

Ending turn: ownership transfers.

## Scene 7: Second Breakfast

Location: berth / departure prep.

Tamsin and Keo reveal the new name: **Second Breakfast**.

Reactions:

- Nora loves it.
- Ingrid approves because ships should be fed and named honestly.
- Sven laughs and is surprised by how much it helps.
- Freya recognizes that the ship belongs to someone else now.

The new name does not erase *Last Good Haul*. It gives the ship a next life.

Function: emotional release with warmth.

Ending turn: departure clearance begins.

## Scene 8: Departure

Location: viewing point / Flight Control audio / berth.

*Second Breakfast* departs under proper clearance.

No dramatics. No chase. No debt. No hidden cargo. No desperate departure from Kestrel Minor.

Just a repaired old freighter leaving with people who know what it is.

The Andersens watch. Nora says goodbye now. Sven and Freya let it go. Ingrid says the ship passed inspection for its next life.

Ending turn: *Second Breakfast* departs.
