## Premise

Sven Andersen, an unemployed young starship engineer from Kestrel Minor, arrives at Der Himmelskrug aboard the patched old freighter *Last Good Haul* with his wife Freya, their young daughter Nora, and Sven's widowed mother Ingrid.

In the freighter's docking bay is *Opportunity Knocks*, a legendary Andersen racing ship passed from Nils Andersen to Lars Andersen and now to Sven.

The Andersen family is nearly bankrupt after Lars's terminal pancreatic cancer, palliative-care costs, the collapse of the family freight business, repairs to *Last Good Haul*, and predatory refinancing by Northstar Collateral Services. Northstar claims a disputed lien reaches *Opportunity Knocks*. Sven and Freya dispute that claim. Northstar uses Mirador Asset Protection Group to pressure Sven, but no Northstar representative is physically present on Der Himmelskrug.

When the Himmelskrug Run is announced, Sven sees one lawful path out: enter *Opportunity Knocks*, win the 25 million credit first prize, clear the family debt, and save the ship.

## Locked race facts

- Race event: **Himmelskrug Run**.
- Official course: **The Knotward Run**.
- Barren moon: **Kaltmond**.
- Kaltmond maneuver: high-speed gravity-assisted slingshot through a legal Flight Control corridor. Too shallow loses the boost; too wide leaves the course and risks disqualification; too deep risks impact with Kaltmond.
- Asteroid gate: **The Twins' Teeth**.
- The Twins' Teeth maneuver: not a straight slot. The legal line is an angled, offset gap that requires precise yaw and roll so the ship presents its narrowest safe profile at speed.
- Added course feature: **The Gauntlet**, a sanctioned high-precision corridor after the Twins' Teeth that tests repeated rapid handling corrections under load.
- Finish-line kink: **The Last Pour**.
- Chicanes connect the major challenges, including opening chicanes, a Kaltmond exit chicane, a chicane between the Twins' Teeth and The Gauntlet, and the final approach into The Last Pour.
- First prize: **25 million credits**.
- Second prize: **20 million credits**.
- Third prize: **15 million credits**.
- Prize funding: Board-approved event fund, sponsors, and media rights.
- Rules: no hyperspace, no active weapons, strict Flight Control authority.

## Main characters

### Sven Andersen

Unemployed young starship engineer from Kestrel Minor and heir to *Opportunity Knocks*. Sven is blacklisted after refusing unsafe or fraudulent ship-repair work. He has skill from simulators, family telemetry, maintenance runs, and short private test flights, but no major public racing proof yet.

### Freya Andersen

Sven's wife. Freya helped run the Andersen freight business from the practical side: bookkeeping, route-cost tracking, scheduling, cargo paperwork, creditor communication, insurance forms, lien records, and refinancing records. She also learned medical bureaucracy the hard way during Lars's pancreatic cancer and palliative care. She is not formally a lawyer or doctor, but she knows how paperwork can become a weapon.

Freya does not immediately take a station job after the story. Her ending focuses on relocation, legal cleanup, Nora's transition, financial recovery, and the right to have options after survival mode.

### Nora Andersen

Sven and Freya's young daughter, around five or six. Nora is protected from public exposure and does not become media content. In the ending, she sees *Opportunity Knocks* as a legacy she may one day choose, not a destiny forced on her.

### Ingrid Andersen

Sven's widowed mother. Ingrid becomes the Andersen family's home anchor after relocation. She helps Nora settle, keeps family racing history alive, and sometimes participates in approved family-controlled storytelling, but she is not a full-time public exhibit or race mascot.

Ingrid gives the family-approved public quote about Lars:

> Lars never taught Sven that *Opportunity Knocks* belonged to the fastest hand. He taught him she belonged to the hand that listened.

### Lars Andersen

Sven's father. Lars preserved and modernized the legend of *Opportunity Knocks*. He died after a terminal pancreatic cancer diagnosis. The family pursued palliative care to preserve dignity and reduce suffering, and those costs became a major part of the family debt spiral.

### Nils Andersen

Sven's grandfather. Nils first made *Opportunity Knocks* famous on rough frontier courses.

### Oskar Teague

Senior Starship Garage repair chief and high-performance systems specialist. Oskar is a veteran practical mechanic, calm systems diagnostician, former racer or former race mechanic, blunt, and evidence-driven. He is the first senior garage figure to believe Sven when Sven says *Opportunity Knocks* is answering late.

### Calder Yim

Crew Support worker under Keisha Bell's area. Calder is practical, route-facing, and attentive to access and credential irregularities. Calder witnesses Mirador's behavior near Services and starts the proper report chain.

### Pia Stern

Maintenance safety steward / Guild-adjacent worker. Pia is young but sharp. She corroborates the coercive tone, blocked movement, and threat pattern during the Mirador confrontation.

## Ship facts

### Opportunity Knocks

*Opportunity Knocks* is widely known in the interstellar racing world. Her legend is not that she magically wins. Her legend is that Andersen pilots made her look impossible.

Core reputation:

> She does not make average pilots good. She makes great pilots look impossible.

Fame details:

- Nils Andersen made the legend on rough frontier courses.
- Lars Andersen preserved and modernized the legend.
- *Opportunity Knocks* retired undefeated in her class under Andersen hands.
- Sven inherited the training, instincts, simulator records, maintenance language, and nerve required to fly her.
- Sven has not yet had a major sanctioned chance to prove himself.

After the story, *Opportunity Knocks* remains private Andersen family property. She receives a protected Starship Garage berth with controlled physical access and possible public-viewable feed or scheduled display only by family consent.

Sven still races occasionally in sanctioned events later, but not immediately. The long-term Andersen legacy eventually passes to Nora as a choice, not a forced destiny.

### Last Good Haul

*Last Good Haul* is the Andersen family freighter. It is old, patched, and mechanically questionable. Sven honestly declares its faults to Flight Control:

- cargo-bay seal wear;
- unstable docking-bay power;
- old coolant routing;
- outdated maintenance records;
- rough handling cradle for the stored racing craft.

After the story, *Last Good Haul* is repaired enough to be safe, honest, and useful. The Andersens sell it at a deliberately reasonable cost to Tamsin and Keo Orr, who rename it **Second Breakfast**.
