# Opportunity Knocks — Chapter 18 Scene Plan v1

Status: draft-only planning.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology.

## Chapter 18: A Place to Stay

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 18 turns victory, legal freedom, and public boundaries into a practical future.

The chapter opens with Sven receiving the formal Starship Garage offer hinted at the end of Chapter 17. The offer is not charity, not founder indulgence, and not a reward for being famous. It is earned recognition based on repair collaboration, safety-critical diagnosis, respect for process, race conduct, Oskar’s recommendation, Rolf’s confirmation, and Flight Control’s notes.

Sven does not accept alone. He brings the offer to Freya and Ingrid in the room. The family decision matters because relocation affects all of them. The chapter then moves through practical steps: housing, Nora’s Learning Center intake, Ingrid’s role as home anchor, Freya’s deliberate choice not to rush into work, and the first signs that Der Himmelskrug can be home.

The ending turn is *Last Good Haul* being prepared for its next life.

## Authority lanes to preserve

- **Oskar / Garage Ops:** recommends Sven because of demonstrated diagnostic and repair competence; does not make sentimental rescue offers.
- **Rolf / Engineering:** confirms the role’s technical fit and boundaries with station systems, high-performance craft diagnostics, and safety process.
- **Keisha / Crew Support:** supports family transition logistics without turning them into dependents.
- **Freya:** evaluates the offer as family stability, not just Sven’s dream.
- **Ingrid:** reads whether the family is choosing safety or fleeing fear; becomes the practical home anchor.
- **Nora:** gets Learning Center intake and child-safe transition support.
- **Heinz:** may be institutionally delighted but should not be framed as gifting Sven the job.
- **Feli:** may keep public attention from contaminating the employment process.

## Scene 1: Future Nuisance Classification

Location: private meeting room near Crew Support or Garage Ops.

Oskar, Rolf, Keisha, Sven, Freya, and Ingrid meet privately. Oskar opens with dry language and makes the job sound like an inconvenience because he refuses to make it a miracle.

The offer:

- probationary senior-track Starship Garage engineering role;
- focus on high-performance craft diagnostics;
- supervised onboarding;
- safety-critical systems and historical craft profiles;
- no authority over his own ship’s race clearance;
- standard employment review;
- family transition support separate from employment.

Function: show the job is real and bounded.

Ending turn: Sven is handed the formal offer.

## Scene 2: Not Pity

Location: same meeting.

Sven asks, or nearly asks, whether this is pity. Oskar is offended. Rolf explains the technical basis:

- Sven identified a subtle control-feedback fault ordinary intake might miss;
- he helped reproduce it without overclaiming;
- he respected inspection authority;
- his race conduct supported Flight Control’s safety notes;
- Garage Ops needs people who listen to old ships without worshipping them.

Keisha clarifies that Crew Support logistics are separate; the job is not payment for suffering.

Function: make recognition concrete.

Ending turn: Sven looks at Freya and Ingrid instead of answering.

## Scene 3: We Decide Here

Location: same room.

Sven says he cannot accept alone. Oskar grumbles approval. Freya asks practical questions:

- housing;
- pay;
- probation length;
- childcare/Learning Center;
- family privacy;
- Opportunity Knocks access boundaries;
- what happens if Sven fails probation;
- whether declining affects station support or title protection.

Rolf and Keisha answer plainly.

Function: make family consent operational.

Ending turn: Freya says they should take the offer seriously, but not because they are afraid of leaving.

## Scene 4: Ingrid Names the Difference

Location: Crew Support room, after meeting.

The family talks privately. Ingrid distinguishes staying because Der Himmelskrug is safe from staying because they are trapped.

They are no longer being chased by immediate debt. The ship is protected. The public statement held. This means the family can choose.

Sven admits he wants the work. Freya admits she wants time. Ingrid says wanting a safe table is not weakness.

Function: emotional family decision.

Ending turn: Freya says yes, if they build the decision around all four of them.

## Scene 5: Nora’s Intake

Location: Learning Center intake area.

Nora visits the Learning Center with Freya, Sven, Ingrid, and Keisha or a Learning Center staff member. The scene should be light, practical, and child-centered.

Nora asks about:

- moon-bear rules;
- snack drawing;
- whether race ships count as homework;
- whether Flight Control can give detention;
- whether station maps are allowed to be silly.

The staff answer with warmth and process.

Function: show Nora gaining a future that is not crisis-shaped.

Ending turn: Nora likes one thing enough to ask when she comes back.

## Scene 6: Freya’s Not-Yet

Location: station residential/services transition office or Crew Support.

Freya is offered options, not pressure:

- legal cleanup support;
- business-record closure;
- family finance planning;
- possible future station work or training if she wants;
- time before choosing.

She does not immediately take a station job. Her ending in this chapter is safety, recovery, and choices.

Function: avoid reducing Freya to spouse-of-employed-engineer.

Ending turn: Freya schedules time to breathe before scheduling work.

## Scene 7: First Home Inventory

Location: temporary-to-longer-term family quarters.

The Andersens move from Crew Support lodging toward a more stable residence or transitional family quarters. Ingrid takes charge of practical home details:

- where the kettle goes;
- where Nora’s drawings go;
- where the documents are stored;
- what food counts as emergency food;
- what cannot be put near ship parts.

Sven brings too few personal belongings and too many tool habits. Freya makes the documents safe. Nora names a corner for moon-bear.

Function: make home tangible.

Ending turn: Ingrid says the room is not home yet, but it has accepted inspection.

## Scene 8: Last Good Haul Waits

Location: Starship Garage / controlled berth.

Sven visits *Last Good Haul*. The old freighter is repaired enough to be safe, honest, and useful, but no longer the family’s future.

The family discusses selling it. They will not pass the burden forward. Any buyer must know the ship’s truth.

This sets up Tamsin and Keo in Chapter 19.

Function: begin letting go.

Ending turn: *Last Good Haul* is prepared for its next life.
