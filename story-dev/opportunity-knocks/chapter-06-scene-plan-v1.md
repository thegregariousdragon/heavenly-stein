# Opportunity Knocks — Chapter 6 Scene Plan v1

Status: draft-only planning.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology.

## Chapter 6: Heinz Is Being Very Normal

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 6 brings *Opportunity Knocks* to station leadership awareness without turning Heinz into the solution or letting ship fame override process. Heinz is delighted, Feli prevents delight from becoming a privacy incident, Rolf focuses on station structural and handling-system risk, and Oskar keeps the garage floor under diagnostic authority.

Because the current draft has established strict safety gates, Chapter 6 should **not** end with full race registration. Instead, it should end with Sven entering a preliminary eligibility review path: no race registration, no public announcement, and no powered movement until diagnostics, Engineering, Garage Ops, and Flight Control say the ship is safe.

## Heinz competence watch item

Heinz has aerospace engineering training and ship/flight competence. The chapter should restrain him by **role, privacy, and process**, not by technical ignorance. He may understand flight-handling, pitch/yaw/roll, control-feedback, and racecourse implications, but he is not the assigned diagnostic lead, structural authority, communications authority, or Flight Control clearance authority.

Useful distinction:

> Heinz may know what the problem means.
>
> He may not be the person authorized to clear it.

## Scene 1: The Name Reaches Crown Level

Location: Crown Admin / Communications and Civic Interface.

Feli receives Oskar's operational notification about *Opportunity Knocks*. She reads the privacy flag before reacting to the ship name. Heinz notices her stillness and guesses badly. Feli makes clear that this is not a fan moment; it is an operational privacy and safety matter involving a family under creditor pressure.

Function: establish Feli as gatekeeper for public consequences.

Ending turn: Heinz learns the name and tries very hard to be normal.

## Scene 2: Strategic Operations, Not Garage Command

Location: Crown Admin or small operational conference nook.

Heinz, Feli, Rolf, and possibly Amara receive the controlled summary. Rolf cares about the port-forward asymmetry, station handling systems, and structural/load-path implications. Amara cares that no race registration action happens before Flight Control clearance. Heinz understands the aerospace handling danger but must be reminded that technical understanding is not clearance authority.

Function: show station departments doing their jobs while keeping Heinz competent.

Ending turn: Heinz is allowed awareness and technical context, not control.

## Scene 3: Feli Writes the Anti-Rumor Plan

Location: Feli's office / comms station.

Feli drafts internal privacy language: staff are not to leak, speculate, photograph, or turn the ship into a station story. Communications prepares for possible public attention only if the family consents later. She explicitly protects Nora, medical/debt details, and the active garage diagnostic process.

Function: protect family dignity and station credibility.

Ending turn: Feli authorizes a limited leadership visit only if Oskar agrees and the visit does not disrupt garage work.

## Scene 4: Heinz Visits the Garage Under Supervision

Location: Starship Garage observation boundary.

Heinz arrives with Feli and Rolf, not alone. Oskar controls the floor. Heinz sees *Opportunity Knocks* on handling pads and has a near-fanboy failure, but then reads the diagnostic problem intelligently. He asks one useful handling question. Oskar answers as a professional, not as someone humoring him.

Function: deliver warm/comedic payoff while preserving Heinz's aerospace competence and garage authority.

Ending turn: Heinz asks what the ship needs, and Oskar answers with diagnostics, not reverence.

## Scene 5: Sven Meets the Founder

Location: owner observation console / controlled garage boundary.

Sven is embarrassed and braced for spectacle. Heinz meets him respectfully, acknowledges the ship's history without making Sven perform family legacy, and clarifies that Der Himmelskrug will treat *Opportunity Knocks* as a privately owned craft under protocol, not as a trophy.

Function: give Sven support without rescue.

Ending turn: Sven admits he does not know whether he is allowed to try for the Run. Heinz redirects him to the proper process.

## Scene 6: Preliminary Eligibility, Not Registration

Location: Garage Ops / Flight Control / Race Ops intake channel.

Amara or a Flight Control representative confirms the correct path: preliminary eligibility file may be opened, but race registration remains inactive until ship diagnostics, Engineering review, pilot eligibility, telemetry requirements, and safety signoff. Sven signs only an intent-to-evaluate / eligibility review acknowledgment.

Function: preserve the safety gates and avoid premature race commitment.

Ending turn: Sven leaves with a controlled process, not a promise.

## Chapter ending

Heinz is thrilled. Feli is watching. Rolf is already thinking about the frame. Oskar is testing the ship. Sven has not registered, but he has stopped refusing before evidence.

Final turn candidate:

> For the first time, the Himmelskrug Run was not a miracle in the distance.
>
> It was a file with gates.
>
> Sven could work with gates.
