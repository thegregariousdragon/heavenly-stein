## Chapter 9: Near Services

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 9 converts Mirador’s private debt pressure into a station safety and access-control issue.

Callum and Ilya do not storm the Starship Garage, threaten Nora, or attempt an open seizure. They use the tactic established earlier: narrow time, narrow options, narrow language. They find Sven near a Services / crew-support transition point, isolate him briefly from Freya, and try to make him believe signing or surrendering *Opportunity Knocks* is the only adult way to protect his family.

The station does not magically know their intent. The problem becomes visible because controlled spaces have staff, route logs, witness habits, and workers trained to notice blocked movement and access irregularity.

## Canon / routing constraints

- Public visitors cannot casually access Services, Starship Garage, Residential, or Restricted Works.
- Services is controlled staff/pilot/operational support, not a public atrium level.
- Callum and Ilya arrived as public visitors under false identities, so any Services-adjacent presence must come from edge behavior: waiting near controlled thresholds, exploiting public-facing counters, following someone through a directed access point, or hovering near crew-support routing.
- They cannot reach *Opportunity Knocks* directly.
- They should not physically attack Sven in a cartoonish way. The threat pattern is coercive pressure, legal intimidation, blocked movement, false inevitability, and implied consequences.
- Nora is not used as a lever beyond the existence of family stakes. Callum already drew that professional line earlier.
- Freya is the person most likely to recognize the paperwork trap.
- Calder Yim notices route/access irregularity under Keisha’s Crew Support area.
- Pia Stern corroborates blocked movement, coercive tone, and threat pattern.

## Scene 1: Crew Support Makes a Door

Location: Services-adjacent crew-support waiting route.

After Chapter 8, Sven is sent to update Freya, Ingrid, and Nora in controlled crew-support space. Keisha’s team has provided food, wash access, quiet seating, and a way to speak with family without turning the ship into public spectacle.

Sven is tired but steadier. He is carrying truthful language: not cleared, repair investigation, useful work, no race promise.

Function: place Sven near the Services/Crew Support edge for a legitimate reason.

Ending turn: Freya steps away briefly to handle paperwork / lodging / legal-contact intake, and Sven is momentarily separated.

## Scene 2: Callum and Ilya Find the Seam

Location: public-to-controlled edge near directed crew/pilot support.

Callum and Ilya cannot enter Services freely. They have been watching public flows, controlled doors, event staff, pilot-support signs, and directed access points. They identify Sven when he appears near a controlled transition or staff-mediated waiting area.

They use false public identities and plausible visitor confusion to get near enough to speak without immediately triggering a hard stop.

Function: show the station as procedural, not omniscient.

Ending turn: Callum opens with legal language, not a threat.

## Scene 3: The Door Gets Narrow

Location: a controlled-edge corridor / waiting alcove.

Callum uses Northstar’s lien language: preservation asset, collateral integrity, voluntary resolution, asset deterioration, public status change, third-party process capture. He suggests that Der Himmelskrug’s inspection and preliminary eligibility file make Sven’s position worse if he does not cooperate now.

Ilya positions herself to make the conversation feel private and hard to leave. She does not grab Sven. She blocks sightlines and movement with ordinary body placement.

Function: make coercion feel administrative.

Ending turn: Sven realizes they know too much about the ship, the debt, and his family’s pressure.

## Scene 4: Freya Hears the Shape of the Trap

Location: same area, entering from Crew Support / paperwork counter.

Freya returns before Sven signs anything. She immediately recognizes that Callum’s language is not a lawful station process but a pressure tactic. She asks pointed questions:

- Who authorized station-side contact?
- Are they registered with Compliance?
- Are they claiming enforcement authority?
- Why are they speaking to Sven outside counsel / crew support / station process?
- Why are they using Northstar’s language under false visitor posture?

Callum tries to keep it civil. Ilya tries to end the interaction quickly.

Function: bring Freya’s paperwork competence into the confrontation.

Ending turn: Callum drops the mask enough to imply consequences if Sven refuses.

## Scene 5: Calder Notices the Wrong Shape

Location: Crew Support / Port Services sightline.

Calder Yim notices the access irregularity: two public-release visitors are too close to a controlled crew-support route, one is positioned wrong for casual conversation, and Sven is being kept from easy movement back toward the counter. The words may sound polite, but the geometry is wrong.

Calder starts the report chain quietly rather than confronting first. He logs:

- possible unauthorized contact near Services threshold;
- public visitors matching arrival identities;
- blocked movement / coercive posture;
- vulnerable crew-family party;
- creditor / lien language overheard.

Function: convert scene from private intimidation to station safety issue.

Ending turn: Calder routes the alert to Keisha’s Crew Support / Port Services channel and Station Security intake.

## Scene 6: Pia Sees the Pattern

Location: nearby maintenance/service transition.

Pia Stern is present for a maintenance task, Guild-adjacent safety check, or service corridor transition. She hears enough to identify coercive tone and blocked movement. She may not know all legal details, but she knows a pressure pattern: two people narrowing another person’s options in controlled space.

She corroborates Calder’s report and gives a worker-safety framing: this is not “a private conversation.” It is a controlled-route interference pattern.

Function: show labor/safety culture catching the issue.

Ending turn: Pia moves where she can keep the line of sight open without escalating physically.

## Scene 7: The Mask Slips

Location: corridor / waiting edge.

Callum tries one last pressure turn. He may say that signing now keeps Nora out of public debt proceedings, keeps Freya from being dragged through process, keeps the ship from being locked in station bureaucracy, or keeps Sven from proving Northstar right. He does not explicitly threaten harm to Nora.

Sven nearly folds because the pressure hits shame. Freya counters with process: no signature, no surrender, no conversation outside station channels, no creditor access to a ship under Garage Ops.

Ilya realizes the window has closed.

Function: emotional danger without making Sven foolish.

Ending turn: Security call becomes active.

## Scene 8: Security Is Alerted

Location: same.

Calder’s alert reaches the proper channel. A calm station voice asks everyone to remain where they are. Doors / route lights / staff positions subtly change. The public visitors have not been tackled yet, but the station has noticed them.

Callum and Ilya understand that the station has turned from background into system.

Function: end on procedural teeth.

Ending turn: Station Security is responding.
