# Opportunity Knocks — Chapter 7 Scene Plan v1

Status: draft-only planning.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology.

## Chapter 7: Answering Late

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 7 makes Sven earn credibility in the Starship Garage. He has declared that *Opportunity Knocks* is “answering late,” but the first ordinary diagnostic passes do not prove him right. Oskar does not dismiss him, but he also does not accept instinct as evidence. Sven’s family knowledge, mechanic’s touch, and inherited familiarity with the craft help the team reproduce the problem under the right passive/high-load simulation conditions.

The chapter should end with Oskar escalating to Rolf Petersen for structural/load-path and station-side handling review, and to Heinz Schmitt for aerospace flight-handling context. This preserves the role distinction: Rolf owns station structural/handling-system implications; Heinz is technically competent in ship and racecraft handling; neither of them clears Garage Ops, Engineering, or Flight Control lanes by enthusiasm or insight alone.

## Scene 1: The Machine Refuses to Confess

Location: Starship Garage diagnostic berth.

Oskar’s team runs ordinary no-power and low-power-safe diagnostics: actuator path, local indicator, trim input chain, feedback calibration, cradle stress, old repair seam review, and basic control-return timing. The data mostly comes back acceptable. Sven’s concern remains unproven.

Function: avoid instant validation. The story should not reward instinct without evidence.

Ending turn: Sven starts to look like he may have wasted everyone’s time, and Oskar refuses to let shame become part of the diagnostic process.

## Scene 2: “Answering Late”

Location: owner observation console / diagnostic replay station.

Oskar asks Sven to describe the problem without apology, metaphor, or family grief. Sven explains the sensation: not a command failure, not a dead actuator, not obvious drift. The ship answers, but the answer arrives after his hand has already started correcting.

Function: define the technical/emotional title phrase.

Ending turn: Oskar separates three possibilities: pilot fatigue, dirty feedback path, or frame/control coupling under load.

## Scene 3: Ordinary Tests Are Too Polite

Location: diagnostic station and passive handling rig.

The first tests reproduce ordinary motion, not race stress. Everything looks just barely fine. The ship is old, repaired, and asymmetrical, but within normal acceptance. Oskar explains that ordinary dock tests can miss a problem that only appears when multiple systems are asked to agree quickly.

Function: connect canon systems logic to story stakes.

Ending turn: Oskar asks what kind of motion Lars would have used to wake the problem.

## Scene 4: Family Knowledge as Evidence

Location: same berth, with Sven at observation console.

Sven remembers old Andersen maintenance language: Lars hated lazy transitions; Nils said *Opportunity Knocks* did not mind hard flying, she minded lazy thinking. Sven also frames Ingrid correctly as his mother, a practical family truth-keeper who would challenge Nils’s mythmaking.

Sven realizes they should not test a single-axis trim input; they should simulate a combined attitude transition with pitch, yaw, and roll under load, similar to old race-line correction.

Function: make family history technically useful without making it mystical.

Ending turn: Oskar authorizes a passive, no-power, model-only high-load attitude profile.

## Scene 5: The Late Answer Appears

Location: diagnostic display / passive simulation.

Under the combined profile, the data finally shows the tiny lag: actuator command is clean, but pilot-feedback return is smoothed/delayed by resonance through the port-forward historical repair seam. The problem is small, old, and easy to miss in gentle handling.

Function: prove Sven’s concern while keeping it technical and consequence-based.

Ending turn: the garage becomes quiet because the issue is real.

## Scene 6: Oskar Names What He Can, Not What He Can’t

Location: diagnostic station.

Oskar still refuses to overstate the diagnosis. He says they have enough to escalate, not enough to clear repair. Sven wants to know if he broke the ship, if Lars missed it, or if Nils flew with it. Oskar does not answer what the evidence cannot prove.

Function: preserve truth boundaries.

Ending turn: Oskar tells Sven that finding a problem is not the same as blame.

## Scene 7: Escalation to Rolf and Heinz

Location: Garage Ops internal channel.

Oskar sends the escalation to Rolf Petersen and Heinz Schmitt in different lanes:

- Rolf receives the structural/load-path and station-handling packet: port-forward historical asymmetry, station handling interfaces, pad-load boundaries, passive-to-active simulation envelope, and any repair path touching station hardware.
- Heinz receives the aerospace handling-context packet: what the late pilot-feedback return means under combined pitch/yaw/roll workload, especially if the ship ever moves toward race-profile review.

The message explicitly states that Heinz is not being asked to clear the ship and that the escalation does not alter Garage Ops, Engineering, or Flight Control authority.

Function: hand off cleanly to Chapter 8 while honoring the hard canon that Heinz is technically competent but role-bound.

Ending turn: Oskar tells Sven the good news and the bad news: he was right, and that means the ship is not cleared.
