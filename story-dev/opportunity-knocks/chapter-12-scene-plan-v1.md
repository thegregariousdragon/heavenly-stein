# Opportunity Knocks — Chapter 12 Scene Plan v1

Status: draft-only planning.

This file does not create active canon or active chronology.

## Chapter 12: Race Clearance

## Chapter purpose

Chapter 12 moves *Opportunity Knocks* from private repair success to public race participation without turning Sven’s family, debt, medical history, or Mirador incident into spectacle.

Flight Control, Race Ops, Garage Ops, Compliance, Communications, and Crew Support all have separate gates. The chapter should make clear that passing Garage Ops inspection was not race clearance. Race clearance is earned through telemetry review, pilot eligibility, remote-abort compatibility, course-risk review, public-status boundaries, and final Flight Control signoff.

The ending turn is the Himmelskrug Run beginning.

## Authority lanes to preserve

- **Flight Control / Amara:** final authority over race participation, traffic windows, remote abort, course telemetry, start hold, and release.
- **Race Ops:** entrant file, race rules, sponsor/race logistics, public entrant listing, transponder display.
- **Garage Ops / Oskar:** craft inspection packet and ship-readiness handoff; no longer owns race clearance.
- **Engineering / Rolf:** confirms station-side test envelope and repair structural signoff already transmitted.
- **Communications / Feli:** public-status boundary, media language, privacy lock, family-safe framing.
- **Crew Support / Keisha:** protected viewing, family support, child-safe language.
- **Compliance / Mara:** no creditor or private-enforcement access; public announcement must not expose private evidence, debt, medical history, or investigation details.
- **Heinz:** may be emotionally invested and technically aware, but does not announce clearance, override Flight Control, or make Sven a public pet project.

## Scene 1: Not Yet Yes

Location: Flight Control / Race Ops review channel.

Open with Amara reviewing the next-gate package:

- Garage Ops inspection passed.
- Structural/test-envelope review complete.
- Telemetry package pending review.
- Remote abort compatibility pending.
- Pilot eligibility pending.
- Public-status language pending.
- Race Ops entrant file still inactive.

Amara’s mood should be practical and unsentimental. She likes clean packets; she does not like people treating clean packets as permission.

Function: separate inspection from race clearance.

Ending turn: Sven is summoned for a controlled pilot/telemetry review, not celebration.

## Scene 2: The Pilot Review

Location: controlled Flight Control review room / simulator pod.

Sven completes pilot-eligibility review:

- rules acknowledgement;
- no hyperspace;
- no active weapons;
- Flight Control remote abort authority;
- telemetry transparency;
- emergency compliance;
- no creditor or prize-pressure excuse for unsafe flight;
- no romantic heroics.

Amara or a Race Ops official asks directly whether he understands that he can be pulled from the course even after start.

Sven says yes and means it.

Function: show Sven choosing process over desperation.

Ending turn: Amara authorizes telemetry integration test, not race entry yet.

## Scene 3: Telemetry Tells the Truth

Location: Garage Ops / Flight Control telemetry bridge.

*Opportunity Knocks* is powered only as authorized for telemetry and control-path verification. Oskar supervises ship-side work. Flight Control receives feed. Race Ops checks display integration. Engineering confirms the repaired profile reports honestly.

The ship’s public-facing display should not expose private repair details. Technical truth goes to Flight Control; public race feed gets only approved craft status.

Function: let the ship “speak” to Flight Control.

Ending turn: telemetry integration passes.

## Scene 4: The Public Status Problem

Location: Feli’s Communications / Civic Interface office.

Feli, Mara, Keisha, possibly Heinz, and Race Ops discuss how to list *Opportunity Knocks* publicly.

Known public facts may include:

- craft name;
- entrant/pilot name if race participation accepted;
- craft class/entrant number;
- historical significance in racing feeds;
- Race Ops standard statistics.

Protected facts:

- debt amount;
- Lars’s medical history;
- Mirador incident details;
- tracker evidence;
- Nora;
- family lodging;
- private repair notes beyond approved craft status;
- Crew Support support details.

Feli draws the line: the comeback can be public only where Sven/Freya consent and where safety/race integrity require disclosure. The family is not content.

Function: prevent “legend returns” from devouring the family.

Ending turn: Sven and Freya are asked to approve a public-status statement.

## Scene 5: Consent Is Not a Formality

Location: protected Crew Support room.

Feli and Keisha bring the proposed public statement to Sven and Freya.

The statement says enough:

- *Opportunity Knocks* has entered the Himmelskrug Run after completing required inspection and Flight Control eligibility gates.
- Sven Andersen is the registered pilot.
- The craft is privately owned.
- The family requests privacy.
- Der Himmelskrug will not comment on private family, legal, medical, or support matters.

Sven wants to hide. Freya wants to control every word. Ingrid is proud but protective. Nora wants to know whether moon-bear is in the race. Keisha ensures the child-safe explanation is accurate: Papa is flying, the grown-ups checked the ship, and watching does not mean promising a win.

Function: give the family agency over public exposure.

Ending turn: Sven and Freya consent to the public-status statement.

## Scene 6: The Feed Notices

Location: public broadcast booth / race feed.

Ren Calder and Maude Kess prepare race coverage. They are allowed to recognize *Opportunity Knocks* and her history, but they do not get private family context.

Ren is polished, public-feed smooth. Maude is a retired racer who understands what the ship’s return means but is careful not to turn it into myth.

They frame:

- *Opportunity Knocks* is famous.
- Andersen hands made her look impossible.
- Sven Andersen is not Nils or Lars.
- The ship passed the required gates.
- The course, not nostalgia, will decide.

Function: build public stakes and skepticism.

Ending turn: the public entrant list updates and crowd reaction begins.

## Scene 7: Protected Viewing

Location: family-safe viewing room.

Freya, Ingrid, and Nora watch from a protected space, not public stands. Crew Support has arranged:

- no public camera access;
- delayed/filtered family feed;
- sound controls;
- emergency route;
- child-safe overlay option;
- private staff contact.

Nora asks if Papa can hear them. Freya says not during the race. Ingrid says listening works both directions only when the channel is meant for it.

Function: preserve family privacy and emotional stakes.

Ending turn: Sven sends a short pre-race message through approved private channel.

## Scene 8: Start Hold

Location: Flight Control / race start queue / cockpit.

Sven is in *Opportunity Knocks*. He completes pre-start checks. The ship answers honestly.

Amara runs final status:

- telemetry green;
- remote abort green;
- no active weapons;
- no hyperspace path;
- course corridor controlled;
- public feed live;
- family privacy lock active;
- Race Ops green;
- Flight Control green.

Amara reminds all pilots that Flight Control owns the course.

Function: move from preparation to race start with authority intact.

Ending turn: the Himmelskrug Run begins.
