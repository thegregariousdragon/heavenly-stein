# Mechanics Cheat Sheet

For Der Himmelskrug physical scene blocking, visual description, level placement, lighting modes, and route separation, also check [`quick-reference/STATION_BLOCKING_GUIDE.md`](STATION_BLOCKING_GUIDE.md).

Use this as the fast-reference guide for world systems. The full rules remain in the numbered canon files.

## Transport and movement

- Personnel teleportation is point-to-point.
- A valid transport needs an authorized origin point, authorized destination point, sufficient power, clear destination volume, identity/route authorization, and destination acceptance.
- Personal remotes or data slates carry authorization data. They do not create destinations.
- Destinations can accept, deny, delay, reroute, quarantine, or lock down arrivals.
- Der Himmelskrug routes public personnel arrivals through the Transit Reception Center inside Arrival Control.
- No routine direct teleportation into the taproom, platz, residences, school, brewery production spaces, or command areas.
- Emergency overrides route to the safest controlled receiving point, not necessarily the requested destination.

Useful line: “Nobody appears behind the beer.”

## Arrival Control

Public-facing arrival flow:

1. Docking or authorized personnel transport.
2. Secured arrival zone.
3. Shared identity, security, decontamination, customs, weapons, and clean/dirty declaration process.
4. Public release gate.
5. Controlled entrance corridor.
6. Hospitality Level 1 atrium threshold, with the main taproom across the atrium.

Departures use a separate exit corridor back toward exit screening, customs, and the handle departure path.

Friendly sign: “Clear security. Wash off space. Ask before translating German. Drink later.”

## Station vertical movement

- Public scenic bubble elevators serve only the public atrium levels: Observation, Entertainment, Hospitality Level 1, Hospitality Level 2, and Retail.
- Bubble elevators are iconic and public-facing, but they are not the emergency backbone.
- Retail is the bottom of normal public access. Public visitors do not wander into Residential, Services, Starship Garage, or Restricted Works.
- Crown, Residential, Services, Medical, Command, Learning Center, Museum, garages, brewery back-of-house, and dirty-side areas use controlled hard-service cores, resident/staff lifts, Medical/emergency lifts, and protected stairs.
- Services is controlled staff/pilot/operational support, not a public atrium level.

## Clean-side and restricted-side routing

- Clean means clean.
- Dirty, hazardous, undeclared, suspicious, contaminated, artifact-adjacent, battle-damaged, weapons-irregular, ordnance-handling, or legally complicated traffic goes below.
- Restricted-side items do not leave Restricted Works until cleared, processed, decontaminated, stored, researched, disposed of, or released by the appropriate authority.
- Public visitors, clean-side pilots, restricted-side cargo, residents, staff, VIPs, medical cases, and emergency traffic normally use distinct controlled routes.

Useful lines:

- “Probably fine goes below.”
- “If it belongs below, say so before we find it.”


## Station sensory mechanics

- For detailed surface, lighting, air, smell, and sound rules, use [`canon/08-der-himmelskrug-station.md`](../canon/08-der-himmelskrug-station.md) and [`quick-reference/STATION_BLOCKING_GUIDE.md`](STATION_BLOCKING_GUIDE.md).
- Odor control is mechanical infrastructure, not mood. Brewery, food, cannabis, salon, Medical, garage, decon, and Restricted Works air systems are locally controlled.
- Any smell in the wrong zone is a system clue: ventilation bleed, door misuse, filter failure, decon stress, cargo leak, or policy breach.
- Acoustic separation is mechanical and architectural. Loud venues use vestibules, buffers, and sound isolation; hotel, Residential, Medical, and Crown areas should not receive casual noise bleed.
- Lighting signals authority and mode. Warm public light does not remove access controls; hazard light does not make Restricted Works public; emergency blue-white/red modes override decorative public glow.

## Starship Garage and Restricted Works

- Formal names: Starship Garage for clean-side normal ship handling; Restricted Works for restricted dirty-side / hazardous industrial handling. Staff slang may still say clean side / dirty side.
- Starship Garage handles normal berths, clean cargo, inspection, recharge, crew transition, non-hazardous ship support, and Lass ma einen bauen's pride berth/feed.
- Restricted Works handles decon, quarantine, hazardous salvage, dirty repair, artifact/anomaly intake, restricted ordnance/rearming, evidence/compliance/security, industrial drones, and heavy engineering.
- Consumable starship-weapon rearming belongs in Restricted Works, not normal Starship Garage berths. Rearming is licensed hazardous work under station authority.
- A clean-side ship can still be moved to Restricted Works for ordnance handling, weapons irregularities, suspicious cargo, battle damage, contamination, or artifact-adjacent material.

## Flight Control

Flight Control authority covers approach lanes, queue priority, docking order, beacon advisories, fault declarations, traffic holds, race control, solar-array exclusion zones, and emergency vectors.

Amara may sound warm, but safety instructions are not suggestions.

Rules:

- Joy does not modify traffic law.
- Wealth does not modify traffic law.
- Founder status does not modify traffic law.
- Elections do not modify traffic law.
- Faults must be declared before Lichtbrunn Approach.

- Niemand Wird Dich Vermissen: Decimator; hunts and ends isolated threats.

## Starship systems

For full non-weapon starship system rules, check [`canon/20-starship-systems.md`](../canon/20-starship-systems.md). Use [`canon/05-ships.md`](../canon/05-ships.md) for specific ship roles and [`canon/18-weapons-combat-and-force-rules.md`](../canon/18-weapons-combat-and-force-rules.md) for weapons, shields, force, and disablement.

Core terms:

- Sublight drive: high-speed normal-space propulsion below FTL. Used for in-system travel, station approach/departure, traffic-lane maneuvering, combat maneuvering, rescue positioning, and local travel when hyperdrive is unnecessary or unsafe.
- Hyperdrive: faster-than-light interstellar drive. Requires stable primary power, charged field systems, route geometry, navigation data, safe activation separation, confirmed hyperdrive access-path integrity, and clearance where applicable.
- Maneuvering/fine-control systems: thrusters and controls for docking, attitude, station-keeping, cargo alignment, rescue holds, rotation, berth approach, and precise local positioning.
- Reactors / primary power: vary by ship class, route role, manufacturer, era, colony standard, certification, and upgrade history. Available power does not mean safe power. Heat, conduits, containment, safety interlocks, crew workload, cargo state, damage isolation, and traffic law all matter.
- Tool cartridges and diagnostics: maintenance tools are safety-critical. Firmware, authorization, context, and physical effect all matter. A valid-looking maintenance action can still be dangerous.

Useful rules:

- Flight Control clearance is operational law, not politeness.
- A ship may be physically capable of travel but not legally or safely cleared to travel.
- Cloaks do not erase mass, gravity effects, collision risk, heat needs, drive wake, or legal consequences.
- Disabling a ship is not automatically safe; check life support, heat, reactor stability, cargo containment, comms, rescue access, and crew/passenger harm.
- Cargo is part of ship safety. Dirty-side and hazardous-cargo handling exist because cargo problems can become station problems.


## Personal weapons and force vocabulary

Use the standardized effects vocabulary:

- Stun: incapacitate a living target; intended nonlethal, not harmless.
- Restrain: immobilize, bind, slow, separate, or contain.
- Disrupt: interfere with shields, electronics, locks, drones, comms, or weapons.
- Disable: stop a machine, drone, weapon, vehicle, ship subsystem, or ship without necessarily destroying it.
- Wound: injure a living target.
- Kill: lethal force against a living target.
- Destroy: catastrophic damage to a machine, structure, drone, ship, or habitat system.

Personal weapon classes:

- Class S: stun weapon.
- Class R: restraint weapon.
- Class D: disruptor weapon.
- Class L: laser sidearm.
- Class C: laser carbine/rifle.
- Class P: precision laser.
- Class B: breaching/cutting weapon.
- Class H: heavy personal weapon.

Core rule: nonlethal does not mean harmless.

## Heinz's personal weapons

Heinz's personal weapon names are:

- Schmitt Pattern Field Carbine: general-purpose Class C combat laser.
- Schmitt Pattern Longbeam: long-range / high-focus Class P precision laser.
- Schmitt Pattern Scatterbeam: short-range multi-emitter pulse-pattern weapon; not a projectile firearm.

His black leather weapon belt is a personal combat power harness. Weapon fire and shield protection draw from the same endurance pool. Heavy shield impacts reduce weapon endurance, and high-output shots reduce shield endurance.

## Starship weapons and shields

Ship weapons use families, not exact stat blocks:

- PD: point-defense laser.
- LB/HB: light/heavy beam laser.
- PC: pulse cannon.
- DB: disabling beam.
- EM: electromagnetic disruptor.
- GR: grapple/tractor system.
- MS/TP: missile/torpedo system.
- AP: armor-piercing kinetic shell.
- DR: combat drone bay.
- MN: mine or denial charge.

Ship shields include civilian deflectors, reinforced civilian shields, combat shields, heavy layered shields, directional reinforcement, and emergency hull-hugging shields.

Core starship rule: disabling a hostile ship is usually harder than destroying it.

Starship weapons are limited by capacitor recharge, heat rejection, ammunition, firing arcs, sensor lock, shield state, power allocation, crew workload, maintenance state, legal authorization, traffic safety, and collateral risk.

## Cloaks

- Ships cannot fire starship weapons while cloaked.
- Shields can remain active while cloaked, but impacts may reveal approximate presence.
- Cloaks do not hide mass, gravity effects, heat-management needs, collision risk, drive wake, or consequences.
- Ships do not dock while cloaked.
- Normal trade-route travel uses normal transponders unless authorized emergency/IFP stealth protocols apply.

Useful line: “Cloaks hide ships. They do not hide consequences.”

## Economy

Primary settlement unit: IFP Standard Credit, usually “standard credit” or “credit.”

- Station fees, docking, recharge, wages, official fines, payroll, and inter-route contracts usually clear in standard credits.
- Local currencies, colony accounts, barter traditions, guild vouchers, and corporate accounts exist.
- Emergency services can be billed later when safety law requires service.
- Fines are not ordinary profit; serious penalties route through safety, audit, restitution, or restricted infrastructure funds.

Heinz can fund impossible beginnings. He cannot personally bankroll legitimacy forever.

## Law enforcement and jurisdiction

Layered enforcement:

- Station Security: immediate station safety, weapons, detentions, access control, evidence preservation.
- Flight Control: traffic authority, docking, vectors, holds, fault declarations.
- Compliance/Customs: cargo declarations, contraband, import rules, manifest fraud, bans, costs.
- Dirty-Side Operations: hazardous intake, artifact containment, quarantine, decon.
- Medical: triage, medical holds, life-support, sobriety/fitness protocols.
- IFP: piracy, sabotage, terrorism, cross-jurisdiction crimes, route security, diplomatic threats, major investigations.
- Partner colonies: citizen protection, consular roles, civil claims, family rights, local legal/political follow-up.

Der Himmelskrug has holding rooms, quarantine, Medical restraint capacity, and temporary brig capacity. It does not run a prison.

Emergency authority may act first, but serious uses are logged and reviewed.

## Communications and devices

Personal device stack:

- Data slate: core device for hypernet access, identity, payments, maps, records, messages, route advisories, permits, work orders, menus/allergens, transport requests.
- Wristpad: wearable extension for haptics, alerts, confirmations, access taps, payment taps, short replies, emergency pings.
- Earpiece: audio extension for calls, comms, translation audio, alerts, noise filtering, tactical prompts, accessibility audio.
- Staff badge: optional role credential and local access token.
- Ship/station console: authoritative infrastructure.

The hypernet is the network, not a separate handheld device.

A data slate can request. It cannot override ship, station, colony, or IFP systems.

## Communications limits

Communications are useful, not magic.

They can be degraded by:

- distance from settled routes,
- solar weather,
- beacon damage,
- jamming,
- dirty-side shielding,
- security lockdowns,
- artifact interference,
- legal privacy walls,
- crowd noise or station bulkheads.

## Translation

Translation works well for standard speech but struggles with:

- slang,
- dialect,
- songs,
- jokes,
- ritual language,
- legal nuance,
- intoxicated speech,
- overlapping crowd noise,
- culturally loaded ship names.

Lass ma einen bauen means “Let’s roll one” in the relevant slang context. Literal “Let’s build one” is mainly useful for misunderstandings.

## Food and allergens

Post-music-festival principle: “Labels are life support.”

Allergen data must appear where guests, servers, kitchen staff, supply-chain reviewers, and Medical can use it before harm. Supplier documentation alone is not enough.
