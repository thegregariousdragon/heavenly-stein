# Character Quick Cards

## Name/pronoun drafting rule

Use full names only when introducing a character in a story or when formal context requires it. After introduction, use the first name or common quoted name. Check pronouns here or in canon before drafting; do not infer pronouns from names or older drafts.


Use these cards for fast scene drafting. For full details, see `canon/17-character-personality-and-scene-fingerprints.md` and the formal character/role files.

## Core cast

### Heinz Schmitt

- Core: huge German engineer/captain; dry, practical, sentimental, dangerous when serious.
- Contradiction: builds safety and welcome, but prefers usefulness over emotional availability.
- Notices: escape routes, load paths, bad process, weapon angles, who is pretending not to be scared.
- Stress: gets quieter, stiller, flatter in humor.
- Blind spot: mistakes endurance for emotional maturity.
- Beat: checks custom wristpad when avoiding an emotional answer.
- Line: “I am not avoiding the subject. I am letting it become less stupid before I answer.”

### Feli Brandt

- Core: Director of Communications and Civic Interface; publicly Heinz’s partner.
- Contradiction: translates fear into usable language, but over-functions because silence feels negligent.
- Notices: who is excluded, what the media really asks, when Heinz hides inside engineering language.
- Stress: warmer, more precise, fewer wasted words.
- Blind spot: makes everyone else’s feelings survivable before allowing her own.
- Beat: one-word slate messages: “Breathe.” “No.” “Later.”
- Line: “I am not softening the truth. I am removing the shrapnel.”

### Rolf Petersen

- Core: Director of Engineering and Structural Systems; clamps, seals, tolerances, access.
- Contradiction: distrusts sentiment in design but is deeply sentimental through repair.
- Notices: load paths, fasteners, inspection access, temporary things pretending not to need inspection.
- Stress: becomes specific, checklist-heavy, hardware-hostile.
- Blind spot: underestimates social systems because they lack visible bolts.
- Beat: physically tests railings, brackets, chairs, doors, metaphors.
- Line: “Temporary is where permanent hides until it kills someone.”

### Clara/Klara Obermeier

- Core: Director of Legal-Cultural Affairs; case-law prevention.
- Contradiction: believes rules prevent disaster, but is drawn to people who prove rules need courage and interpretation.
- Notices: precedent, cultural insult risk, who will be blamed later.
- Stress: becomes more formal and complete in sentences.
- Blind spot: treats uncertainty as paperwork failure.
- Beat: pauses because the absurd thing is useful, then demands a category.
- Line: “Civilization is mostly people writing down what went wrong before someone calls it tradition.”

### Tariq/Teriq Neumann

- Core: Director of Cultural Atmosphere and Public Experience; load-bearing vibes.
- Contradiction: makes serious work look effortless, then resents when people believe the effortlessness.
- Notices: crowd rhythm, sound changes, lost children, wrong silence, bad room mood.
- Stress: less flashy, voice drops, instructions become practical.
- Blind spot: style as armor against being hurt.
- Beat: adjusts the room before explaining the problem.
- Line: “A crowd tells you the truth before it knows the words.”

### Matthias Schmitt

- Core: master brewer, father, process authority.
- Contradiction: emotionally reserved but makes things that bring people together.
- Notices: cleanliness, temperature, timing, yeast, rushed process.
- Stress: slows down and asks process questions.
- Blind spot: praise too rare.
- Beat: wipes a surface before touching it, even if clean.
- Line: “Beer forgives patience. It does not forgive arrogance.”

### Karin Schmitt

- Core: science teacher, systems thinker, remote Learning Center curriculum guide.
- Contradiction: curiosity saves people, but her children are clever enough to make curiosity dangerous.
- Notices: missing questions, watching children, untested assumptions.
- Stress: asks system-revealing questions.
- Blind spot: thinks better explanation can fix bad faith.
- Beat: “What do we know?” before arguments.
- Line: “A rule no one understands is only a superstition with better typography.”

### Lukas Schmitt

- Core: day-to-day brewing operations lead.
- Contradiction: wants independence from Matthias and Heinz, but is proudest when family tradition works through him.
- Notices: flow rate, pressure, server rhythm, bad pours, worker abuse by schedule.
- Stress: bossy in practical ways.
- Blind spot: can confuse high standards with unclear standards.
- Beat: turns a glass and checks the foam line.
- Line: “If the line is wrong, the room feels it before the guest names it.”

### Annika Schmitt

- Core: university-based, survived OmniWay-linked assault, refuses revenge logic.
- Contradiction: refuses fragility but is still learning how to live with being targeted because she is loved.
- Notices: adult euphemisms, protective lies, manipulation, masculine protective stupidity.
- Stress: fear sharpens into moral clarity.
- Blind spot: may reject care she needs.
- Beat: folds arms to stop herself from shaking.
- Line: “Do not make my pain convenient for your courage.”

### Tante Hedi

- Core: New Bavarian moral authority; treats Heinz like an unruly nephew.
- Contradiction: respects authority only when it behaves like responsibility.
- Notices: who has not eaten, who is grieving badly, who hides behind title.
- Stress: food, chair, blanket, name, song, witness, scolding.
- Blind spot: moral rightness can make her impatient with safe procedure.
- Beat: sits in the chair no one offered her.
- Line: “If you wanted me quiet, you should not have made this important.”
