# Canon Locks

These are high-priority continuity rules that should not drift unless a later canon update deliberately changes them.

## Project identity

- The core mode is ridiculous premise, serious engineering underneath.
- Competent people should not be made stupid just to create comedy.
- Der Himmelskrug is funny from a distance and serious up close.
- The station is a working civic / route-safety institution, not merely a novelty brewery-port.


## Naming and one-off character locks

- One-off, guest, victim, witness, perpetrator, contractor, visitor, media, freight, and support-character names must not duplicate or closely resemble established ensemble, recurring, or canon-significant names.
- Avoid same first names, near-homophones, visually similar names, or names likely to confuse screen-reader users or continuity review.
- Germanic names are allowed and appropriate when context supports them, but they should not dominate the broader pool of one-off/supporting characters unless the story specifically calls for it.
- Der Himmelskrug is a route hub serving many human cultures, colonies, crews, faiths, languages, and labor networks; guest names should reflect that variety.

## Current status

- Der Himmelskrug is fully operational at Fünfstromknoten / The Knot near Lichtbrunn.
- It serves the New Bavaria Route, Zion Drift Route, Tannenfall Route, Relay Haven Spur, and Coreward Freight Line.
- The relocation from the temporary assembly mooring is complete.
- The Himmelskrug Learning Center Phase One is complete.


## Station interior redesign locks

- The current Der Himmelskrug interior layout is the completed public/residential/services/garage redesign in `canon/08-der-himmelskrug-station.md` and `quick-reference/STATION_BLOCKING_GUIDE.md`.
- Crown is fixed as a four-quadrant civic cap: Command, Administration/Civic, Learning Center, and Museum.
- Command is the strictly restricted Crown quadrant. Admin/Civic, Learning, and Museum are controlled by purpose but are not Command-level spaces.
- The dedicated press suite is on Hospitality Level 1, not in the Crown.
- Senior/critical staff housing is on Residential, not in the Crown, except for limited duty-rest/call rooms tied to emergency operations.
- The Learning Center is in the Crown Learning Center Quadrant, reached by protected Residential/school routing.
- Observation remains an open 360-degree viewing/event level with the public atrium void and chandelier; it does not contain the Museum.
- The former Cultural Level is now the Entertainment Level: venues, music, comedy, games, festival/event rooms, and back-of-house performer support.
- Main Level is formally Hospitality Level 1. It still contains the arrival reveal and main taproom across the atrium from arrivals.
- Secondary Hospitality is formally Hospitality Level 2. It contains food hall, lower brewery/large bar, hotel/short-term visitor lodging, and personal services.
- Retail is the final public level and bottom of the public atrium. The Green Room / Grünraum belongs on Retail as a controlled adult cannabis pilot/lounge/shop adjacent to but distinct from Music / Media / Gifts.
- Residential is below Retail and above Services, organized around the Wohnhof and four residential quadrants.
- Services is controlled staff/pilot/operational support, not a general public atrium level.
- Bubble elevators are public scenic elevators only for Observation, Entertainment, Hospitality 1, Hospitality 2, and Retail. They do not serve Crown, Residential, Services, Medical, Starship Garage, Restricted Works, Command, school, museum, or brewery production.
- Hidden hard-service cores, protected stairs, Medical lifts, resident lifts, staff/service lifts, command cores, and dirty-side cores provide real emergency and operational routing.
- Formal bottom-level names are Starship Garage and Restricted Works. “Clean side,” “clean garage,” “dirty side,” and “dirty garage” may appear as staff slang, but formal maps use Starship Garage and Restricted Works.
- Services is controlled staff/pilot/crew/operational support, not a public convenience floor.
- Starship Garage is the clean-side ship harbor for normal berths, clean cargo, inspection, recharge, crew transition, and non-hazardous ship support.
- Restricted Works is the restricted dirty-side / hazardous industrial deck for decon, quarantine, hazardous salvage, dirty repair, artifact/anomaly intake, restricted ordnance/rearming, evidence/compliance, industrial drones, and heavy engineering.
- Consumable starship-weapon rearming happens only through Restricted Works’ restricted ordnance/rearming facilities, not ordinary Starship Garage berths.
- Lass ma einen bauen has a Starship Garage pride-of-place berth plus a controlled public-viewable feed for scheduled civic, museum, educational, founding-anniversary, birthday, or special celebration events. The feed does not grant public physical garage access.
- Nur Für Den Fall may use a controlled Starship Garage long-stay cutter berth when safed, declared, and logged. It is not part of the public pride display.

## Rebuild-era safety tone

- The station remains warm and funny, but it is not naive about sabotage, hostile media, oversight, or the cost of authority.
- Trust requires verification.
- Serious safety/security stories should show scarred competence without requiring any old story event to have happened.
- The dead, injured, frightened, accused, grieving, or privately wronged are not public content.

## Transport and arrival

- Teleportation is point-to-point, not anywhere-to-anywhere.
- Personal remotes/data slates carry authorization. They do not create destinations.
- Der Himmelskrug does not allow routine direct transport into public hospitality, residential, school, brewery production, or command areas.
- Public arrivals route through Arrival Control and shared clearance.
- Nobody appears behind the beer.

## Clean/dirty separation

- Clean and dirty routing are station-wide safety principles.
- Dirty, hazardous, suspicious, undeclared, artifact-adjacent, contaminated, or battle-damaged traffic goes below.
- “Probably fine goes below” is operational truth, not only a joke.

## Flight Control

- Flight Control authority is real and safety-based.
- Joy, elections, media attention, founder status, yacht wealth, and festival excitement do not modify traffic law.
- The Himmelskrug Run is annual, sanctioned, no hyperspace, no active weapons, and under strict Flight Control authority.

## Ships

- Use narrative capability profiles, not old exact stat blocks.
- Lass ma einen bauen is normally based at Der Himmelskrug’s Starship Garage in a pride-of-place berth with controlled public-viewable feed for approved special events.
- Combat ships Niemand Wird Dich Vermissen, Komm Her Und Stirb Schnell, and Das Geht Schnell remain at IFP Bastion Meridian or other appropriate combat staging facilities, not normally at Der Himmelskrug.
- Cloaks do not permit firing while cloaked or docking while cloaked.

## Governance and labor

- The Assembly speaks as a town. The Guild speaks as labor.
- The Guild represents non-senior workers, not senior directors or emergency-authority roles.
- Heinz supporting oversight is growth, not humiliation.

## Weapons and force locks

- Nonlethal does not mean harmless.
- Disabling is usually harder than destroying.
- Heinz's personal weapons are the Schmitt Pattern Field Carbine,
  Longbeam, and Scatterbeam, integrated with his personal combat power
  harness.
- The Scatterbeam is not a projectile firearm.
- Weapon fire and shield protection draw from the same harness endurance
  pool for Heinz.
- Ships cannot fire starship weapons while cloaked.
- Starship weapons use narrative families and loadout tiers, not exact
  numerical stat blocks.
- Starship weapons near stations, traffic lanes, races, convoys, solar
  arrays, or civilian habitats require clear authorization except
  immediate defense against imminent harm.
- Consumable starship ordnance, including missiles, torpedoes, mines/denial charges, combat-drone munitions, and armor-piercing kinetic reloads, is handled only through restricted ordnance/rearming facilities.
- Der Himmelskrug weapons policy is strict in public hospitality, retail, residential, education, and event spaces because alcohol, crowds, pressure doors, lifts, and civilian infrastructure make weapon incidents unacceptable.

## Tested Precedent Locks

- Do not treat nonlethal weapons as harmless or clean.
- Do not treat disabling fire as free, precise, or consequence-free by default.
- Do not let private enforcement or debt claims override Flight Control in controlled approach zones.
- Do not let safety support automatically erase debt or contract disputes.
- Do not let valid grievances excuse dangerous acts.
- Do not let dangerous acts become an excuse to bury valid grievances.
- Do not make Heinz override assigned institutional authority just because he is the protagonist.
- Do not turn old test scenarios into rigid formulas; preserve the principles, not every beat.

## Cannabis and Consequence Locks

- The Green Room is the public name of Der Himmelskrug's controlled adult cannabis pilot; Grünraum is the internal/licensing/technical name.
- Legal cannabis on Der Himmelskrug does not override destination law, shipmaster authority, crew contracts, or safety-duty sobriety rules.
- Zion Drift / Port Irie is broadly permissive; Tannenfall / Larkspur is restrictive and biosecurity-minded; New Bavaria is cautious around public intoxication and cannabis/alcohol pairing; Relay Haven is pragmatic but strict about safety-duty impairment; the Veyr Compact is severe-prohibition.
- No sealed cannabis takeaway to Veyr-bound passengers or crew.
- No cannabis/alcohol bundle promotions.
- Corridor odor bleed from cannabis areas is an engineering/policy failure.


## Station blocking guide lock

- For scenes on Der Himmelskrug, use `quick-reference/STATION_BLOCKING_GUIDE.md` as the practical map/blocking reference.
- The station is both a vertical stack and a route-separation system. A scene should know its level, route class, public/restricted status, visibility, sound environment, and authority owner.
- Crown/lid media facilities are no longer a Crown press room. Press belongs in the controlled Hospitality Level 1 press suite with remote feeds from Crown Command/Admin.
- The alpenhorn balcony was not implemented as station architecture. Alpenhorns may remain instruments, jokes, or cultural details, but not a dedicated built balcony feature.


## Interior sensory locks

- Surface, lighting, air, and sound guidance in `canon/08-der-himmelskrug-station.md` is active station canon.
- Bavarian warmth is architectural influence, not costume. Do not turn Der Himmelskrug into a plastic novelty mug, fake castle, or generic space mall.
- Odor bleed is a failure unless the scene is specifically about malfunction or containment stress. Brewery, food, cannabis, salon products, Medical disinfectant, garage air, decon, and Restricted Works air must remain locally controlled.
- The Green Room / Grünraum and any performer cannabis-permitted space require dedicated ventilation. Corridor cannabis odor means engineering/policy failure.
- Acoustic separation is architecture. Entertainment venues, hotel rooms, Residential, Medical, Crown workrooms, and the public atrium do not rely on people simply being quiet.
- Lighting follows authority owner: public levels are warm and legible; Command, Medical, Starship Garage, and Restricted Works are task-led; Residential is circadian and domestic.
- Restricted Works should normally smell neutral because filtration and containment are working. Any noticeable hazard odor is a plot-relevant problem.

## Staff privacy and The Spare Tap lock

- The Spare Tap is hard-canon as Der Himmelskrug's staff-only off-shift bar and decompression room behind the brewery service level. It is not secret; it is simply not public.
- Active-duty staff do not drink alcohol there. Flight Control, Security, Engineering, Medical, Dirty-Side, and other safety-sensitive roles follow duty-specific restrictions.
- Off shift is not off record unless everyone agrees. Recording requires room consent; external sharing requires consent from identifiable staff.
- Staff joy is not station content unless staff choose to make it content. Accountability does not mean surveillance.
