# Der Himmelskrug Station Blocking Guide

Status: revised layout reference for the station interior redesign pass.
Purpose: prevent layout, routing, visibility, sound, access, and emergency-response errors when writing scenes on Der Himmelskrug.

## Core rule

Der Himmelskrug is funny from a distance and serious up close. It is not a flat venue, not a mall, and not a generic spaceport. It is a vertically stacked public station, residential community, brewery, clean-side/restricted logistics facility, and route-safety institution.

Scene blocking should always ask four questions:

1. What level is this scene on?
2. Which route class are the people or objects allowed to use?
3. Who owns the space operationally?
4. What can characters see, hear, smell, access, or be blocked from accessing?

Route class matters as much as location.

## Exterior and major access points

Use these as scene anchors. The exterior remains an iconic beer stein from public approach, but the working side shows real orbital infrastructure.

### Crown / lid

- Function: four-quadrant civic cap: Command, Admin/Civic, Learning Center, Museum.
- Scene use: command, school, museum, civic, consular, governance, emergency coordination.

### Observation band

- Function: glowing 360-degree ring below the crown.
- Scene use: viewing, receptions, dances, major celebrations, quiet spectacle.

### Upper handle connection

- Function: public docking / arrival access into Arrival Control.
- Scene use: normal public entry, VIP intake before clearance, arrivals from upper-handle docking.

### Lower handle connection

- Function: Starship Garage portal only.
- Scene use: clean-side ship movement, long-stay parking, pilot/crew services after clearance.

### Working service side

- Function: solar truss, radiators, utility arcs, maintenance rails, drone ports, and service panels.
- Scene use: maintenance, power infrastructure, radiator/array hazards, engineering work. Not a public approach route.

### Lower body/base restricted-side doors

- Function: Restricted Works / hazardous intake.
- Scene use: salvage, battle damage, unverified artifacts, contaminated cargo, quarantine, dirty repair, rearming.

The handle is functional infrastructure, not decoration. The upper handle, lower handle, and utility chase are pressure-rated and separated. They do not create a casual public shortcut into the Starship Garage.

### 1. Crown

### 3. Entertainment

### 4. Hospitality Level 1

- Primary function: arrival reveal, main taproom, upper brewery, cafés/restaurants, press suite.

### 5. Hospitality Level 2

- Public status: public / guest-controlled.

### 6. Retail

- Public status: public, final public level.

### 7. Residential

### 9. Starship Garage

### 10. Restricted Works

- Public status: restricted / hazardous.
- Primary function: decon, quarantine, salvage, dirty repair, artifact intake, rearming, evidence, heavy engineering.

## Public atrium / platz

The public atrium spans only:

1. Observation
2. Entertainment
3. Hospitality Level 1
4. Hospitality Level 2
5. Retail

Retail is the bottom of normal public movement. The atrium does not extend into Services. Older blocking that places the public fountain basin on Services is superseded; Retail now contains the atrium terminus, such as a catch-basin, water/light, or foam-and-starlight feature.

### Chandelier and bubble elevators

- The Observation ceiling supports the massive foam/starlight chandelier descending into the central atrium void.
- Bubble elevators are iconic public scenic elevators serving Observation, Entertainment, Hospitality 1, Hospitality 2, and Retail.
- Bubble elevators travel around the atrium perimeter/inner wall; they do not pass through the chandelier zone.
- Bubble elevators do not normally stop at Crown, Residential, Services, Starship Garage, Restricted Works, Medical, school, museum, brewery production, or Command.
- Bubble elevators are not the emergency backbone.

### Acoustic rule

The atrium provides dramatic sightlines, not reliable shouted conversation. Crowd noise, water/light feature sound, elevator motion, taproom spillover, music, public announcements, and event traffic make cross-level dialogue unreliable unless amplified or carried by comms.

## Hidden vertical systems

Use hidden hard-service systems for real operations.

### Command Core

- Serves: Command, emergency ops, Services/Medical interface, Starship Garage operations.
- Normal users: Flight Control, Security, emergency staff.

### Admin/Civic Core

- Serves: Crown Admin/Civic, controlled public/civic reception, Arrival/VIP links.
- Normal users: staff, appointment visitors, consular users.

### Learning/Museum Core

- Serves: Learning Center, Museum, supervised public/school access, Residential school route.
- Normal users: students, teachers, school groups, adult learners.

### Residential Core

- Serves: Residential, Services, protected school route, controlled staff routes.
- Normal users: residents, long-term crew, authorized staff.

### Medical/Emergency Core

- Serves: Medical, public incident access points, Residential, Starship Garage, emergency Crown interface.
- Normal users: Medical, Security, emergency teams.

### Garage/Service Core

- Serves: Starship Garage, Services, service corridors, H1/H2 back-of-house, brewery service spine.
- Normal users: staff, maintenance, vendors, Starship Garage crews.

### Restricted-Side Core

- Serves: Restricted Works, decon, quarantine, dirty repair, containment/research handoff.
- Normal users: Restricted Works staff, Security, Medical isolation, drones.

Four protected emergency stair towers around the public atrium can connect public levels, but controlled doors prevent casual public descent into Residential or Services.

## Crown fixed layout

The Crown is a four-quadrant civic cap. The quadrants share civic importance, not shared access.

### Command Quadrant

Flight Control, route control, secure comms, emergency operations, crisis coordination, technical support. Strict restricted access. No press, school, museum, consular waiting, senior housing, or public shortcut.

### Administration / Civic Quadrant

Station administration, consular services, permits, documentation, governance support, labor/governance rooms, mediation, civic records, public-facing counters. Screened/appointment access with internal controls for consulates, records, and admin back offices.

### Learning Center Quadrant

Child and adult education, apprenticeships, safety/route classes, station orientation, language/cultural education, remote learning, school commons, counselor/school-nurse support. Protected route from Residential and scheduled public/class access.

### Museum Quadrant

Station history, colony exhibits, brewing history, route-safety/process exhibits, rotating galleries, and cleared artifact exhibits only under strict review. Controlled museum access; no direct Command or public Residential shortcut.

### Press

The dedicated press suite is on Hospitality Level 1, not in the Crown. It receives remote feeds from Crown Command/Admin and never grants press physical access to Flight Control, secure comms, Residential, Medical, or consular back offices.

## Observation Level

Essential open 360-degree public viewing level. Mostly clear space to the glass, with central atrium void and chandelier support. Used for viewing, receptions, dances, formal civic events, celebrations, and quiet spectacle. No museum quadrant, school, press room, restaurants, shops, or command spillover.

Blocking zones:

- Outer viewing ring along the glass.
- Flexible event ring for receptions/dances.
- Atrium guard ring around the central void.
- Ceiling/chandelier support canopy with hidden maintenance/safety access.

## Entertainment Level

Public/event-controlled entertainment neighborhoods around the atrium.

Rings:

1. Atrium promenade with bubble elevator landings and wayfinding.
2. Front-of-house ring with venue entrances, queues, ticket/check-in, restrooms.
3. Venue ring with performance and social rooms.
4. Back-of-house ring with performer staging, dressing rooms, technical booths, Security/Medical response, loading, service corridors.

Neighborhoods:

- Festhalle District: big performances, dances, festivals, large crowd programming.
- Cabaret / Comedy District: comedy, cabaret, storytelling, small theater, late-night stage work.
- Music / Listening District: live music, acoustically controlled rooms, listening spaces, rehearsal/recording support.
- Games / Social District: games, trivia, private party rooms, karaoke-equivalent rooms, flexible social events.

The performer lounge is not called The Green Room. Use Performer Staging Suite or Künstlerzimmer if adopting the German term. If cannabis use is allowed there, ventilation, odor control, access, duty, and storage rules must be explicit.

## Hospitality Level 1

Former Main Level. Formal map name: Hospitality Level 1. “Main” can remain casual shorthand in dialogue.

Quadrants:

- Arrivals Quadrant: controlled entrance corridor from Arrival Control and separate exit corridor back toward departures.
- Main Taproom Quadrant: taproom directly across the atrium from arrivals, upper brewery behind it.
- Café / Light Dining Quadrant: coffee, bakery, sober options, light meals, guest services overflow.
- Restaurant / Social Dining Quadrant: sit-down dining, smaller restaurant venues, family/event dining.

The press suite belongs here: public-facing, controlled, command-separated, fed remotely from Crown Command/Admin.

## Hospitality Level 2

Split-use deck: lively toward the atrium, quiet toward the hotel band.

Districts:

- Food Hall District: casual counters, colony food stalls, shared seating, dish return, food-service spine.
- Brewery Bar District: largest H2 bar, lower brewery behind it, cellar/cold storage, beverage dispatch, tap-line service, controlled stock/service relationship with The Spare Tap.
- Guest House District: short-term visitor lodging, hotel lobby, guest rooms/suites, accessible rooms, family rooms, luggage, housekeeping, linen, quiet lounge.
- Personal Services District: barber, salon, shoe store/repair, tailor/clothing repair, garment steaming, luggage repair, toiletries/travel essentials.

Hotel rooms must be buffered from bars/brewery by service spaces, storage, restrooms, workrooms, vestibules, or acoustic thresholds. Hotel room doors do not open directly onto the atrium promenade.

## Retail Level

Final public level and bottom of the atrium. It should feel like an orbital market hall, not a luxury mall or basement.

Districts:

- Colony Market: rotating colony goods, crafts, packaged specialties, seasonal/festival stalls.
- Practical Outfitters: clothing, stationwear, boots, bags, luggage, event/travel goods.
- Tools / Parts / Brewing Supplies: approved seals, clamps, hand tools, repair kits, brewing supplies, monitored practical goods. No weapons, dirty-side gear, suspicious salvage, or artifact trade.
- Music / Media / Gifts: Feli’s music shop, recordings, instrument accessories, merch, books, maps, games, gift items.
- The Green Room / Grünraum: public controlled adult cannabis pilot/lounge/shop. Adjacent to but distinct from Music / Media / Gifts. Requires eligibility checks, destination-law checks, sealed takeaway rules, no alcohol bundling, water/snack/dosage support, odor monitoring, quiet-down capacity, and no corridor odor bleed.

Retail contains the controlled threshold to lower non-public station functions. Public visitors do not wander below Retail.

## Residential Level

Restricted town-like residential level below Retail and above Services.

Central feature: Wohnhof, a quiet residential atrium/commons for orientation, community, resident notices, protected elevator access, school route, and domestic decompression. It is not a tourist plaza.

Quadrants:

- Family Quadrant: staff families, child-safe corridors, playrooms, domestic gardens, school-route access.
- Senior / Critical Staff Quadrant: modest apartments, duty-rest suites, secure call rooms, crisis sleep rooms, quick-response lift to Command; not elite penthouse housing.
- Rotational Crew / Visiting Worker Quadrant: dorms, compact rooms, shared kitchens, lockers, shift-ready changing, union/labor information.
- General Staff / Long-Term Housing Quadrant: studios, shared lounges, communal kitchens, quiet rooms, ordinary domestic life.

Short-term visitor lodging belongs on Hospitality Level 2, not Residential. The Learning Center is in the Crown, reached by protected route.

## Services Level

Controlled staff/pilot/crew/operational support. Not a public atrium level and not a tourist convenience deck.

Access: station staff, residents, authorized pilots/crews, visiting workers/contractors, Medical patients/escorts, Security, garage staff, maintenance, and operational users with a specific purpose. Casual tourists, hotel guests, shoppers, press, school groups, and entertainment crowds do not wander Services.

Districts:

- Services Control: credential checks, routing, crew/pilot check-in, Medical intake routing, Starship Garage transition, status boards, staff assistance, Security presence.
- Medical / Pharmacy: triage, treatment, occupational health, fatigue/intoxication evaluation, pharmacy, clean-side isolation, controlled dirty-side Medical interface.
- Crew Recovery: showers, laundry, workwear cleaning, rest pods, fatigue rooms, quiet sleep rooms, hydration/food support, lockers, gear drying, decompression, no-fly/sobering holds.
- Pilot and Crew Operations: crew boards, contract boards, route advisories, berth displays, cargo/inspection status, departure holds, shipmaster consultation, union/guild/route-user notices.
- Documentation / Translation / Mediation: operational paperwork, temporary crew/contractor access, cargo discrepancy forms, berth extensions, medical clearance paperwork, translation, route/vendor/crew mediation.
- Quiet Room / Memorial / Decompression: private reflection, low-stim room, post-incident decompression, escorted family/staff waiting. Not a tourist memorial.
- Starship Garage Transition: credential gates, pilot/crew access control, clean cargo interface, tool/gear pickup, ship service requests, inspection check-in, maintenance dispatch, emergency route back to Medical.

Public grooming/tailoring/shoe repair/luggage repair belongs on Hospitality Level 2. Public consular/civic services belong in Crown Admin/Civic or arrival/H1 interfaces unless a controlled Services appointment is needed.

## Brewery and The Spare Tap

The brewery is shortened to Hospitality Level 1 and Hospitality Level 2.

- H1: upper/front-of-house brewery behind main taproom; ceremonial taps, visible serving tanks, tour windows, craft/process displays, quality-control/tasting support.
- H2: lower/working brewery behind largest bar; production brewhouse, fermentation galleries, yeast lab, water chemistry, kegging/sealed beverage dispatch, cold storage, service-line hub.
- Brewery education lives in Museum, viewing windows, and controlled tours; Entertainment is not the exhibit level.

The Spare Tap is hard canon:

- Staff-only off-shift bar and decompression room.
- Two corridors behind the H2 brewery service level, past a staff-only pressure door.
- Not secret, not public, not residential, not command, not brewery production.
- Connected to brewery stock/service for fast, controlled alcohol retrieval by serving staff.
- Not a pass-through corridor.
- Staff privacy is not station content.

## Starship Garage and Restricted Works

Formal map names:

- **Starship Garage**: clean-side normal ship harbor below Services.
- **Restricted Works**: restricted dirty-side / hazardous industrial deck below the Starship Garage.

Staff may still say clean side / dirty side in dialogue. Formal station maps and scene blocking should use Starship Garage and Restricted Works.

### Starship Garage

- Lower handle connects to the Starship Garage only.
- Functions: standard berths, extended-stay berths, priority/emergency clean berths, maintenance/inspection berths, recharge, clean cargo, pilot/crew services, garage operations, and clean-side maintenance dispatch.
- Clean cargo uses cargo locks, scan lanes, manifest checks, seal verification, freight lifts, and service corridors. It does not move through public Retail, hotel corridors, taproom routes, crew showers, Medical waiting, or Residential family routes.
- The Starship Garage can perform safed diagnostics, ordinary clean maintenance, non-live weapon-system checks, and declaration paperwork. It does not casually handle live missiles, torpedoes, mines/denial charges, combat-drone munitions, or open ordnance crates.
- Lass ma einen bauen has the pride-of-place berth here when in port. Authorized garage traffic can see it. Public audiences see a controlled feed during scheduled special events, not the garage itself.
- Utility craft attached to Lass ma einen bauen normally remain docked with or aboard the Explorer unless deployed or under service.
- Nur Für Den Fall may use a controlled clean-side long-stay cutter berth when safed, declared, and logged. It is not part of the public pride display.
- Heinz’s dedicated combat ships are not normally based at Der Himmelskrug.

### Restricted Works

- Lower body/base restricted-side doors connect to Restricted Works only.
- Functions: decon, quarantine, hazardous salvage, dirty repair, artifact/anomaly intake, restricted ordnance and rearming, industrial drone handling, evidence/compliance/security, and heavy engineering.
- Districts: Decon and Quarantine; Hazardous Salvage; Dirty Repair; Restricted Ordnance/Rearming; Artifact and Anomaly Intake; Industrial Drone and Remote Handling; Evidence/Compliance/Security; Heavy Engineering.
- Decon is a process, not a hallway. Nothing exits Restricted Works to clean-side use without scan, decon, custody, Medical, Security, Compliance, and/or Garage Ops release as applicable.
- Consumable starship-weapon rearming belongs in Restricted Works’ restricted ordnance area, even if the ship itself is clean in the contamination sense. Rearming is licensed hazardous work under station authority, not shopping.

### Shared rules

- Starship Garage and Restricted Works do not share casual circulation.
- Transfer between them uses sealed transfer, tow cradles, controlled service locks, or authorized industrial routes.
- Dirty-side traffic never passes through public levels until cleared.
- “Probably fine goes below.”

## Arrival and route rules

Public arrivals:

1. Docking or authorized personnel transport.
2. Secured arrival zone.
3. Shared identity/security/decontamination/customs/weapons/clean-dirty declaration.
4. Public release gate.
5. Controlled entrance corridor.
6. Hospitality Level 1 atrium threshold.

Departures use a separate exit corridor.

No routine direct transport into taproom, atrium, hotel, Residential, Learning Center, Museum, brewery production, Command, Services, or garage areas.

## Surface, lighting, air, and sound quick guide

Use this section for fast scene description. Use `canon/08-der-himmelskrug-station.md` for the full sensory palette.

### Global sensory locks

- Bavarian warmth is influence, not costume.
- Public warmth never overrides routing, decon, security, labor, Medical, Flight Control, or emergency access.
- Lighting follows authority: public areas are warm; Command, Medical, Starship Garage, and Restricted Works are task-led; Residential is circadian and domestic.
- Odor bleed is a failure unless the scene is about a malfunction. Brewery, food, cannabis, salon products, Medical disinfectant, garage air, and decon air must be locally controlled.
- Acoustic separation is architecture. Entertainment, hotel rooms, Residential, Medical, Crown workrooms, and the atrium do not rely on people simply being quiet.

### Crown

- Surfaces: civic panels, dark metal, quiet wood, frosted glass, acoustic rooms.
- Lighting: calm civic light; cooler Command task light; controlled museum/gallery light.
- Air: neutral, filtered, paper/electronics/wood.
- Sound: hushed, compartmented, low comms in Command.

### Observation

- Surfaces: glass, pale floor, polished rails, brass or dark-metal guard details.
- Lighting: exterior/starfield light plus amber event light and chandelier glow.
- Air: clean, cool, lightly filtered.
- Sound: spacious, controlled, soft crowd murmur unless programmed for events.

### Entertainment

- Surfaces: darker woods, brass trim, sound-absorbing panels, acoustic vestibules.
- Lighting: marquee warmth and stage-controlled color, with safe promenade lighting.
- Air: neutral with faint drinks/clothes/stage haze where allowed; no cannabis bleed.
- Sound: zoned by neighborhood and venue.

### Hospitality Level 1

- Surfaces: white plaster, dark wood, polished brass, cask wood, durable tile/stone-like floors.
- Lighting: warm amber, arrival-friendly, clear wayfinding.
- Air: beer, bread/pretzel, coffee, warm food, polished wood; no decon or garage smell.
- Sound: lively but intelligible.

### Hospitality Level 2

- Surfaces: food-hall durability, hotel-grade quiet corridors, personal-service finishes, sealed brewery floors.
- Lighting: warm public food/bar light, softer hotel light, clean personal-services light, task brewery light.
- Air: food hall, malt/yeast near bar, salon/barber products; neutral hotel corridors.
- Sound: public ring lively; hotel ring buffered.

### Retail

- Surfaces: market wood, polished metal, practical displays, secure cases.
- Lighting: bright enough for shopping, warm enough for market feeling.
- Air: packaged goods, paper, textiles, light coffee/tea where allowed; no cannabis corridor odor.
- Sound: browsing, low music, bubble elevators, catch-basin/water-light feature.

### Residential

- Surfaces: domestic finishes, resilient floors, acoustic panels, gardens, washable family-safe materials.
- Lighting: circadian, soft at night, brighter in the Wohnhof by day.
- Air: clean domestic air, plants, laundry, cooking only in residential food spaces.
- Sound: child-safe and shift-worker respectful.

### Services

- Surfaces: washable floors, sealed panels, privacy glass, medical surfaces, lockers.
- Lighting: steady; brighter in Medical; softer in Crew Recovery; industrial near garage transition.
- Air: laundry, Medical disinfectant in Medical, gear/metal near garage transition.
- Sound: low announcements, status boards, showers/laundry, tired crew.

### Starship Garage

- Surfaces: industrial plating, docking seals, dark metal, rubberized edges, numbered berth panels.
- Lighting: task light, berth-status lights, docking signals, emergency strips.
- Air: filtered machine air, metal, coolant/ozone hints within safety limits.
- Sound: clamps, collars, utility hum, service carts, low comms.

### Restricted Works

- Surfaces: sealed industrial panels, blast-rated doors, washable/decon surfaces, hazard striping.
- Lighting: functional, segmented, status-colored, red/hazard markers where appropriate.
- Air: ideally neutral. Any odor means containment, decon, or ventilation is now part of the scene.
- Sound: pressure doors, drones, ventilation, tools, alerts, remote handling.

## Scene-use rule

Use two or three specific design details per scene beat, then return to action. Do not stop every scene for a station tour unless a character is truly arriving, lost, showing someone around, or noticing a system under stress.
